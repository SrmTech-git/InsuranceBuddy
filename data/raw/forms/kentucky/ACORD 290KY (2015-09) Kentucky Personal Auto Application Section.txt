ACORD 290KY (2015-09) — Kentucky Personal Auto Application Section
===================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290KY
Edition: 2015-09
Title: Kentucky Personal Auto Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Kentucky state variant)
States: KY
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Kentucky. Used
in combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application —
Applicant Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The
290KY holds the auto-specific data needed to underwrite a Kentucky
auto policy under Kentucky's Motor Vehicle Reparations Act (KRS 304.39).

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM/UIM, PIP (no-fault), MedPay, comp, coll
- PIP / no-fault election (default applies; insured may reject the
  no-fault tort threshold in writing)
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Kentucky-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity is captured on ACORD 88, not duplicated here)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290KY) rather than the standalone full app (90KY)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Kentucky minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 split (or $60K CSL)
- Kentucky is a "choice no-fault" state — PIP mandatory by default at
  $10,000 (KRS § 304.39-020); insured may reject no-fault tort
  threshold in writing (KRS § 304.39-060)
- Kentucky UM/UIM: must OFFER; rejectable in writing (KRS § 304.20-020)
- Primary statutes: KRS 304 (Insurance), KRS 304.39 (MVRA), KRS 186A
- State regulator: Kentucky Department of Insurance (KDI)
- Use 290KY with ACORD 88; use 90KY standalone
