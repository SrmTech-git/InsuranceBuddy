ACORD 64KY (2011-01) — Kentucky FAIR Plan Homeowners Application
=================================================================
Form number: ACORD 64KY
Edition: 2011-01
Title: Kentucky FAIR Plan Homeowners Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Kentucky state variant)
States: KY
Policy term: 12 months (typical for FAIR Plan homeowners)
Transaction types: New business (FAIR Plan submission); renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Application for owner-occupied residential property coverage through
the Kentucky FAIR Plan — Kentucky's state-mandated residual property
insurance market for property owners who cannot obtain coverage in
the voluntary market. FAIR (Fair Access to Insurance Requirements)
Plans were created post-1968 federal Urban Property Protection and
Reinsurance Act to ensure baseline property coverage availability.

Captures:
- Insured / owner information (name, address, contact)
- Property location and address
- Property description: construction type, year built, square footage,
  number of stories, roof type, heating type, protection class
- Occupancy: owner-occupied confirmation
- Coverage selected: dwelling, other structures, personal property,
  loss of use, personal liability (if available), medical payments
- Coverage amounts and deductibles
- Mortgagee / loss payee information
- Prior insurance carrier(s), denial date(s), and reason(s) for denial
- Loss history (typically 5 years)
- Kentucky FAIR Plan statutory disclosures and acknowledgments
- Applicant signature and date

When used:
- Submission to the Kentucky FAIR Plan after the applicant has been
  denied coverage in the voluntary market
- Renewal of an existing FAIR Plan homeowners policy
- Reporting significant property changes that affect coverage

Notes:
- FAIR Plan coverage is typically more limited than voluntary-market
  homeowners — often basic fire + extended perils with limited
  liability or no liability coverage
- Premium is generally higher than the voluntary market
- Companion forms in the Kentucky FAIR Plan family: 65KY (Dwelling
  Fire — non-owner-occupied), 66KY (Farm Fire), 68KY (Commercial Fire)
