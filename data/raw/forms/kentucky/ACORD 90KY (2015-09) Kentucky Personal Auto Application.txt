ACORD 90KY (2015-09) — Kentucky Personal Auto Application
==========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90KY
Edition: 2015-09
Title: Kentucky Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Kentucky state variant)
States: KY
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Kentucky. Captures
the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and state-required
disclosures needed to underwrite, bind, and issue a Kentucky auto
policy under Kentucky's Motor Vehicle Reparations Act (KRS 304.39).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM/UIM, PIP (no-fault) elections, MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages
- Discounts claimed
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Kentucky-required disclosures, PIP / no-fault election, and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Kentucky personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Kentucky policy
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Kentucky minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 split (or $60,000 CSL in
  lieu of split limits) under KRS § 304.39 (Motor Vehicle Reparations Act)
- Kentucky is a "choice no-fault" state — PIP is mandatory by DEFAULT
  at $10,000 minimum (KRS § 304.39-020); insured may reject the no-fault
  tort threshold in writing (KRS § 304.39-060) and retain full tort
  rights. Guest/pedestrian PIP still required even if insured rejects.
- Kentucky UM/UIM: insurer must OFFER; insured may reject in writing
  (KRS § 304.20-020)
- Primary statutes: KRS Chapter 304 (Insurance Code), KRS § 304.39
  (MVRA), KRS Chapter 186A (vehicle registration/verification)
- State insurance regulator: Kentucky Department of Insurance (KDI)
- Electronic verification: monthly VIN reports; AVIS/KAVIS database;
  30-day notice sent if VIN absent for 2 consecutive months
- Section version (290KY) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
