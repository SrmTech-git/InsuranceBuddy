ACORD 138KY (2015-09) — Kentucky Garage and Dealers
====================================================
Form number: ACORD 138KY
Edition: 2015-09
Title: Kentucky Garage and Dealers
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Kentucky state variant)
States: KY
Policy term: 12 months (typical for commercial garage policy)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change, audit-driven adjustment

Purpose:
Kentucky-specific Garage and Dealers section. Captures business
information, exposures, coverages, and Kentucky-required disclosures
for a Garage / Dealers policy under Kentucky's Motor Vehicle
Reparations Act (KRS 304.39). State-specific overlay to the national
ACORD 128 (Garage and Dealers Section).

Captures:
- Insured business type: new-car dealer, used-car dealer, service station,
  body shop, repair shop, towing operation, etc.
- Dealer / business license number(s)
- Premises: locations, age, square footage, lot size
- Operations: gross receipts, vehicles in inventory / sold / serviced
- Garage liability (premises and operations)
- Auto Liability (covered autos)
- Garagekeepers (CCC — care, custody, control)
- Products and Completed Operations
- UM/UIM for covered autos
- PIP (no-fault) — applies to garage covered autos in KY by default
- Kentucky-required dealer disclosures and acknowledgments

When used:
- New garage policy submission for a Kentucky auto-related business
- Renewal where Kentucky-specific coverages or dealer info are reselected
- Adding/removing locations or operations
- Midterm endorsement that changes coverage or operations

Notes:
- Kentucky is a "choice no-fault" state — PIP applies to garage covered
  autos by default
- Kentucky dealer regulations are administered by the Kentucky Motor
  Vehicle Commission
- Kentucky personal-auto minimums applicable to garage covered autos:
  25/50/25 or $60K CSL (KRS § 304.39); default PIP $10K applies
- Primary statutes: KRS 304 (Insurance), KRS 304.39 (MVRA)
- State regulator: Kentucky Department of Insurance (KDI)
