ACORD 60KY (2017-11) — Kentucky Auto Supplement – Rejection of Uninsured / Underinsured Motorist Coverage
==========================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 60KY
Edition: 2017-11
Title: Kentucky Auto Supplement – Rejection of Uninsured / Underinsured Motorist Coverage
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Kentucky state variant)
States: KY
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; rejection persists until revoked in writing
Transaction types: New business (UM/UIM rejection election); change to UM/UIM coverage

Purpose:
Captures the Kentucky insured's rejection of Uninsured Motorists (UM)
and/or Underinsured Motorists (UIM) coverage. Kentucky offers UM and
UIM coverage as part of every auto policy by default; the insured may
reject either or both coverages in writing per Kentucky's Motor
Vehicle Reparations Act (KRS 304.39) and KRS 304.20-020.

Captures:
- Statutory notice describing UM/UIM coverage purposes
- Insured's election to reject UM coverage (if applicable)
- Insured's election to reject UIM coverage (if applicable)
- Insured's election to select limits lower than BI liability
  (if rejection not made but reduction elected)
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Kentucky auto submission — UM/UIM are provided by default unless
  rejected via this form
- Renewal or midterm change where UM/UIM is being rejected or reduced
- Anytime the insured wishes to reinstate or modify UM/UIM coverage

Notes:
- Kentucky UM and UIM are coverages offered by default with every auto
  policy; written rejection is required to remove either coverage
- Rejection remains effective until revoked in writing
- Some Kentucky case law treats UIM rejection requirements strictly —
  carriers must obtain a knowing, written waiver
- Kentucky UM/UIM: insurer must OFFER; insured may reject in writing
  (KRS § 304.20-020). UM minimum 25/50 (matches BI minimum).
- Kentucky PIP/no-fault rejection is handled separately under KRS
  § 304.39-060 — typically a distinct rejection form, not this 60KY
  (which covers UM/UIM only)
- Kentucky liability minimums: 25/50/25 split or $60K CSL
