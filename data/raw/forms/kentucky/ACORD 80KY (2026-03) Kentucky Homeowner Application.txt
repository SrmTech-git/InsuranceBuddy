ACORD 80KY (2026-03) — Kentucky Homeowner Application
======================================================
Form number: ACORD 80KY
Edition: 2026-03
Title: Kentucky Homeowner Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Kentucky state variant)
States: KY
Policy term: 12 months (typical for homeowners)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change

Purpose:
Standalone application for a homeowners policy in Kentucky. Captures
applicant, dwelling, coverages, underwriting questions, and Kentucky-
required disclosures needed to underwrite and issue an HO policy.
State-specific variant of national ACORD 80.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Property location and address
- Property description: construction type, year built, square footage,
  number of stories, roof type and age, heating type and age, electrical,
  plumbing, foundation
- Protection class (PPC)
- Occupancy: primary / secondary / seasonal
- Coverage form: HO-2 / HO-3 (typical) / HO-5 / HO-6 / HO-8
- Coverage A (Dwelling) through Coverage F (Medical Payments)
- Deductible selected
- Discounts: multi-policy, alarm, smoke detectors, age, claim-free
- Endorsements: scheduled personal property, water backup,
  earthquake (Kentucky's western counties are in the New Madrid
  Seismic Zone — earthquake coverage is a notable consideration)
- Mortgagee / loss payee information
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history (typically 5 years)
- Underwriting questions: dogs, pools, business use, woodstoves
- Mine subsidence acknowledgment (Kentucky has mining history in
  several counties — see companion 67KY-equivalent or the multi-state
  ACORD 67 Mine Subsidence Supplement)
- Kentucky-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Kentucky homeowners policy
- Renewal where the carrier requires a fresh application
- Adding endorsements or scheduled property
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Kentucky's western counties lie within the New Madrid Seismic Zone —
  earthquake endorsement is a meaningful consideration not present in
  most non-quake states
- Kentucky is one of four states in the multi-state ACORD 67 Mine
  Subsidence Supplement (IL/IN/KY/WV) — mine subsidence is a relevant
  Kentucky homeowner exposure in former mining counties
