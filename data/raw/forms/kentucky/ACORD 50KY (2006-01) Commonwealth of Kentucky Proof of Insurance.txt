ACORD 50KY (2006-01) — Commonwealth of Kentucky Proof of Insurance
===================================================================
Form number: ACORD 50KY
Edition: 2006-01
Title: Commonwealth of Kentucky Proof of Insurance
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Kentucky state variant)
States: KY
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); reissued at each renewal
Transaction types: N/A (proof-of-insurance card, not a transaction document)

Purpose:
Proof of mandatory liability and no-fault insurance issued under a
Kentucky personal auto policy. Carried in the vehicle to satisfy
Kentucky's Motor Vehicle Reparations Act (KRS 304.39), which requires
liability coverage and Personal Injury Protection (no-fault) by default.

Captures:
- Insured's name and address
- Policy number
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Year, make, model, and VIN of the insured vehicle
- Insurer name and NAIC code
- Agent / agency name and contact (if applicable)

When used:
- Carried in the vehicle as proof of insurance
- Presented to law enforcement during a traffic stop or accident investigation
- Presented to the Kentucky Transportation Cabinet for registration / renewal
- Required at accident scenes to verify financial responsibility

Notes:
- Kentucky is a "choice no-fault" state — PIP (Personal Injury Protection)
  applies by default, but drivers may reject the no-fault tort threshold
  in writing on a separate election form
- "Commonwealth of Kentucky" reflects Kentucky's status as one of four
  U.S. commonwealths (terminology choice on the card itself)
- [TBD — verify whether the 2006-01 edition is still the current
  ACORD-published edition for Kentucky proof-of-insurance cards]
- Kentucky minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 (or $60,000 CSL in lieu
  of split limits) — Kentucky Motor Vehicle Reparations Act
- Kentucky default PIP coverage: $10,000 minimum, required by default
  under KRS § 304.39-020 (covers medical + lost wages up to $200/wk
  regardless of fault); may be rejected in writing except for motorcycles
- State insurance regulator: Kentucky Department of Insurance (KDI)
- Electronic verification: monthly VIN reports to Dept. of Vehicle
  Regulation; AVIS/KAVIS database
