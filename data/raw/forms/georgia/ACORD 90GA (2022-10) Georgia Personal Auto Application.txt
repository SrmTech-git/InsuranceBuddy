ACORD 90GA (2022-10) — Georgia Personal Auto Application
=========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90GA
Edition: 2022-10
Title: Georgia Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Georgia. Captures
the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and state-required
disclosures needed to underwrite, bind, and issue a Georgia auto policy
under Georgia's compulsory insurance law (O.C.G.A. § 33-34).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM (with Georgia's "added-on vs reduced-by"
  election), MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages
- Discounts claimed
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Georgia-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Georgia personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Georgia policy
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Georgia's UM coverage has a unique "added-on vs reduced-by" election —
  the insured chooses whether UM stacks on top of the at-fault driver's
  coverage ("added on" / true excess) or is offset by it ("reduced by" /
  difference-in-limits). Election typically required at app stage.
- Georgia uses an electronic insurance database (GEICO — Georgia
  Electronic Insurance Compliance Online) — carriers report active
  policy status electronically
- Companion forms commonly used:
  - 62GA — UM Coverage Selection/Rejection
  - 54GA / 55GA — SR-22 / SR-22A (Safety Responsibility certificates,
    when required for high-risk drivers)
- Section version (290GA) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
- Georgia minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-10)
- NEW: 2025 SB 121 (Act 287, effective May 14, 2025; O.C.G.A. § 33-7-16)
  triggers ENHANCED minimums for DUI convictions for 3 years:
  * 1st DUI conviction: 50/100/50
  * 2nd+ DUI conviction: higher tier
- Georgia UM/UIM: must include in every policy (add-on default); must
  REJECT IN WRITING to remove (O.C.G.A. § 33-7-11). Two structures:
  ADD-ON (excess on top of at-fault BI — default) or REDUCED-BY (DIL)
- GA is at-fault tort with MODIFIED COMPARATIVE NEGLIGENCE (50% bar)
- Primary statutes: O.C.G.A. § 40-6-10 (minimums), § 33-7-11 (UM/UIM),
  § 33-24-44/45 (cancellation/non-renewal), § 33-6-34 (Unfair Claims),
  § 33-7-16 (DUI enhanced minimums)
- State regulator: Georgia Office of the Commissioner of Insurance and
  Safety Fire (OCI) — "Safety Fire" reflects the Commissioner's
  additional fire-safety oversight role, unique to Georgia
- Electronic verification: GEICS (Georgia Electronic Insurance
  Compliance System) under O.C.G.A. § 40-2-137 — real-time reporting;
  10+ day lapse triggers automatic notification
