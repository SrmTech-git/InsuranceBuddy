ACORD 137GA (2025-08) — Georgia Commercial Auto
================================================
Form number: ACORD 137GA
Edition: 2025-08
Title: Georgia Commercial Auto
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: 12 months (typical for commercial auto)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change, audit-driven adjustment

Purpose:
Georgia-specific Commercial Auto Coverages section. Captures coverage
selections, limits, deductibles, and Georgia-required disclosures for a
commercial auto policy under O.C.G.A. § 33-34. Used alongside national
ACORD 125 (Applicant Info) and ACORD 127 (Business Auto Section).

Captures:
- Liability limits (BI/PD split or Combined Single Limit)
- Comp / collision deductibles
- UM coverage selections (Georgia "added-on vs reduced-by" election)
- MedPay
- Hired Auto Liability
- Non-Owned Auto Liability
- Drive Other Car coverage (where applicable)
- Towing & labor / rental reimbursement / loss of use
- Trailer interchange (for trucking)
- Georgia-required disclosures and acknowledgments

When used:
- New commercial auto submission for a Georgia-based fleet
- Renewal where Georgia-specific coverages are being reselected
- Midterm endorsement that changes coverage or limits

Notes:
- Georgia's UM "added-on vs reduced-by" election applies to commercial
  as well as personal — captured here or on companion form 62GA
- Georgia uses electronic insurance database (GEICO)
- SR-22 / SR-22A (Georgia forms 54GA / 55GA) may apply for high-risk
  drivers operating commercial vehicles
- Georgia personal-auto minimum limits: 25/50/25 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-10).
  Commercial fleets subject to federal MCS-90 for interstate operations.
- 2025 SB 121 (eff. May 14, 2025) triggers enhanced minimums for DUI
  convictions for 3 years (1st: 50/100/50; 2nd+ higher)
- Georgia UM/UIM: add-on default; reject in writing (O.C.G.A. § 33-7-11)
- State regulator: Georgia OCI (Commissioner of Insurance and Safety Fire)
- Electronic verification: GEICS
