ACORD 80GA (2025-03) — Georgia Homeowner Application
=====================================================
Form number: ACORD 80GA
Edition: 2025-03
Title: Georgia Homeowner Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: 12 months (typical for homeowners)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change

Purpose:
Standalone application for a homeowners policy in Georgia. Captures
applicant, dwelling, coverages, underwriting questions, and Georgia-
required disclosures needed to underwrite and issue an HO policy.
State-specific variant of national ACORD 80.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Property location and address
- Property description: construction type, year built, square footage,
  number of stories, roof type and age, heating, electrical, plumbing,
  foundation
- Protection class (PPC)
- Distance to coast (Georgia coastal properties — Savannah, Brunswick,
  Sea Islands — have wind exposure)
- Occupancy: primary / secondary / seasonal
- Coverage form: HO-2 / HO-3 / HO-5 / HO-6 / HO-8
- Coverage A through F at selected limits
- Deductible selected (all perils)
- Hurricane / named-storm deductible (Georgia coastal properties)
- Wind/hail deductible (where separate)
- Discounts: multi-policy, alarm, smoke detectors, age, claim-free,
  fortified roof
- Endorsements: scheduled personal property, water backup, service line,
  equipment breakdown
- Mortgagee / loss payee information
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Underwriting questions: dogs, pools, business use, woodstoves
- Georgia-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Georgia homeowners policy
- Renewal where the carrier requires a fresh application
- Adding endorsements or scheduled property
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Georgia coastal counties typically have hurricane / named-storm
  deductibles
- Tornado exposure across the state is meaningful — Georgia is in the
  southern tornado corridor
- Flood is NOT covered under the HO policy — separate NFIP or private
  flood policy needed
