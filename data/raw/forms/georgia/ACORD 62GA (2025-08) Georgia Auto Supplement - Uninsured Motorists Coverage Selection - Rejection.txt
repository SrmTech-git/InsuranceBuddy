ACORD 62GA (2025-08) — Georgia Auto Supplement – Uninsured Motorists Coverage Selection / Rejection
====================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 62GA
Edition: 2025-08
Title: Georgia Auto Supplement – Uninsured Motorists Coverage Selection / Rejection
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (UM election required, including added-on/reduced-by choice); change of UM coverage

Purpose:
Captures the Georgia insured's Uninsured Motorists (UM) coverage
selection — including Georgia's distinctive "added-on vs reduced-by"
election — at the chosen limits, or rejection of UM coverage in
writing. Georgia statute (O.C.G.A. § 33-7-11) gives the insured two
distinct structural choices for how UM applies relative to the
at-fault driver's coverage.

Captures:
- UM coverage offer at specified limits
- Election between "Added-On" UM (true excess; UM stacks ON TOP of
  the at-fault driver's coverage) or "Reduced-By" UM (UM is offset
  by the at-fault driver's coverage — difference-in-limits)
- Limits selected
- Rejection of UM coverage (in writing, where permitted)
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Georgia auto submission — UM election (including added-on/
  reduced-by choice) required
- Renewal where UM coverage is being modified
- Midterm change to UM coverage

Notes:
- Georgia's "Added-On vs Reduced-By" UM is distinctive nationally.
  Added-on UM provides true excess recovery — e.g., $100K UM + $25K
  at-fault BI = $125K total available. Reduced-by UM provides DIL
  recovery — $100K UM − $25K at-fault BI = $75K UM available.
- Added-on is typically more expensive and provides better protection;
  reduced-by is cheaper but recovery-limited
- Companion to the Georgia personal/commercial auto application
- Georgia UM minimum: matches BI minimum 25/50 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-10).
  UM/UIM under O.C.G.A. § 33-7-11 — must include in every policy as
  ADD-ON default; insured must REJECT IN WRITING to remove.
- ~12% uninsured driver rate in Georgia — UM retention strongly
  recommended; "Added-On" structure provides true excess recovery
  while "Reduced-By" provides DIL recovery
