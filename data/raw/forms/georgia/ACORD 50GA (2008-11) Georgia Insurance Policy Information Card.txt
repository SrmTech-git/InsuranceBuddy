ACORD 50GA (2008-11) — Georgia Insurance Policy Information Card
=================================================================
Form number: ACORD 50GA
Edition: 2008-11
Title: Georgia Insurance Policy Information Card
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); reissued at each renewal
Transaction types: N/A (proof-of-insurance card, not a transaction document)

Purpose:
Proof of mandatory liability insurance issued under a Georgia personal
auto policy. Carried in the vehicle to satisfy Georgia's compulsory
insurance law (O.C.G.A. § 33-34), which requires every owner to maintain
minimum liability coverage.

Captures:
- Insured's name and address
- Policy number
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Year, make, model, and VIN of the insured vehicle
- Insurer name and NAIC code
- Agent / agency name and contact (if applicable)

When used:
- Carried in the vehicle as proof of insurance
- Presented to law enforcement during a traffic stop or accident investigation
- Presented to the Georgia Department of Revenue for registration / renewal
- Required at accident scenes

Notes:
- Georgia uses an electronic insurance database (GEICO — Georgia Electronic
  Insurance Compliance Online) — carriers report active policy status
  electronically; the ID card supplements the electronic record
- Title says "Policy Information Card" rather than "Identification Card"
  — Georgia-specific terminology
