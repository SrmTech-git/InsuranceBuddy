ACORD 54GA (2026-05) — Georgia Safety Responsibility Insurance Certificate (SR-22)
===================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 54GA
Edition: 2026-05
Title: Georgia Safety Responsibility Insurance Certificate (SR-22)
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; SR-22 filing typically required for a multi-year period from the triggering event (see notes for duration)
Transaction types: SR-22 filing (new), termination/cancellation of SR-22 (must be reported to state), renewal

Purpose:
Georgia-specific SR-22 financial responsibility filing. SR-22 is a
state filing — NOT a separate insurance policy — that certifies to
the Georgia Department of Driver Services (DDS) that a driver
carries automobile liability insurance meeting Georgia minimum
requirements. The SR-22 is required by the Georgia Safety
Responsibility Law for drivers who have had certain driving
infractions and need to demonstrate continuous financial
responsibility to maintain or restore their license.

Captures:
- Driver's name, address, DOB, driver's license number
- Driver's license issuing state
- Insurance carrier name and NAIC code
- Policy number
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Liability limits carried (must meet or exceed Georgia minimum
  BI/PD limits)
- Type of SR-22 filing:
  * Operator (covers the driver in any vehicle they drive,
    including non-owned)
  * Owner (covers vehicles the driver owns)
  * Operator-Owner (combined)
- Certification by the carrier to the Georgia DDS
- Insured / driver signature acknowledgment
- Filing transmission to Georgia DDS

When used:
- Required filing after triggering driving infractions in Georgia —
  typically standard violation categories (multiple moving violations,
  certain at-fault accident situations, license suspension reinstatement)
- Required for license / registration reinstatement after suspension
- Required to maintain license validity during the filing period
- Termination notice required when policy cancels (cancellation
  reported by carrier to state — typically triggers license suspension)

Notes:
- SR-22 is a CERTIFICATE / FILING, not an insurance policy. The
  underlying liability policy is the actual insurance; SR-22 is the
  state-required proof.
- Companion form: 55GA — Georgia SR-22A (the variant used in more
  serious situations involving security/bond posting or repeated
  no-insurance violations — see 55GA card for details)
- Cancellation or lapse of the underlying policy triggers automatic
  notification to the Georgia DDS and typically results in license
  suspension
- Georgia Safety Responsibility Law: O.C.G.A. § 40-9 et seq.;
  SR-22 specifically O.C.G.A. § 40-5-71
- SR-22 filing duration in Georgia: typically 3 years continuous
  coverage from the triggering event. Lapse triggers SR-26 to GA DDS
  → immediate re-suspension.
- 2025 SB 121 (Act 287, eff. May 14, 2025; O.C.G.A. § 33-7-16):
  DUI convictions trigger ENHANCED liability minimums (50/100/50 for
  1st DUI, higher for 2nd+) for 3 years — applies during SR-22 period
