ACORD 55GA (2026-05) — Georgia Safety Responsibility Insurance Certificate (SR-22A)
====================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 55GA
Edition: 2026-05
Title: Georgia Safety Responsibility Insurance Certificate (SR-22A)
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; SR-22A filing duration set by Georgia Safety Responsibility Law
Transaction types: SR-22A filing (new), termination/cancellation (must be reported to state), renewal

Purpose:
Georgia-specific SR-22A financial responsibility filing — the more
stringent variant of the SR-22 used in situations involving multiple
uninsured-driving convictions, post-crash bond/security posting
under Georgia's Safety Responsibility Law, or unsatisfied judgments.
SR-22A imposes the additional requirement that the insured PREPAY
the full policy premium in advance for a 6-month policy term — this
guarantees continuous coverage and is meant to address drivers who
have demonstrated a pattern of letting coverage lapse.

Captures:
- Driver's name, address, DOB, driver's license number
- Driver's license issuing state
- Insurance carrier name and NAIC code
- Policy number
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Liability limits carried (must meet or exceed Georgia minimum
  BI/PD)
- Certification by the carrier that the full 6-month policy premium
  has been PREPAID in advance (the defining feature of SR-22A vs SR-22)
- Type of SR-22A filing (operator / owner / operator-owner)
- Certification by the carrier to the Georgia Department of Driver
  Services (DDS)
- Insured / driver signature acknowledgment
- Filing transmission to Georgia DDS

When used:
- Required filing after specific triggering events:
  * Multiple driving-without-insurance convictions
  * License reinstatement after a second or subsequent conviction
    for driving without insurance
  * Unsatisfied judgments from a motor vehicle accident
  * Post-crash security/bond posting requirements under Georgia's
    Safety Responsibility Law
- Required for license / registration reinstatement in these
  situations
- Termination notice required when policy cancels

Notes:
- KEY DIFFERENCE FROM SR-22: SR-22A requires PREPAYMENT of the full
  6-month policy premium in advance. Standard SR-22 (54GA) does not
  require prepayment — the policy can be paid in installments.
- Required filing duration in Georgia: typically 3 years of
  continuous coverage from the triggering event, requiring sequential
  6-month prepaid policy terms; [TBD verify current Georgia duration]
- The prepayment requirement reflects the regulatory concern that
  drivers triggering SR-22A obligations have demonstrated a pattern
  of letting coverage lapse — prepayment guarantees uninterrupted
  coverage for the prepaid period
- Georgia DDS may also require posting a cashier's check, certified
  check, money order, real property bond, or surety bond as
  additional security in some cases
- SR-22A is a CERTIFICATE / FILING, not an insurance policy
- Companion form: 54GA — Georgia SR-22 (the standard variant for
  general triggering events without prepayment requirement)
- Georgia Safety Responsibility Law: O.C.G.A. § 40-9 et seq.;
  SR-22 statute: O.C.G.A. § 40-5-71
- SR-22A duration: typically 3 years of continuous coverage via
  sequential 6-month prepaid policy terms
- 2025 SB 121 (Act 287, eff. May 14, 2025; O.C.G.A. § 33-7-16): DUI
  convictions trigger ENHANCED liability minimums (50/100/50 for 1st
  DUI; higher for 2nd+) for 3 years — applies during SR-22A period
