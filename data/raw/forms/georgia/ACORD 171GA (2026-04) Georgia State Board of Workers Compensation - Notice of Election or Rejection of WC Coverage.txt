ACORD 171GA (2026-04) — Georgia State Board of Workers' Compensation – Notice of Election or Rejection of Workers' Compensation Coverage
=========================================================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 171GA
Edition: 2026-04
Title: Georgia State Board of Workers' Compensation – Notice of Election or Rejection of Workers' Compensation Coverage
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: Matches the underlying WC policy term (typically 12 months); election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (election/rejection notice), renewal, change in election

Purpose:
Georgia Workers' Compensation election or rejection form filed with
the Georgia State Board of Workers' Compensation. Georgia allows
certain workers — sole proprietors, partners, LLC members, and
corporate officers of small businesses — to elect or reject WC
coverage. This form documents that election or rejection on file
with the State Board.

Captures:
- Employer / business information
- Business structure (sole proprietorship, partnership, LLC, corporation)
- Individual's name and role (sole proprietor, partner, LLC member,
  corporate officer)
- Election or rejection of WC coverage
- Effective date of the election or rejection
- Statement that an excluded/rejecting individual is not entitled to
  WC benefits if injured
- Individual signature and date
- Authorized employer signatory
- Filing acknowledgment with the Georgia State Board of WC

When used:
- New Georgia WC submission where eligible individuals are making
  election decisions
- Renewal where existing elections are being maintained or modified
- Midterm change when an individual's election decision changes
- Filed with the Georgia State Board of WC to be on the official record

Notes:
- Georgia State Board of Workers' Compensation administers WC under
  O.C.G.A. Title 34, Chapter 9
- Georgia WC threshold: employers with 3 or more regular employees
  are required to carry WC; below threshold, coverage is optional —
  [TBD verify current employee threshold]
- Default treatment for owners/officers varies by entity:
  * Sole proprietors and partners — typically excluded; may elect IN
  * LLC members — typically excluded; may elect IN
  * Corporate officers — typically auto-included; may opt OUT
    (subject to limits on number of officers excludable)
