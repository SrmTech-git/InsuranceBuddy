ACORD 290GA (2022-10) — Georgia Personal Automobile Application Section
========================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290GA
Edition: 2022-10
Title: Georgia Personal Automobile Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Georgia state variant)
States: GA
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Georgia. Used
in combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application —
Applicant Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The
290GA holds the auto-specific data needed to underwrite a Georgia auto
policy under O.C.G.A. § 33-34.

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM (with Georgia's "added-on vs reduced-by"
  election), MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Georgia-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity is captured on ACORD 88, not duplicated here)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290GA) rather than the standalone full app (90GA)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Georgia UM has a unique "added-on vs reduced-by" election — captured
  here or on companion form 62GA (UM Coverage Selection/Rejection)
- Georgia uses electronic insurance database (GEICO — Georgia Electronic
  Insurance Compliance Online)
- Use 290GA with ACORD 88; use 90GA standalone
- Georgia minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-10).
  2025 SB 121 (eff. May 14, 2025) triggers enhanced minimums for DUI
  convictions (1st: 50/100/50; 2nd+ higher) for 3 years.
- Georgia UM/UIM: add-on default; reject in writing (O.C.G.A. § 33-7-11)
- GA is at-fault with modified comparative negligence (50% bar)
- State regulator: Georgia OCI (Commissioner of Insurance and Safety Fire)
- Electronic verification: GEICS
