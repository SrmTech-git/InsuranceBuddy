ACORD 67 (2025-01) — Illinois / Indiana / Kentucky / West Virginia Mine Subsidence Property Insurance Supplement
=================================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 67
Edition: 2025-01
Title: Illinois / Indiana / Kentucky / West Virginia Mine Subsidence Property Insurance Supplement
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, multi-state regional form)
States: IL, IN, KY, WV
Policy term: Matches the underlying property policy (typically 12 months); election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (mine subsidence offer/election); change in coverage; election change at renewal

Purpose:
Multi-state supplement for Mine Subsidence Insurance — covering
property damage caused by the collapse of underground mines —
applicable in Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, and West Virginia. Standard
homeowners and dwelling fire policies exclude mine subsidence; each
of these four states operates a state-administered mine subsidence
insurance fund / pool that funds coverage offered through standard
policies as an endorsement at state-set premiums and limits.

Captures:
- Property location and county (eligibility is typically county-
  specific based on historical mining activity)
- Property type (owner-occupied dwelling, dwelling fire / rental,
  commercial)
- State-specific election to accept or reject mine subsidence
  coverage (each of the four states has its own mandatory-offer rules)
- Coverage limit selected (state pools typically cap limits, e.g.,
  up to $300,000 — varies by state and property type)
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New property policy submission in an eligible mining county in IL,
  IN, KY, or WV — mandatory offer typically required
- Renewal where the mine subsidence election is being changed
- Reporting property changes that affect eligibility

Notes:
- State mine subsidence pools:
  * Illinois Mine Subsidence Insurance Reserve Fund (administered
    through the IL DOI)
  * Indiana Mine Subsidence Insurance Fund
  * Kentucky Mine Subsidence Insurance Fund
  * West Virginia Mine Subsidence Insurance Fund
- Coverage is for ground sinking / settling caused by collapse of
  underground mine workings — distinct from earth movement (typically
  excluded from HO) and earthquake (also typically excluded; see
  separate earthquake notices)
- Mandatory-offer counties vary by state — based on historical
  underground mining (predominantly coal)
- Companion Ohio forms: 67OH and 68OH cover Ohio mine subsidence
  separately under Ohio's own program
