ACORD 64IA (1998-02) — Iowa Auto Supplement – UM/UIM Stacking Coverage Notice
==============================================================================
Form number: ACORD 64IA
Edition: 1998-02
Title: Iowa Auto Supplement – UM/UIM Stacking Coverage Notice
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Iowa state variant)
States: IA
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (election required if multiple covered autos); change of stacking election

Purpose:
Iowa-specific notice and election form addressing UM/UIM "stacking" —
the practice of combining UM or UIM limits across multiple insured
vehicles on a single policy or across separate policies in the same
household. Iowa permits or restricts stacking under certain conditions;
this form notifies the insured and captures any election.

Captures:
- Statutory notice describing stacking and its effect on UM/UIM
  available limits
- Election regarding stacking (typically election to stack or not stack)
- Insured acknowledgment and signature

When used:
- New Iowa auto submission with multiple covered autos
- Adding a vehicle to an existing policy
- Anytime the stacking election is being made or changed

Notes:
- Stacking can dramatically change UM/UIM recovery: e.g., 3 vehicles at
  $100K UM each could provide $300K total if stacking is permitted, or
  $100K if not
- Iowa UM/UIM statutory authority: Iowa Code § 516A.1
- Iowa UIM is REDUCED-BY (DIL) structure — UIM limits must exceed
  at-fault driver's BI limits for any coverage to apply
- Stacking and anti-stacking provisions are governed by policy
  language and Iowa case law
- Companion forms: 61IA (Offer/Rejection) and 62IA (Notice)
