ACORD 66IA (2006-07) — Iowa Personal Insurance Supplement – Disclosure Use of Claims History
=============================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 66IA
Edition: 2006-07
Title: Iowa Personal Insurance Supplement – Disclosure Use of Claims History
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Iowa state variant)
States: IA
Policy term: Disclosure delivered at issuance and at periodic renewals
Transaction types: New business (disclosure required), renewal (re-disclosure where required)

Purpose:
Iowa-required disclosure informing personal insurance applicants and
insureds that the carrier uses claims history information (typically
obtained from CLUE — Comprehensive Loss Underwriting Exchange — and
similar claims databases) in underwriting, rating, and renewal
decisions. The disclosure helps the consumer understand how prior
claims affect insurance availability and pricing.

Captures:
- Statutory disclosure that the carrier uses claims history in
  underwriting and rating decisions
- Description of typical sources: CLUE (auto and property), A-PLUS,
  internal claims records, third-party claims databases
- Description of how claims history is used: underwriting (accept /
  decline), rating (premium adjustment), renewal decisions, eligibility
  for discounts
- Insured's rights under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) and
  Iowa Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Act to obtain
  the report and dispute inaccuracies
- Insured acknowledgment of receipt
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Iowa personal insurance submission — disclosure delivered with
  application
- Renewal — many carriers redeliver the disclosure annually
- After adverse underwriting decision based on claims history (where
  required by FCRA)

Notes:
- Iowa Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Act (Iowa Code
  Ch. 505B or similar — [TBD verify exact citation]) governs use of
  personal information including claims history
- FCRA (federal) also governs adverse-action notices when claims-
  history-based decisions are made
- Insureds have the right to obtain a free annual CLUE report from
  LexisNexis (and similar disclosures from other providers)
- Companion forms: 65IA (aftermarket parts), 67IA (extraordinary
  life circumstances)
