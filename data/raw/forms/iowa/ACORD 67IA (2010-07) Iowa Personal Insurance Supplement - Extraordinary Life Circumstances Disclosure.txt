ACORD 67IA (2010-07) — Iowa Personal Insurance Supplement – Extraordinary Life Circumstances Disclosure
========================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 67IA
Edition: 2010-07
Title: Iowa Personal Insurance Supplement – Extraordinary Life Circumstances Disclosure
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Iowa state variant)
States: IA
Policy term: Disclosure delivered at issuance and at periodic renewals
Transaction types: New business (disclosure required), renewal (re-disclosure where required)

Purpose:
Iowa-required disclosure addressing how insurers may consider
"extraordinary life circumstances" when making underwriting and rating
decisions for personal insurance. Iowa law recognizes that certain
life events (catastrophic illness, divorce, death of a family member,
unemployment, identity theft, etc.) may temporarily affect a consumer's
financial or credit profile in ways that don't reflect ordinary
underwriting risk. The disclosure informs insureds of their right to
request consideration of such circumstances.

Captures:
- Statutory description of "extraordinary life circumstances"
  recognized under Iowa law:
  * Catastrophic illness or injury
  * Death of a spouse, child, or parent
  * Loss of employment for 3+ months
  * Divorce
  * Identity theft
  * Temporary loss of income
  * Military deployment
  * Natural disaster affecting the insured
- Insured's right to request that the carrier consider the
  circumstance in underwriting or rating
- Process for requesting consideration (typically written request
  with supporting documentation)
- Insured acknowledgment of receipt of the disclosure
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Iowa personal insurance submission — disclosure delivered with
  application
- Renewal — many carriers redeliver the disclosure annually
- After adverse underwriting or rating decision where the insured
  may invoke an extraordinary life circumstance

Notes:
- The "extraordinary life circumstances" framework is part of Iowa's
  consumer-protection insurance regulation — typically tied to credit-
  based insurance scoring use
- Iowa is one of several states with extraordinary life circumstances
  provisions designed to mitigate adverse credit-scoring effects from
  temporary hardship
- Companion forms: 65IA (aftermarket parts), 66IA (claims history use)
