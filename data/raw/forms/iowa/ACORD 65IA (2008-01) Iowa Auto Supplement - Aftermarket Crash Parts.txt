ACORD 65IA (2008-01) — Iowa Auto Supplement – Aftermarket Crash Parts
======================================================================
Form number: ACORD 65IA
Edition: 2008-01
Title: Iowa Auto Supplement – Aftermarket Crash Parts
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Iowa state variant)
States: IA
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; disclosure delivered at issuance
Transaction types: New business (disclosure required), renewal (re-disclosure where required)

Purpose:
Iowa-required disclosure to auto insureds explaining that the policy
may pay for repairs using aftermarket (non-OEM, non-original-
equipment-manufacturer) crash parts rather than OEM parts. Iowa
statute requires carriers to disclose this practice to insureds so
they understand the potential parts-source difference at the time
of repair after a covered loss.

Captures:
- Statutory disclosure that the carrier may specify or pay for the
  use of aftermarket crash parts in repairs
- Description of "aftermarket parts" (non-OEM replacement parts —
  may be made by various manufacturers, certified or non-certified)
- Insured's rights regarding parts selection (where applicable
  under Iowa law)
- Statement of the warranty / quality standards for aftermarket
  parts the carrier specifies
- Insured acknowledgment of receipt
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Iowa auto policy issuance — disclosure delivered with the
  application
- Renewal — many carriers redeliver the disclosure annually
- Reporting of an auto claim — disclosure may be reiterated when
  parts selection is being discussed

Notes:
- Aftermarket crash parts have been a long-standing consumer-protection
  issue — concerns about fit, quality, and safety relative to OEM
- Iowa Insurance Division regulates this disclosure under Iowa
  insurance regulations (specific code section [TBD verify])
- Insureds may have the option to upgrade to OEM parts at their own
  cost in some cases — depends on carrier policy and Iowa rules

  (other Iowa personal lines disclosures)
