ACORD 85MN (2024-04) — Minnesota Mobile Home Application
=========================================================
Form number: ACORD 85MN
Edition: 2024-04
Title: Minnesota Mobile Home Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for mobile home insurance)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change

Purpose:
Standalone application for a mobile home (manufactured home) policy
in Minnesota. Captures applicant, mobile home unit, coverages,
underwriting questions, and Minnesota-required disclosures.
State-specific variant of national ACORD 85.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Mobile home location and address (park name if in mobile home park)
- Mobile home description: make, model, year, VIN / serial number,
  dimensions (single-wide / double-wide / triple-wide), square footage
- Anchoring / tie-down details
- Setup details: foundation type, skirting
- Park or owned-lot information
- Coverage form: ACORD/HO mobile home equivalent (HO-7 / MH-style)
- Coverage A through F at selected limits
- Deductible selected
- Wind/hail deductible (separate hail deductibles are common in
  Minnesota due to hail exposure)
- Replacement cost vs actual cash value selection
- Discounts: multi-policy, alarm, age, claim-free
- Endorsements: trip / transit coverage, scheduled personal property,
  water backup, frozen-pipe / ice-dam coverage considerations
- Mortgagee / lienholder information
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Underwriting questions: dogs, age of mobile home, snow load capacity
- Minnesota-required disclosures and acknowledgments, including
  Insurance Guaranty Association notice
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Minnesota mobile home policy
- Renewal where the carrier requires a fresh application
- Adding endorsements
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Pre-1976 mobile homes are often declined or rated higher
- Hail damage is a major Minnesota mobile home exposure — separate
  wind/hail deductible common
- Snow load on roof is a meaningful underwriting consideration in
  Minnesota's climate
- Frozen pipe and ice dam exposure is heightened for mobile homes
  with less insulation than site-built homes
