ACORD 834MN (2014-12) — Minnesota Cyber and Privacy Coverage Section
=====================================================================
Form number: ACORD 834MN
Edition: 2014-12
Title: Minnesota Cyber and Privacy Coverage Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for cyber — claims-made, some first-party occurrence-style components)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change (especially common as carrier re-rates frequently)

Purpose:
Minnesota-specific section for Cyber and Privacy Liability coverage —
addresses data breach response, privacy liability, network security
liability, business interruption from cyber events, cyber extortion
(ransomware), and related exposures. The cyber market has changed
dramatically since the 2014-12 edition of this form (especially due
to ransomware proliferation 2018-onward), and underwriting questions
have grown more rigorous. Used alongside ACORD 125.

Captures:
- Insured entity information
- Industry and data sensitivity (healthcare, financial services,
  retail, etc.)
- Annual revenue
- Number of records held by data type:
  * PII (personally identifiable information)
  * PHI (protected health information — HIPAA-regulated)
  * PCI (payment card information — PCI DSS scope)
  * SSN, financial account, biometric, other regulated data
- Data security practices (often the heart of underwriting):
  * Encryption at rest and in transit
  * Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for remote access, privileged
    accounts, email
  * Backup practices: offline/immutable backups, restore testing
  * Patch management cadence
  * Endpoint detection and response (EDR) deployment
  * Security awareness training, phishing simulation
  * Network segmentation
  * Incident response plan, tabletop testing
- Prior cyber coverage and claims (cyber claims history is a major
  underwriting factor)
- Underwriting questions:
  * Prior data breaches (HIPAA, state breach notification triggers)
  * Prior ransomware events
  * Recent network intrusions, attempted intrusions
  * Vendor / third-party data sharing
- Coverage selections (cyber is multi-section):
  * First-party (insured's own losses):
    - Breach response (notification, credit monitoring, PR, forensics)
    - Business interruption from cyber events
    - Cyber extortion / ransomware
    - Data restoration / digital asset restoration
    - Reputational harm
  * Third-party (claims by others):
    - Privacy liability (HIPAA, state breach laws, FTC)
    - Network security liability (transmission of malware,
      contractual breach)
    - Regulatory defense and penalties
    - Media liability (online content, copyright, defamation)
- Limits, retentions, and sub-limits per coverage section
- Minnesota-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Minnesota cyber submission — section attached to ACORD 125
- Renewal where cyber coverage is being modified (often re-rated
  significantly each renewal as market conditions and security
  practices change)
- Reporting significant cyber exposure changes (M&A, new data
  collection, security incident)

Notes:
- Cyber market has hardened significantly since 2018; ransomware
  changed underwriting requirements (MFA, EDR, immutable backups
  often required)
- This 2014-12 edition predates the ransomware-driven market shift —
  newer ACORD cyber forms have substantially more detailed

- HIPAA (federal) and Minnesota breach notification law both apply
  to Minnesota PHI holders
- Companion forms: 807MN (D&O), 832MN (Misc E&O) — coordination
