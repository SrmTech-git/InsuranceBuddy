ACORD 137MN (2015-12) — Minnesota Commercial Auto
==================================================
Form number: ACORD 137MN
Edition: 2015-12
Title: Minnesota Commercial Auto
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for commercial auto)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change, audit-driven adjustment

Purpose:
Minnesota-specific Commercial Auto Coverages section. Captures coverage
selections, limits, deductibles, and the no-fault PIP / UM / UIM
elections required for commercial autos under Minnesota's No-Fault
Automobile Insurance Act (Minn. Stat. § 65B). Used alongside national
ACORD 125 and 127.

Captures:
- Liability limits (BI/PD split or Combined Single Limit)
- Comp / collision deductibles
- Mandatory PIP basic economic loss benefits (medical + non-medical)
- UM / UIM coverage selections (typically at limits equal to liability)
- MedPay
- Hired Auto Liability
- Non-Owned Auto Liability
- Drive Other Car coverage (where applicable)
- Towing & labor / rental reimbursement / loss of use
- Trailer interchange (for trucking)
- Minnesota-required disclosures, including Insurance Guaranty
  Association notice
- Acknowledgments and signatures

When used:
- New commercial auto submission for a Minnesota-based fleet
- Renewal where Minnesota-specific coverages are being reselected
- Midterm endorsement that changes coverage or limits

Notes:
- Minnesota is a no-fault state — PIP basic economic loss benefits are
  mandatory and apply to commercial autos as well
- Companion forms: 38MN (Authorization), 62MN (Auto Supplement),
  65MN (Guaranty Association Notice)
- Federal motor carrier minimums (MCS-90) may override state minimums
  for interstate trucking risks
- Minnesota personal-auto minimum limits: 30/60/10 (Minn. Stat. § 65B).
  Commercial fleets subject to federal MCS-90 for interstate operations.
- Mandatory PIP: $40,000 total ($20K medical + $20K non-medical);
  applies to commercial autos as well
- State regulator: Minnesota Department of Commerce
