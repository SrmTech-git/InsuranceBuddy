ACORD 290MN (2019-12) — Minnesota Personal Auto Application Section
====================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290MN
Edition: 2019-12
Title: Minnesota Personal Auto Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Minnesota. Used
in combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application —
Applicant Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The
290MN holds the auto-specific data and the no-fault PIP / UM / UIM
elections required under Minnesota's No-Fault Automobile Insurance Act
(Minn. Stat. § 65B).

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, mandatory PIP (basic economic loss), UM, UIM,
  comp, coll, optional coverages
- PIP basic economic loss benefit selection (medical + non-medical)
- UM/UIM elections (typically at limits equal to liability)
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Minnesota-required disclosures, including Insurance Guaranty
  Association notice
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity is captured on ACORD 88, not duplicated here)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290MN) rather than the standalone full app (90MN)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Minnesota is a no-fault state — PIP basic economic loss benefits are
  mandatory
- Companion forms commonly used: 38MN (authorization), 62MN (auto
  supplement), 65MN (Insurance Guaranty Association Notice)
- Use 290MN with ACORD 88; use 90MN standalone
- Minnesota minimum liability limits: 30/60/10 (Minn. Stat. § 65B)
- Mandatory PIP: $40,000 total ($20K medical + $20K non-medical)
- UM REQUIRED 25/50 minimum; UIM REQUIRED 25/50 minimum unless
  rejected in writing; UIM is REDUCED-BY (DIL) basis
- Primary statute: Minn. Stat. Ch. 65B
- State regulator: Minnesota Department of Commerce
