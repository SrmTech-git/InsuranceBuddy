ACORD 833MN (2014-12) — Minnesota Lawyers Professional Liability Section
=========================================================================
Form number: ACORD 833MN
Edition: 2014-12
Title: Minnesota Lawyers Professional Liability Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for Lawyers PL — claims-made)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change (partner additions/departures, practice area changes)

Purpose:
Minnesota-specific section for Lawyers Professional Liability (LPL)
coverage — the E&O policy for attorneys and law firms. Covers claims
arising from professional services rendered (or failed to render) as
a lawyer: malpractice, missed deadlines, conflicts of interest,
inadequate representation, etc. Used alongside ACORD 125.

Captures:
- Firm information: name, address, year established, structure
  (sole practitioner, partnership, LLP, PC, etc.)
- Bar admissions for each attorney
- Number of attorneys by class: equity partner, non-equity partner,
  associate, of counsel, contract attorney
- Practice area breakdown by revenue percentage (e.g., personal
  injury 30%, real estate 20%, business transactions 15%, family
  law 10%, etc.)
- Highest-risk practice areas:
  * Patent / IP / trademark prosecution (deadlines critical)
  * Securities (heightened regulatory exposure)
  * Trust / estate (large fiduciary exposure)
  * Class action plaintiff work
  * Personal injury (statute-of-limitations exposure)
- Annual gross fees and average matter value
- Largest single matter / case (revenue and exposure)
- Prior LPL coverage and claims (over 5-10 years typical)
- Underwriting questions:
  * Prior bar disciplinary actions or grievances
  * Conflicts management procedures and conflicts software
  * Trust account / IOLTA practices
  * Engagement letter / fee agreement practices
  * Calendaring and deadline-management systems
  * Lateral hire integration practices
- Coverage selections:
  * Limits (per claim, aggregate)
  * Retention
  * Retro date / prior acts coverage (extremely important for
    Lawyers PL — gaps create exposure)
  * Defense outside or inside limits
  * Extended Reporting Period (ERP / "tail") availability
  * Subpoena response / disciplinary defense sub-limits
- Minnesota-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Minnesota Lawyers PL submission — section attached to ACORD 125
- Renewal where LPL coverage is being modified
- Reporting significant firm changes (lateral partner additions,
  practice area expansion)

Notes:
- Lawyers PL is strictly claims-made — retro date and prior acts
  coverage are critical for continuity
- Lawyers face significant exposure from missed deadlines (statutes
  of limitations) and from "drift" claims discovered years after
  the underlying work
- Companion forms: 807MN (D&O), 832MN (Misc E&O), 838MN
  (Accountants PL)
- Minnesota state bar regulates attorneys; the Minnesota Lawyers
  Professional Responsibility Board handles disciplinary matters
