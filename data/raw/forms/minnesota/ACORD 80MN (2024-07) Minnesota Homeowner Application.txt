ACORD 80MN (2024-07) — Minnesota Homeowner Application
=======================================================
Form number: ACORD 80MN
Edition: 2024-07
Title: Minnesota Homeowner Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for homeowners)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change

Purpose:
Standalone application for a homeowners policy in Minnesota. Captures
applicant, dwelling, coverages, underwriting questions, and Minnesota-
required disclosures needed to underwrite and issue an HO policy.
State-specific variant of national ACORD 80.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Property location and address
- Property description: construction type, year built, square footage,
  number of stories, roof type and age, heating type (cold-climate
  factors more prominent), electrical, plumbing, foundation
- Protection class (PPC)
- Occupancy: primary / secondary / seasonal (cabin/lake property is
  common in Minnesota)
- Coverage form: HO-2 / HO-3 / HO-5 / HO-6 / HO-8
- Coverage A through F at selected limits
- Deductible selected
- Wind/hail deductible (separate hail deductibles are common in
  Minnesota due to hail exposure)
- Discounts: multi-policy, alarm, smoke detectors, age, claim-free
- Endorsements: scheduled personal property, water backup (sump pump
  failure / sewer backup endorsement is meaningful in Minnesota),
  service line, equipment breakdown, frozen-pipe and ice-dam coverage
  considerations
- Mortgagee / loss payee information
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Underwriting questions: dogs, pools, business use, woodstoves /
  wood-burning fireplaces, snow load on roof
- Minnesota-required disclosures and acknowledgments, including
  Insurance Guaranty Association notice (see companion 65MN)
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Minnesota homeowners policy
- Renewal where the carrier requires a fresh application
- Adding endorsements or scheduled property
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Hail damage is a major Minnesota homeowners exposure — separate
  wind/hail deductibles (often percentage-of-Coverage-A) are common
- Ice dam and frozen pipe exposure is climate-driven; underwriting
  often asks about roof insulation and heat-tape installation
- Sump pump / sewer backup endorsement is a frequent add-on
