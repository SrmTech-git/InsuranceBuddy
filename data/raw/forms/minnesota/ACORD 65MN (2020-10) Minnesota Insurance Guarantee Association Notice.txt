ACORD 65MN (2020-10) — Minnesota Insurance Guarantee Association Notice
========================================================================
Form number: ACORD 65MN
Edition: 2020-10
Title: Minnesota Insurance Guarantee Association Notice
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: Matches the underlying policy; notice delivered at issuance and renewal
Transaction types: New business (notice required); renewal

Purpose:
Minnesota-required notice informing policyholders about the Minnesota
Insurance Guaranty Association (MIGA) — the state's insurance
guaranty fund that pays covered claims if a licensed property/casualty
insurer becomes insolvent. Every state has a similar guaranty
association; this form is the Minnesota-specific disclosure.

Captures:
- Statutory notice describing MIGA's purpose and what it covers
- Coverage limits (typically a per-claim cap and per-policy aggregate;
  precise amounts set by Minnesota statute)
- Disclosure of what MIGA does NOT cover (e.g., reinsurance, surplus-
  lines, certain large commercial policies above caps, life/health
  policies — those have a separate guaranty association)
- Statement that the existence of MIGA may not be used by carriers
  for marketing purposes (standard guaranty notice rule)
- Insured acknowledgment of receipt
- Optional signature

When used:
- New Minnesota property/casualty policy issuance — notice delivered
- Renewal — many carriers deliver the notice annually
- Required by Minn. Stat. § 60C.20 (or similar) at policy issuance

Notes:
- MIGA = Minnesota Insurance Guaranty Association — Minn. Stat. Ch. 60C
- Pays claims of insolvent Minnesota P&C insurers up to statutory caps
- Funded by assessments on solvent member insurers
- This is purely an informational notice — not a coverage form or
  election
- Most personal lines and commercial submissions in Minnesota include
  this notice in the application package
