ACORD 83MN (2025-07) — Minnesota Personal Umbrella Application
===============================================================
Form number: ACORD 83MN
Edition: 2025-07
Title: Minnesota Personal Umbrella Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for personal umbrella)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, increasing limits, midterm change to underlying policies

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal umbrella liability policy in
Minnesota. Captures the applicant, household members, underlying
policies (auto, HO, watercraft, etc.) and their limits, and the
umbrella limit selected. The umbrella provides excess liability
coverage above underlying policy limits plus broader coverage for
certain claims.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Household members: spouse, resident relatives, licensed drivers
- Underlying auto policy: carrier, policy number, BI/PD limits,
  UM/UIM limits — Minnesota's no-fault PIP also feeds into the
  underlying coverage picture
- Underlying homeowners policy: carrier, policy number, personal
  liability and medical payments limits
- Underlying watercraft policy: carrier, policy number, BI/PD limits
  (significant in Minnesota given the recreational-boating exposure)
- Other underlying policies (rental properties, recreational vehicles,
  ATVs, snowmobiles — common in Minnesota)
- Umbrella limit selected (typically $1M, $2M, $5M, $10M)
- Drivers and licensed members of household
- Vehicles, properties, watercraft, recreational vehicles owned
- Loss history (typically 5 years)
- Underwriting questions: prior umbrella, prior umbrella claims,
  professional/occupational exposures, youthful drivers, business use
- Minnesota-required disclosures, including Insurance Guaranty
  Association notice
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New Minnesota personal umbrella submission
- Renewal where the umbrella limit is being modified
- Adding/removing underlying policies
- Reporting significant household or exposure changes

Notes:
- Personal umbrella requires underlying liability minimums to be met
  (carrier-specific — typically 250/500/100 auto, $300K HO liability)
- Coverage broader than underlying: defense costs in addition,
  personal injury coverage, worldwide
- Minnesota recreational exposures (boats, snowmobiles, ATVs) add
  meaningful underlying-policy considerations for the umbrella
  application
- Companion forms: 88MN-equivalent (no 88MN in our catalog — likely
  uses national 88), 283MN (Personal Umbrella Application Section
