ACORD 82MN (2025-07) — Minnesota Watercraft Application
========================================================
Form number: ACORD 82MN
Edition: 2025-07
Title: Minnesota Watercraft Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for watercraft policy; some carriers offer seasonal terms)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, adding/removing watercraft, midterm change

Purpose:
Standalone application for a Minnesota personal watercraft policy.
Captures applicant, watercraft, operators, coverages, and Minnesota-
required disclosures (including the boat BI decline notice — see
companion 67MN). Minnesota is a meaningful watercraft state — 10,000+
lakes and substantial recreational boating activity drive a large
watercraft market.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Watercraft schedule, each with:
  * Type: outboard, inboard/outboard, inboard, jet ski/PWC, sailboat,
    pontoon, houseboat, canoe/kayak (where insured)
  * Make, model, year, length, beam, HIN (Hull Identification Number)
  * Engine: make, model, horsepower, propulsion type (outboard,
    inboard, jet drive, sail)
  * Hull material, insured value
- Operators: name, DOB, boating experience, safety course completion
- Use: pleasure, water-skiing, tubing, fishing, racing (typically
  excluded or extra-rated)
- Storage: in-water (slip/mooring), on-water-lift, on-trailer,
  storage facility; seasonal vs year-round
- Coverage selected:
  * Hull (physical damage)
  * Liability (BI/PD) — see companion 67MN for BI decline option
  * Medical payments
  * Uninsured boater
  * Personal property on board
  * Fuel spill / oil pollution
  * Towing / on-water assistance
  * Trailer coverage (if separately insured)
- Limits, deductibles
- Lienholder information (if financed)
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history on watercraft
- Minnesota-required disclosures, including Insurance Guaranty
  Association notice
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New Minnesota personal watercraft policy submission
- Renewal where coverage is being modified
- Adding a new watercraft to the schedule
- Adding a new operator

Notes:
- Minnesota's lake culture makes watercraft a meaningful personal
  lines exposure — boats, pontoons, jet skis, and ice fishing
  shelters (some carriers cover) are common
- Boat BI coverage is typically offered; the insured may decline BI
  in writing — see companion form 67MN (Personal Boat Insurance
  Notice Election to Decline BI Coverage)
- Section version (282MN) drops applicant identity in favor of
  ACORD 88
- Off-season storage typically reduces premium; on-water year-round
  storage (rare in MN climate) typically increases premium
