ACORD 828MN (2016-03) — Minnesota Fiduciary Liability Coverage Section
=======================================================================
Form number: ACORD 828MN
Edition: 2016-03
Title: Minnesota Fiduciary Liability Coverage Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for Fiduciary — claims-made)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change (plan changes, M&A affecting plan structure)

Purpose:
Minnesota-specific section for Fiduciary Liability coverage — covers
ERISA-related liability for sponsors and administrators of employee
benefit plans (retirement, health, welfare). ERISA imposes strict
fiduciary duties on plan sponsors and administrators; this coverage
responds to claims alleging breach of those duties. Used alongside
ACORD 125.

Captures:
- Plan sponsor / employer information
- Plans covered:
  * Retirement plans: 401(k), pension, profit-sharing, ESOP, 403(b)
  * Health and welfare plans
  * Other benefit plans
- Plan financial information: total plan assets, participant count,
  number of plans
- Plan administrator and trustee information
- Investment manager / fiduciary information
- Prior fiduciary coverage and claims
- Underwriting questions:
  * Prior DOL or IRS investigations / audits
  * Pending or threatened benefit-related claims
  * Recent plan amendments or restructurings
  * Recent M&A activity affecting benefit plans
  * ESOP-specific exposure (heightened scrutiny)
  * Stock-drop / pension underfunding considerations (if pension plan)
- Coverage selections:
  * Limits and retention
  * Voluntary settlement program (e.g., DOL Voluntary Fiduciary
    Correction Program, VFCP) — common Fiduciary endorsement
  * Settlor capacity coverage
  * Defense outside or inside limits
- Minnesota-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Minnesota Fiduciary submission — section attached to ACORD 125
- Renewal where Fiduciary coverage is being modified
- Reporting plan changes, plan acquisitions/divestitures

Notes:
- Fiduciary is claims-made — retro date matters
- ERISA Section 404 fiduciary duties: loyalty, prudence, diversification,
  following plan documents — claims commonly allege breach of any
- ESOP exposures are particularly scrutinized — heightened DOL focus
- Companion forms: 807MN (D&O), 827MN (EPLI), 832MN (Misc E&O); often
  packaged together as management liability program
