ACORD 38MN (2021-08) — Minnesota Authorization
===============================================
Form number: ACORD 38MN
Edition: 2021-08
Title: Minnesota Authorization
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: Authorization typically persists for the period needed to complete underwriting and beyond as permitted by statute
Transaction types: New business (authorization required for consumer reports), renewal where re-authorization is needed

Purpose:
Minnesota-required authorization form by which the insurance applicant
grants the carrier permission to obtain consumer reports, credit
information, motor vehicle records (MVR), claims history (CLUE), and
other underwriting information from third-party sources. The
authorization satisfies federal Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
requirements as well as Minnesota-specific privacy law requirements.

Captures:
- Applicant identification (name, address, DOB, SSN where required)
- Categories of information the applicant authorizes the carrier to
  obtain:
  * Consumer reports (credit-based insurance scores)
  * Motor Vehicle Records (MVR) from MN DPS-DVS
  * CLUE (Comprehensive Loss Underwriting Exchange) claims history
  * A-PLUS or similar claims database history
  * Prior carrier loss runs (commercial submissions)
  * Medical information (with separate HIPAA-compliant authorization
    where applicable)
- Statement of FCRA rights — applicant's right to obtain a free copy
  of any consumer report used in an adverse decision
- Duration of the authorization (typically the policy term plus an
  underwriting tail)
- Applicant signature and date

When used:
- New Minnesota insurance submission — authorization captured with
  the application package
- Renewal where re-authorization is needed (carrier-specific)
- Reissuance after lapse / reinstatement
- Anytime the carrier needs to obtain a fresh consumer report

Notes:
- Title is generically "Minnesota Authorization" — based on the form
  context (ACORD 38 series across states being privacy / authorization
  notices) and typical usage, this captures consumer-report
  authorization
