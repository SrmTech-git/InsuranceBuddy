ACORD 90MN (2019-12) — Minnesota Personal Auto Application
===========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90MN
Edition: 2019-12
Title: Minnesota Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Minnesota. Captures
the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and the no-fault PIP / UM /
UIM elections required under Minnesota's No-Fault Automobile Insurance
Act (Minn. Stat. § 65B).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, mandatory PIP (basic economic loss benefits),
  UM, UIM, comp, coll, optional coverages
- PIP basic economic loss benefit selection (medical + non-medical)
- UM/UIM elections (typically mandatory at limits equal to liability
  unless rejected)
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Discounts claimed
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Minnesota-required disclosures, including Insurance Guaranty
  Association notice
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Minnesota personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Minnesota policy
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Minnesota minimum liability limits: 30/60/10 (Minn. Stat. § 65B).
  Note PD minimum of $10K is one of the LOWEST in the U.S.
- Minnesota is a no-fault state — mandatory PIP at $40,000 total per
  person: $20,000 medical + $20,000 non-medical (lost wages up to
  $500/wk, replacement services, funeral up to $2,000) under
  Minn. Stat. § 65B.44. PIP cannot be rejected.
- Minnesota UM is REQUIRED at minimum 25/50 (cannot be waived); UIM
  REQUIRED at minimum 25/50 unless rejected in writing (Minn. Stat.
  § 65B.49 subd. 3a). UIM operates on REDUCED-BY (DIL) basis — UIM
  pays the difference between at-fault driver's BI limits and insured's
  UIM limits, not additive on top.
- Primary statutes: Minn. Stat. Ch. 65B (Auto Ins / No-Fault),
  § 72A.201 (Unfair Claims Settlement Practices), Ch. 169 (Traffic)
- State insurance regulator: Minnesota Department of Commerce (not
  "Department of Insurance" — MN's regulator is part of Commerce)
- Companion forms commonly used: 38MN (Authorization), 62MN (Auto
  Supplement), 65MN (Insurance Guarantee Association Notice)
- Section version (290MN) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
