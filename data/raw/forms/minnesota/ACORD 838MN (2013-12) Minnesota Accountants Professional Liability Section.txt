ACORD 838MN (2013-12) — Minnesota Accountants Professional Liability Section
=============================================================================
Form number: ACORD 838MN
Edition: 2013-12
Title: Minnesota Accountants Professional Liability Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Minnesota state variant)
States: MN
Policy term: 12 months (typical for Accountants PL — claims-made)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change (partner additions, service mix changes)

Purpose:
Minnesota-specific section for Accountants Professional Liability
(APL) coverage — the E&O policy for CPAs, accounting firms, and tax
practitioners. Covers claims arising from professional accounting
services: audit failures, tax errors, advisory missteps, etc. Used
alongside ACORD 125.

Captures:
- Firm information: name, address, year established, structure
  (sole practitioner, partnership, LLP, PC, PA)
- CPA licensing by state
- Number of accountants by class: partner, manager, senior, staff,
  contract
- Service revenue breakdown by service line:
  * Audit / attestation (typically highest exposure)
  * Review and compilation
  * Tax preparation and planning
  * Advisory / consulting
  * Bookkeeping
  * Forensic / litigation support
- Highest-risk service areas:
  * Audits of SEC registrants (heightened PCAOB and SEC scrutiny)
  * Audits of broker-dealers or investment advisers
  * Audits of employee benefit plans (ERISA exposure)
  * Tax shelter / aggressive tax position work
  * Forensic / litigation support
- Annual gross fees and average matter value
- Largest single audit / engagement
- Prior APL coverage and claims (typically 5-10 years)
- Underwriting questions:
  * Prior PCAOB inspections (if applicable) and findings
  * Prior state board of accountancy actions
  * Prior peer review results
  * Conflict and independence policies
  * Engagement letter practices
- Coverage selections:
  * Limits (per claim, aggregate)
  * Retention
  * Retro date / prior acts coverage (critical for continuity)
  * Defense outside or inside limits
  * Extended Reporting Period (ERP / "tail") availability
- Minnesota-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Minnesota Accountants PL submission — section attached to
  ACORD 125
- Renewal where APL coverage is being modified
- Reporting significant firm or service-mix changes

Notes:
- Accountants PL is claims-made — retro date and prior acts coverage
  critical for continuity
- Audit work carries the highest exposure; some carriers exclude or
  sub-limit SEC audits
- Companion forms: 807MN (D&O), 832MN (Misc E&O), 833MN (Lawyers PL)
- Minnesota Board of Accountancy regulates Minnesota CPAs; AICPA
  professional standards apply
