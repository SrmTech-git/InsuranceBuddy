ACORD 67OH (2009-07) — Ohio Mine Subsidence Homeowners and Dwelling Supplement
===============================================================================
Form number: ACORD 67OH
Edition: 2009-07
Title: Ohio Mine Subsidence Homeowners and Dwelling Supplement
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: Matches the underlying property policy (typically 12 months); election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (mine subsidence supplement); change in coverage; election change at renewal

Purpose:
Ohio-specific supplement to a homeowners or dwelling fire policy
adding Mine Subsidence Insurance coverage for property damage caused
by collapse of underground mines. Ohio's mine subsidence program
covers historical coal-mining counties in southeastern, eastern, and
parts of central Ohio where underground mining occurred.

Captures:
- Property location and county (Ohio mine subsidence eligibility is
  county-specific based on historical mining activity)
- Property type (owner-occupied dwelling, dwelling fire / rental)
- Coverage details: coverage amount, deductible
- Insured election to accept mine subsidence coverage
- Statutorily required disclosures
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Ohio HO or dwelling fire policy in an eligible mining county
- Renewal where the mine subsidence coverage is being added/modified
- Reporting property changes
