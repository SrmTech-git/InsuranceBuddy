ACORD 80OH (2026-03) — Ohio Homeowner Application
==================================================
Form number: ACORD 80OH
Edition: 2026-03
Title: Ohio Homeowner Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: 12 months (typical for homeowners)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change

Purpose:
Standalone application for a homeowners policy in Ohio. Captures
applicant, dwelling, coverages, underwriting questions, and Ohio-
required disclosures needed to underwrite and issue an HO policy.
State-specific variant of national ACORD 80.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Property location and address
- Property description: construction type, year built, square footage,
  number of stories, roof type and age, heating, electrical, plumbing,
  foundation type (basement common in Ohio)
- Protection class (PPC)
- Occupancy: primary / secondary / seasonal
- Coverage form: HO-2 / HO-3 (typical) / HO-5 / HO-6 / HO-8
- Coverage A through F at selected limits
- Deductible selected
- Discounts: multi-policy, alarm, smoke detectors, age, claim-free
- Endorsements: scheduled personal property, water backup, sewer/drain,
  service line, equipment breakdown
- Mine subsidence (Ohio coal-mining counties — see companion 67OH
  Mine Subsidence Homeowners and Dwelling Supplement and 68OH Offer
  of Mine Subsidence)
- Mortgagee / loss payee information
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Underwriting questions: dogs, pools, business use, woodstoves
- Ohio-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for an Ohio homeowners policy
- Renewal where the carrier requires a fresh application
- Adding endorsements or scheduled property
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Ohio has a robust mine subsidence program for properties in former
  coal-mining counties — see companion forms 67OH (Mine Subsidence
  Homeowners and Dwelling Supplement) and 68OH (Offer of Mine
  Subsidence Coverage)
- Water/sewer backup is a notable Ohio coverage consideration given
  older municipal infrastructure in many cities
