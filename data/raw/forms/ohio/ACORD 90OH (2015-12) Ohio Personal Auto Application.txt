ACORD 90OH (2015-12) — Ohio Personal Auto Application
======================================================
Form number: ACORD 90OH
Edition: 2015-12
Title: Ohio Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Ohio. Captures the
applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and state-required disclosures
needed to underwrite, bind, and issue an Ohio auto policy under Ohio's
Financial Responsibility Law (ORC 4509) and Auto Insurance statutes
(ORC 3937).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM/UIM, MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages
- Discounts claimed
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Ohio-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for an Ohio personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Ohio policy
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Ohio minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 (ORC § 4509; minimums under
  Ohio's Financial Responsibility Law)
- Ohio UM/UIM is NOT required and has NO mandatory-offer requirement
  since 2001 (ORC § 3937.18, as amended by S.B. 97, eff. Oct. 31, 2001).
  An insurer may issue an auto liability policy that OMITS UM/UIM
  entirely — no written rejection needed. If the insured wants UM/UIM,
  they must affirmatively request it.
- Ohio uses electronic insurance verification — insurers report to
  Ohio BMV electronically
- Primary statutes: ORC Chapters 3937 (auto insurance), 4509 (financial
  responsibility), 3901 (insurance department)
- State insurance regulator: Ohio Department of Insurance (ODI)
- Section version (290OH) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
