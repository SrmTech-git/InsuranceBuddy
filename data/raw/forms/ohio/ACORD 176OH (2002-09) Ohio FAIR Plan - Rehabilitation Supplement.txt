ACORD 176OH (2002-09) — Ohio FAIR Plan – Rehabilitation Supplement
===================================================================
Form number: ACORD 176OH
Edition: 2002-09
Title: Ohio FAIR Plan – Rehabilitation Supplement
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: 12 months (matches the master 171OH FAIR Plan submission)
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Rehabilitation supplement to the Ohio FAIR Plan Basic Property
Application (171OH). Captures information specific to properties
under renovation, rehabilitation, or major construction — including
properties partially occupied or temporarily vacant during the rehab
period. Rehabilitation supplements address the elevated risk profile
during construction (theft, vandalism, fire, builders-risk-like
exposure).

Captures:
- Rehabilitation project scope and timeline
- Project description (gut rehab, partial rehab, addition, etc.)
- Estimated completion date
- General contractor / project lead information
- Security measures during rehab (fencing, lighting, alarms)
- Occupancy status during rehab (vacant / partially occupied)
- Coverage during rehab and any specific endorsements
- Applicant signature and date

When used:
- FAIR Plan submission for a property under rehabilitation — attached
  to 171OH
- Renewal of existing FAIR Plan rehab coverage if the project extends
  beyond the original policy term
- Reporting project changes (scope, contractor, schedule)

Notes:
- Used in combination with the master 171OH (Basic Property Application)
- Rehab properties are higher-risk — vacant or partially-occupied
  structures undergoing construction face elevated theft, vandalism,
  and fire exposure
- FAIR Plan rehab coverage may be transitional — once rehab is
  complete, the property may be eligible for voluntary-market coverage
- Companion supplements: 172OH (Homeowners), 173OH (Dwelling Fire),
  174OH (Commercial), 175OH (Farm), 177OH (Bowling Alley)
