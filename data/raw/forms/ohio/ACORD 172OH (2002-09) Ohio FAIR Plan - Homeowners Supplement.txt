ACORD 172OH (2002-09) — Ohio FAIR Plan – Homeowners Supplement
===============================================================
Form number: ACORD 172OH
Edition: 2002-09
Title: Ohio FAIR Plan – Homeowners Supplement
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: 12 months (matches the master 171OH FAIR Plan submission)
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Homeowners-specific supplement to the Ohio FAIR Plan Basic Property
Application (171OH). Captures information specific to owner-occupied
residential properties that don't fit on the master 171OH — coverage
selection, dwelling-specific exposures, and any homeowners-form
selection (e.g., HO-2, HO-3 limited where available).

Captures:
- Owner-occupied confirmation
- Homeowners coverage form selection (where multiple FAIR Plan
  homeowners forms are offered)
- Dwelling-specific replacement cost / actual cash value selection
- Personal liability coverage (if available through Ohio FAIR Plan)
- Medical payments coverage (if available)
- Special endorsements or limits relevant to homeowners
- Applicant signature and date

When used:
- Owner-occupied residential FAIR Plan submission — attached to 171OH
- Renewal of existing FAIR Plan homeowners coverage
- Reporting changes affecting owner-occupancy or coverage selection

Notes:
- Used in combination with the master 171OH (Basic Property Application)
- Ohio FAIR Plan homeowners coverage is typically more limited than
  voluntary-market HO-3 — fewer perils, lower liability availability
- Companion supplements in the same family: 173OH (Dwelling Fire — for
  non-owner-occupied), 174OH (Commercial), 175OH (Farm), 176OH
  (Rehabilitation), 177OH (Bowling Alley)
