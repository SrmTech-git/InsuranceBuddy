ACORD 62OH (2011-05) — Ohio Auto Supplement – Important Optional Disclosure Notice Uninsured Motorist Property Damage Coverage
===============================================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 62OH
Edition: 2011-05
Title: Ohio Auto Supplement – Important Optional Disclosure Notice Uninsured Motorist Property Damage Coverage
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (notice/election required); change to UMPD coverage

Purpose:
Notice and disclosure form for Ohio's Uninsured Motorist Property
Damage (UMPD) coverage. UMPD pays for damage to the insured's vehicle
caused by an at-fault uninsured driver. Ohio requires carriers to
offer UMPD; this form notifies the insured of availability and
captures election or rejection.

Captures:
- Statutory disclosure describing what UMPD covers
- Disclosure that UMPD is optional and how it differs from collision
- Insured's election to accept UMPD (with selected limits) or reject
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Ohio auto submission — UMPD disclosure delivered, election captured
- Renewal where UMPD election is being modified
- Midterm change to UMPD coverage

Notes:
- UMPD differs from collision: UMPD pays only when the at-fault driver
  is uninsured; collision pays regardless of fault
- Ohio insureds without collision often elect UMPD for at least basic
  PD recovery against uninsured drivers
- Ohio UM bodily injury is NOT required and has NO mandatory-offer
  requirement since 2001 (ORC § 3937.18 as amended by S.B. 97). Insurer
  may omit UM entirely; no written rejection needed. UMPD on this form
  is the SEPARATE property-damage variant of UM coverage.
