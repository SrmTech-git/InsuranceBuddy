ACORD 290OH (2014-12) — Ohio Personal Automobile Application Section
=====================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290OH
Edition: 2014-12
Title: Ohio Personal Automobile Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Ohio. Used in
combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application — Applicant
Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The 290OH
holds the auto-specific data needed to underwrite an Ohio auto policy
under ORC 4509 (financial responsibility) and ORC 3937 (auto insurance
statutes).

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM/UIM, MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Ohio-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity is captured on ACORD 88, not duplicated here)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290OH) rather than the standalone full app (90OH)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Ohio minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 (ORC § 4509)
- Ohio UM/UIM is NOT required and has NO mandatory-offer requirement
  since 2001 (ORC § 3937.18 as amended by S.B. 97). Insurer may omit
  UM/UIM entirely; insured must affirmatively request to add it.
- Ohio uses electronic insurance verification via Ohio BMV
- Primary statutes: ORC Chapters 3937, 4509, 3901
- Use 290OH with ACORD 88; use 90OH standalone
