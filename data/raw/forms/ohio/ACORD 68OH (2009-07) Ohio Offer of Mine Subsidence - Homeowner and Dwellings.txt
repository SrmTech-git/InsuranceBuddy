ACORD 68OH (2009-07) — Ohio Offer of Mine Subsidence – Homeowner and Dwellings
===============================================================================
Form number: ACORD 68OH
Edition: 2009-07
Title: Ohio Offer of Mine Subsidence – Homeowner and Dwellings
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: Matches the underlying property policy; election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (mandatory offer); election change

Purpose:
Ohio-specific offer form for Mine Subsidence Insurance, delivered by
the carrier to property owners in mining-county Ohio to make the
statutorily required offer of coverage. The insured then elects to
accept or reject. Distinct from 67OH (which is the supplement adding
coverage); 68OH is the offer itself.

Captures:
- Statutory disclosure that Mine Subsidence Insurance is being offered
- Description of what mine subsidence coverage covers (ground
  collapse / settling from underground mine workings)
- Description of what is NOT covered (earth movement, earthquake,
  natural settling)
- Coverage limit available (state-pool cap)
- Insured election to accept coverage (which triggers issuance of
  67OH as the active supplement) or reject coverage
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Ohio HO or dwelling fire policy issuance in an eligible mining
  county — the offer is required to be delivered
- Renewal where the mandatory offer must be refreshed
- Reporting changes that affect eligibility

Notes:
- Ohio's mandatory-offer rule means carriers must offer mine subsidence
  to insureds in eligible counties — the insured may accept or reject
- A rejected offer typically must be documented in writing on this form
- Once accepted, the active coverage is captured on companion form 67OH
