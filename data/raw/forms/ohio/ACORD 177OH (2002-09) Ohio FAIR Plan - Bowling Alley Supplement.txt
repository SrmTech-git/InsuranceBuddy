ACORD 177OH (2002-09) — Ohio FAIR Plan – Bowling Alley Supplement
==================================================================
Form number: ACORD 177OH
Edition: 2002-09
Title: Ohio FAIR Plan – Bowling Alley Supplement
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Ohio state variant)
States: OH
Policy term: 12 months (matches the master 171OH FAIR Plan submission)
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Specialty supplement to the Ohio FAIR Plan Basic Property Application
(171OH) for bowling alley properties. Bowling alleys are a recognized
hard-to-place property class in commercial markets — combination of
large open-floor structure, specialized equipment, lane construction,
food/beverage operations, and entertainment-venue exposure leads to
voluntary-market denials. This supplement captures bowling-alley-
specific underwriting info.

Captures:
- Number of lanes and lane construction type
- Year of last major renovation
- Building floor area and ceiling height
- Pinsetter equipment description and value
- Lane equipment (ball returns, scoring system) description and value
- Food and beverage operations on premises (snack bar, full bar, etc.)
- Other amenities (arcade, billiards, party rooms)
- Property values for each component
- Applicant signature and date

When used:
- Ohio FAIR Plan submission for a bowling alley — attached to 171OH
- Renewal of existing FAIR Plan bowling alley coverage
- Reporting bowling alley operational or property changes

Notes:
- Used in combination with the master 171OH (Basic Property Application)
- Bowling alleys typically end up in residual markets due to
  property age, large floor plates, and specialized equipment
  exposure; voluntary-market commercial property carriers often
  decline these risks
- Coverage typically named-perils on building + business personal
  property; lane equipment may be scheduled separately
- General Liability is NOT included — separate GL needed for
  slip-and-fall and entertainment-venue exposures
- Companion supplements: 172OH (Homeowners), 173OH (Dwelling Fire),
  174OH (Commercial), 175OH (Farm), 176OH (Rehabilitation)
- The existence of a dedicated bowling-alley supplement in the Ohio
  FAIR Plan family reflects how reliably bowling alleys land in the
  residual market
