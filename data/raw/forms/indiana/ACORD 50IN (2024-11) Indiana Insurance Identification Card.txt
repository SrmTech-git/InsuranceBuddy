ACORD 50IN (2024-11) — Indiana Insurance Identification Card
=============================================================
Form number: ACORD 50IN
Edition: 2024-11
Title: Indiana Insurance Identification Card
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Indiana state variant)
States: IN
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); reissued at each renewal
Transaction types: N/A (proof-of-insurance card, not a transaction document)

Purpose:
Proof of mandatory liability insurance issued under an Indiana personal
auto policy. Carried in the vehicle to satisfy Indiana's Financial
Responsibility Law (IC 9-25), which requires drivers to maintain minimum
liability coverage and provide proof on demand.

Captures:
- Insured's name and address
- Policy number
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Year, make, model, and VIN of the insured vehicle
- Insurer name and NAIC code
- Agent / agency name and contact (if applicable)

When used:
- Carried in the vehicle as proof of insurance
- Presented to law enforcement during a traffic stop or accident investigation
- Presented to the Indiana BMV for vehicle registration / renewal
- Provided after an at-fault accident to satisfy financial responsibility verification

Notes:
- Indiana operates an electronic insurance verification system — carriers
  report active policy status to the state; the ID card supplements the
  electronic record
- Indiana minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 (IC § 9-25)
- Indiana UM/UIM: insurer must OFFER (UM under IC 27-7-6; UIM BI at $50K
  minimum under IC 27-7-5-2); insured may reject in writing. UIM defaults
  to insured's BI liability limits unless rejected.
- State insurance regulator: Indiana Department of Insurance (IDOI)
- Electronic verification: Certificate of Compliance filed electronically
  with Indiana BMV
