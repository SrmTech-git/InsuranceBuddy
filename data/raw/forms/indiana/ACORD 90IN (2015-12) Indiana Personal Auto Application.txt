ACORD 90IN (2015-12) — Indiana Personal Auto Application
=========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90IN
Edition: 2015-12
Title: Indiana Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Indiana state variant)
States: IN
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Indiana. Captures
the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and state-required
disclosures needed to underwrite, bind, and issue an Indiana auto
policy under Indiana's Financial Responsibility Law (IC 9-25).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation, relationship to applicant
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address, ownership
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business / farm, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages selected: BI, PD, UM/UIM, MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages: rental reimbursement, towing, etc.
- Discounts claimed: multi-policy, good student, defensive driver, etc.
- Prior insurance carrier, prior limits, lapse history
- Loss history (typically 3-5 years)
- Indiana-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for an Indiana personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Indiana policy
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Indiana minimum liability limits: 25/50/25 (IC § 9-25; Indiana
  Financial Responsibility Law)
- Indiana UM/UIM: insurer must OFFER; insured may reject in writing.
  UM authority: IC § 27-7-6. UIM BI floor: $50K minimum (IC § 27-7-5-2);
  UIM defaults to insured's BI liability limits unless rejected.
- Primary statutes: IC Title 27 (Insurance Code), IC Title 9 (Motor Vehicles)
- State insurance regulator: Indiana Department of Insurance (IDOI)
- Indiana operates an electronic insurance verification system —
  Certificate of Compliance filed electronically with Indiana BMV
- Section version (290IN) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
  (Applicant Information Section) for agencies using ACORD's modular system
