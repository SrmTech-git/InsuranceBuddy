ACORD 290IL (2014-12) — Illinois Personal Auto Application Section
===================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290IL
Edition: 2014-12
Title: Illinois Personal Auto Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Illinois. Used
in combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application —
Applicant Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The
290IL holds the auto-specific data: drivers, vehicles, coverages,
history, and Illinois-required disclosures under 625 ILCS 5/7-601.

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  relationship to applicant, 3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address, ownership
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business / farm, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages selected: BI, PD, UM/UIM, MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history (3-5 years)
- Illinois-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity — name, address, DOB, occupation, contact —
is captured on ACORD 88 instead, not duplicated on this section)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290IL) rather than the standalone full app (90IL)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Illinois requires UM in limits equal to BI; UIM rejection/reduction
  must be in writing
- Illinois Secretary of State conducts random insurance verification
- Use 290IL with ACORD 88; use 90IL standalone
- Illinois minimum liability limits: 25/50/20 (625 ILCS 5/7-203)
- Illinois UM REQUIRED (625 ILCS 5/7-601); UIM offered (215 ILCS 5/143a)
- Primary statutes: 215 ILCS 5, 625 ILCS 5
- State regulator: Illinois Department of Insurance (IDOI)
