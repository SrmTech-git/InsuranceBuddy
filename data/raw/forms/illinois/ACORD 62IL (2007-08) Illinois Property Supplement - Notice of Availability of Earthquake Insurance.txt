ACORD 62IL (2007-08) — Illinois Property Supplement – Notice of Availability of Earthquake Insurance
=====================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 62IL
Edition: 2007-08
Title: Illinois Property Supplement – Notice of Availability of Earthquake Insurance
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: Matches the underlying property policy (typically 12 months); notice delivered at issuance and renewal
Transaction types: New business (notice required); renewal

Purpose:
Illinois-required notice informing property insureds that earthquake
coverage is NOT included in the standard homeowners / dwelling policy
and is available as a separate endorsement. Illinois is particularly
exposed to earthquake risk due to the New Madrid Seismic Zone in
southern Illinois — historically one of the most seismically active
zones in the central U.S.

Captures:
- Statutory notice that standard property coverage excludes earthquake
- Description of earthquake coverage availability as an endorsement
- Typical deductible structure (often a percentage of Coverage A —
  e.g., 2%, 5%, 10% of dwelling limit — rather than a flat dollar
  deductible)
- Information about the New Madrid Seismic Zone exposure
- Insured acknowledgment of receipt of the notice
- Insured signature

When used:
- New Illinois property submission — notice delivered at issuance
- Renewal — many carriers deliver the notice annually
- Whenever required by statute to refresh the disclosure

Notes:
- The New Madrid Seismic Zone is the major earthquake exposure for
  central U.S. states — runs through Missouri, Tennessee, Arkansas,
  Kentucky, and southern Illinois
- Earthquake coverage typically has a percentage deductible (large
  for severe events) rather than the flat deductible of HO policies
- Pure informational notice — the actual coverage is added by
  earthquake endorsement, not on this form
