ACORD 50IL (2007-03) — Illinois Insurance Identification Card
==============================================================
Form number: ACORD 50IL
Edition: 2007-03
Title: Illinois Insurance Identification Card
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); reissued at each renewal
Transaction types: N/A (proof-of-insurance card, not a transaction document)

Purpose:
Proof of mandatory liability insurance issued under an Illinois personal
auto policy. Carried in the vehicle to satisfy Illinois' compulsory
insurance law (625 ILCS 5/7-601 et seq.), which requires drivers to
maintain minimum liability coverage and produce evidence of insurance
on demand.

Captures:
- Insured's name and address
- Policy number
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Year, make, model, and VIN of the insured vehicle
- Insurer name and NAIC code
- Agent / agency name and contact (if applicable)

When used:
- Carried in the vehicle as proof of insurance
- Presented to law enforcement during a traffic stop or accident investigation
- Presented to the Illinois Secretary of State for vehicle registration / renewal
- Provided in response to random insurance verification requests issued by the SoS

Notes:
- One card issued per vehicle on the policy; new cards issued at each renewal
- Illinois uses random insurance verification — the Secretary of State
  periodically mails verification requests; failure to respond can result
  in registration suspension
- Illinois minimum liability limits: 25/50/20 (625 ILCS 5/7-203)
- Illinois UM is REQUIRED — cannot be waived (625 ILCS 5/7-601)
- Primary statutes: 215 ILCS 5 (Insurance Code), 625 ILCS 5 (Vehicle Code)
- State insurance regulator: Illinois Department of Insurance (IDOI)
