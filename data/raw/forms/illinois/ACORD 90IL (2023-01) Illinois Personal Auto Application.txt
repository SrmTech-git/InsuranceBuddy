ACORD 90IL (2023-01) — Illinois Personal Auto Application
==========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90IL
Edition: 2023-01
Title: Illinois Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Illinois. Captures
the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and state-required
disclosures needed to underwrite, bind, and issue an Illinois auto
policy under the state's compulsory insurance law (625 ILCS 5/7-601).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation, relationship to applicant
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address, ownership
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute (miles each way) / business / farm, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages selected: BI, PD, UM/UIM, MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages: rental reimbursement, towing, custom equipment, etc.
- Discounts claimed: multi-policy, good student, defensive driver, etc.
- Prior insurance carrier, prior limits, lapse history
- Loss history (typically 3-5 years)
- State-required Illinois disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for an Illinois personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required by the carrier)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Illinois policy
- Significant midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Illinois requires UM coverage in limits equal to BI limits; UIM rejection
  or reduction below BI limits must be in writing
- Illinois Secretary of State conducts random insurance verification —
  lapsed coverage can result in registration suspension
- Standalone version (90IL) includes applicant info; the section version
  (290IL) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88 (Applicant
  Information Section) for agencies using the modular ACORD system
- Illinois minimum liability limits: 25/50/20 (625 ILCS 5/7-203)
- Illinois UM is REQUIRED — cannot be waived (625 ILCS 5/7-601)
- UIM not required at the minimum level but insurer must OFFER it
  (215 ILCS 5/143a); reduction below BI liability limits must be in writing
- PIP not part of Illinois framework (at-fault tort state)
- Primary statutes: 215 ILCS 5 (Insurance Code), 625 ILCS 5 (Vehicle Code)
- State insurance regulator: Illinois Department of Insurance (IDOI)
