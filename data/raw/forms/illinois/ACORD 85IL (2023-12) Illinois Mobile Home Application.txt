ACORD 85IL (2023-12) — Illinois Mobile Home Application
========================================================
Form number: ACORD 85IL
Edition: 2023-12
Title: Illinois Mobile Home Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: 12 months (typical for mobile home insurance)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change

Purpose:
Standalone application for a mobile home (manufactured home) policy
in Illinois. Captures applicant, mobile home unit, coverages,
underwriting questions, and Illinois-required disclosures.
State-specific variant of national ACORD 85.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Mobile home location and address (park name if in mobile home park)
- Mobile home description: make, model, year, VIN / serial number,
  dimensions (single-wide / double-wide / triple-wide), square footage
- Anchoring / tie-down details (statutorily required in some states;
  affects underwriting)
- Setup details: foundation type (slab / piers / pad), skirting
- Park or owned-lot information (rental lot vs owned land)
- Coverage form: ACORD/HO mobile home equivalent (HO-7 / MH-style)
- Coverage A (Mobile Home — Dwelling), B (Other Structures), C
  (Personal Property), D (Loss of Use), E (Liability), F (Medical
  Payments)
- Deductible selected
- Replacement cost vs actual cash value selection (often ACV for
  older units)
- Discounts: multi-policy, alarm, age, claim-free
- Endorsements: trip / transit coverage (if mobile home is moved),
  scheduled personal property, water backup
- Mortgagee / lienholder information
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Underwriting questions: dogs, age of mobile home (some carriers
  decline pre-HUD-code units pre-1976)
- Illinois-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for an Illinois mobile home policy
- Renewal where the carrier requires a fresh application
- Adding endorsements (trip coverage for relocation, etc.)
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Pre-1976 mobile homes (manufactured before HUD code took effect)
  are often declined or rated higher due to construction quality
- Mobile homes in flood zones are particularly exposed — flood
  endorsement or NFIP coverage often essential
- Wind exposure is heightened compared to site-built homes
