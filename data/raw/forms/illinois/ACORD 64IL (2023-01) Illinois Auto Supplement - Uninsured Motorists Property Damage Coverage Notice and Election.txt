ACORD 64IL (2023-01) — Illinois Auto Supplement: Uninsured Motorists Property Damage Coverage Notice and Election
==================================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 64IL
Edition: 2023-01
Title: Illinois Auto Supplement: Uninsured Motorists Property Damage Coverage Notice and Election
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (notice/election required); change to UMPD coverage

Purpose:
Notice and election form for Uninsured Motorists Property Damage
(UMPD) coverage in Illinois. UMPD covers the insured's own vehicle
property damage caused by an at-fault uninsured driver. This form
notifies the insured that UMPD is available and captures the insured's
election or rejection.

Captures:
- Statutory notice describing what UMPD coverage is
- Insured election of UMPD (with selected limits) or rejection
- Insured acknowledgment and signature

When used:
- New Illinois auto submission — UMPD election or rejection at issuance
- Renewal where UMPD is being added/removed
- Midterm change to UMPD

Notes:
- UMPD differs from collision coverage: UMPD pays only when at-fault
  driver is uninsured; collision pays regardless of fault
- UMPD is typically a low-cost coverage and frequently elected by
  insureds who do NOT carry collision (so they have some PD protection
  if struck by an uninsured driver)
- Companion form: 61IL — UM/UIM Auto Supplement
