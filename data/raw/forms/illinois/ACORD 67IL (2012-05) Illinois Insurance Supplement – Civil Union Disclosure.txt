ACORD 67IL (2012-05) — Illinois Insurance Supplement – Civil Union Disclosure
==============================================================================
Form number: ACORD 67IL
Edition: 2012-05
Title: Illinois Insurance Supplement – Civil Union Disclosure
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: Notice delivered at issuance and at periodic renewals; not tied to a specific policy term
Transaction types: New business (notice required), renewal (re-disclosure where required)

Purpose:
Illinois-required disclosure form addressing how civil union partners
are treated under insurance policies in Illinois. Following the
Illinois Religious Freedom Protection and Civil Union Act (effective
June 1, 2011), civil union partners must be afforded the same
insurance treatment as spouses under Illinois law. This form
discloses that treatment and captures insured acknowledgment.

Captures:
- Statutory description of how civil union partners are treated
  under the insurance policies issued in Illinois
- Statement that civil union partners are treated as spouses for
  coverage purposes under Illinois insurance policies (subject to
  policy terms and Illinois statute)
- Examples of coverages affected: spouse coverage on auto, HO,
  health, life, disability
- Insured acknowledgment of receipt of the disclosure
- Insured signature and date (where applicable)

When used:
- New Illinois personal lines or commercial submission with potential
  spousal coverage implications
- Renewal (where re-disclosure is required)
- Anytime a civil union relationship is being added or removed

Notes:
- Illinois Religious Freedom Protection and Civil Union Act (750 ILCS
  75) — effective June 1, 2011 — established civil union recognition
- Civil unions in Illinois afford the same rights, benefits, and
  protections as marriage under state law (subject to federal limits)
- After the Obergefell v. Hodges Supreme Court decision (2015) and
  Illinois same-sex marriage law (2014), civil union became one of
  several legal recognition options — couples may also marry. The
  Civil Union Disclosure remains relevant for civil-union couples
  not married.
- Pure informational disclosure — no coverage election occurs
