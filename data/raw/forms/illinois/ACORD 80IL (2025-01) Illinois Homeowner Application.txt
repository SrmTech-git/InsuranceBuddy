ACORD 80IL (2025-01) — Illinois Homeowner Application
======================================================
Form number: ACORD 80IL
Edition: 2025-01
Title: Illinois Homeowner Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: 12 months (typical for homeowners)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change (coverage modification, mortgagee change, scheduled item additions)

Purpose:
Standalone application for a homeowners policy in Illinois. Captures
applicant, dwelling, coverages, underwriting questions, and Illinois-
required disclosures needed to underwrite and issue an HO policy in
the voluntary market. State-specific variant of national ACORD 80.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Property location and address (insured location)
- Property description: construction type, year built, square footage,
  number of stories, roof type and age, heating type and age, electrical
  type and age, plumbing type and age, foundation type
- Protection class (PPC) — distance to fire hydrant and responding station
- Occupancy: primary / secondary / seasonal
- Coverage form: HO-2 (broad), HO-3 (special — typical), HO-5
  (comprehensive), HO-6 (condo), HO-8 (older homes)
- Coverage A (Dwelling), B (Other Structures — typically 10% of A),
  C (Personal Property — typically 50% of A), D (Loss of Use),
  E (Personal Liability), F (Medical Payments to Others)
- Deductible selected (all perils and any peril-specific)
- Discounts: multi-policy, monitored alarm, central station,
  fire/smoke alarms, deadbolts, gated community, new home, age 55+,
  claim-free
- Endorsements: scheduled personal property, water backup, identity
  theft, service line, equipment breakdown
- Mortgagee / loss payee information
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history (typically 5 years)
- Underwriting questions: dog breed/bite history, pools/trampolines,
  business use of premises, daycare operations, woodstoves/space heaters
- Illinois-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for an Illinois homeowners policy
- Renewal where the carrier requires a fresh application (uncommon)
- Adding endorsements or scheduled property to an existing policy
- Significant midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Illinois homeowners is a standard market — no unusual state
  mechanics beyond typical statutory disclosures
- Wind/hail is a covered peril without separate deductible (unlike
  some coastal states)
