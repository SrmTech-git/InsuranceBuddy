ACORD 171IL (2005-06) — Illinois FAIR Plan Application for Commercial Property Insurance
=========================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 171IL
Edition: 2005-06
Title: Illinois FAIR Plan Application for Commercial Property Insurance
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Illinois state variant)
States: IL
Policy term: 12 months (typical for FAIR Plan commercial property)
Transaction types: New business (FAIR Plan submission); renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Application for commercial property coverage through the Illinois
FAIR Plan — Illinois' state-mandated residual property insurance
market administered by the Illinois FAIR Plan Association. For
commercial properties that cannot obtain coverage in the voluntary
market due to location, condition, occupancy, or loss history.

Captures:
- Applicant / business information (named insured, business type, FEIN)
- Property location and address
- Building description: construction type, year built, square footage,
  occupancy class, number of stories, roof type, protection class
- Tenant information (multi-tenant buildings)
- Coverage selected: building, business personal property, business
  income (if available)
- Coverage amounts and deductibles
- Mortgagee / loss payee information
- Prior insurance carrier(s), denial dates, and reasons for denial
- Loss history (typically 5 years)
- Illinois FAIR Plan statutory disclosures and acknowledgments
- Applicant signature and date

When used:
- Submission to the Illinois FAIR Plan for commercial property after
  voluntary-market denial
- Renewal of existing FAIR Plan commercial property policy
- Reporting commercial property or occupancy changes

Notes:
- Illinois FAIR Plan Association administers the plan
- Coverage typically named-perils only — fire, extended coverage, VMM
- General Liability is NOT included — separate GL needed
- Commercial FAIR Plan is more limited and more expensive than
  voluntary-market commercial property
