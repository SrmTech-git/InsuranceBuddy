ACORD 64VA (2005-01) — Virginia Property Supplement – Notice of Flood Insurance
================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 64VA
Edition: 2005-01
Title: Virginia Property Supplement – Notice of Flood Insurance
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Virginia state variant)
States: VA
Policy term: Matches the underlying property policy; notice delivered at issuance and renewal
Transaction types: New business (notice required); renewal

Purpose:
Virginia-required notice informing property insureds that flood
coverage is NOT included in standard homeowners / dwelling policies
and is available through the federal National Flood Insurance Program
(NFIP) or through private flood insurance markets. Virginia has
substantial flood exposure — coastal storm surge (Hampton Roads,
Eastern Shore), riverine flooding, and increasingly significant
inland flooding events.

Captures:
- Statutory notice that the standard policy excludes flood
- Definition of "flood" (typically tied to NFIP definition: surface
  water, overflow of inland or tidal waters, mudflow, etc.)
- Information about NFIP coverage availability
- Information about private flood insurance market alternatives
- Flood zone determination (FEMA-mapped — high-risk zones require
  flood insurance for federally-backed mortgages)
- Insured acknowledgment of receipt of the notice
- Insured signature

When used:
- New Virginia property submission — notice delivered at issuance
- Renewal — many carriers deliver the notice annually
- Anytime a property's flood zone determination changes (e.g., new
  FEMA map)

Notes:
- The National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) is the primary federal
  flood insurance program; coverage caps at $250K dwelling /
  $100K contents for residential (higher for commercial)
- Private flood insurance offers higher limits and broader coverage
  than NFIP — an increasingly viable market option
- Properties in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Areas (SFHA — "high risk"
  zones, typically Zone A or V) with federally-backed mortgages are
  REQUIRED to carry flood insurance — the notice helps the agent
  document compliance
