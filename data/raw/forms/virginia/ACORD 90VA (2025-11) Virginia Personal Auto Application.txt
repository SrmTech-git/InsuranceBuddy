ACORD 90VA (2025-11) — Virginia Personal Auto Application
==========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90VA
Edition: 2025-11
Title: Virginia Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Virginia state variant)
States: VA
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Virginia. Captures
the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and the Virginia-specific
UM elections needed to underwrite, bind, and issue a Virginia auto
policy.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM/UIM (and Virginia-specific UM elections),
  MedPay, comp, coll, optional medical and disability coverage
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages: rental reimbursement, towing, etc.
- Discounts claimed
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Virginia-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Virginia personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Virginia policy
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Virginia minimum liability limits: 50/100/25 — INCREASED Jan 1, 2025
  (was 30/60/20) under Va. Code § 46.2-472. Applies to all policies
  issued/renewed on or after Jan 1, 2025.
- Virginia UMV-fee-in-lieu-of-insurance option ELIMINATED July 1, 2024
  — insurance is now mandatory for all VA drivers
- Virginia UM/UIM REQUIRED, must MATCH liability limits (Va. Code
  § 38.2-2206); cannot be waived without specific written rejection.
  MAJOR 2023 CHANGE: VA UIM now STACKS ADDITIVELY on top of at-fault
  driver's BI limits (eff. July 1, 2023) — distinct from most states'
  reduced-by structure. Example: at-fault has $25K BI, insured has
  $100K UIM = $125K total available.
- VA fault system: At-fault with STRICT CONTRIBUTORY NEGLIGENCE — if
  insured is even 1% at fault, recovery from other driver may be
  completely barred. One of only 4 states still using pure contributory
  negligence — makes UM/UIM critically important.
- Companion VA UM forms: 65VA (notice), 66VA (UIM reduction election),
  68VA (UM alternative selection), 69VA (UM selection/rejection)
- Companion forms commonly used alongside this app:
  - 38VA — Notice of Information Practices (Privacy)
  - 60VA — Rental Reimbursement Coverage supplement
  - 61VA — Optional Medical and Disability Coverage notice
  - 65VA — Notice Regarding UM Coverage
  - 66VA — Election to Reduce UIM
  - 68VA — UM Alternative Coverage Selection
  - 69VA — UM Coverage Selection/Rejection
- Section version (290VA) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
- Primary statutes: Va. Code Title 38.2 (Insurance), § 38.2-2206 (UM/UIM),
  § 46.2-472 (minimum limits), § 38.2-2212 (cancellation/non-renewal),
  14 VAC 5-400 (claims handling regulations)
- State regulator: Virginia Bureau of Insurance under the State
  Corporation Commission (SCC) — not a standalone Department of Insurance
