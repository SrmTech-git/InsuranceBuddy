ACORD 290VA (2025-11) — Virginia Personal Auto Application Section
===================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290VA
Edition: 2025-11
Title: Virginia Personal Auto Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Virginia state variant)
States: VA
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Virginia. Used
in combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application —
Applicant Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The
290VA holds the auto-specific data and Virginia-specific UM elections.

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM/UIM (with Virginia-specific UM elections),
  MedPay, comp, coll, optional medical and disability coverage
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Virginia-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity is captured on ACORD 88, not duplicated here)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290VA) rather than the standalone full app (90VA)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Virginia UM coverage typically required at limits equal to liability;
  insureds may elect to reduce (66VA), accept alternative coverage
  (68VA), or reject UM (69VA) via separate forms
- Companion forms commonly used: 38VA (privacy), 60VA (rental), 61VA
  (optional medical/disability notice), 65VA (UM notice), 66VA (UIM
  reduction election), 68VA (UM alternative selection), 69VA (UM
  selection/rejection)
- Use 290VA with ACORD 88; use 90VA standalone
- Virginia minimum liability limits: 50/100/25 (INCREASED Jan 1, 2025
  from 30/60/20) under Va. Code § 46.2-472
- VA UMV fee option ELIMINATED July 1, 2024 — insurance now mandatory
- VA UM/UIM REQUIRED, must match liability limits; UIM ADDITIVE since
  July 1, 2023 (Va. Code § 38.2-2206)
- VA is strict contributory negligence state (1% bar)
- State regulator: Virginia Bureau of Insurance / State Corp Commission
