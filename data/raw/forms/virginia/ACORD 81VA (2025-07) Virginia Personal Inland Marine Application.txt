ACORD 81VA (2025-07) — Virginia Personal Inland Marine Application
===================================================================
Form number: ACORD 81VA
Edition: 2025-07
Title: Virginia Personal Inland Marine Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Virginia state variant)
States: VA
Policy term: 12 months (typical for personal inland marine / scheduled items policy)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, adding/removing scheduled items, value updates

Purpose:
Standalone application for a Virginia Personal Inland Marine policy.
Captures the applicant and the scheduled valuable items (jewelry,
fine art, furs, silverware, cameras, musical instruments, golf
equipment, antiques, collectibles) that the insured wants to schedule
under a personal articles / personal inland marine floater. State-
specific variant of national ACORD 81.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Schedule of items to be covered, each with:
  * Item description (e.g., "Diamond engagement ring, 1.5ct round
    brilliant, platinum setting")
  * Year acquired and source
  * Insured value
  * Serial / identifying number (where applicable)
  * Appraisal information: appraiser name, date, value
- Coverage type: all-risk (typical) vs named-perils
- Per-item limits and any aggregate limit
- Deductible (often zero or low for scheduled items)
- Storage and security details: home safe, vault, frequent travel,
  display use
- Prior coverage and prior loss history on these items
- Underwriting questions: items appraised within last 3-5 years,
  storage when not in use
- Virginia-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New Virginia personal articles / inland marine policy submission
- Renewal where item values need to be updated (typical every 3-5 years
  with fresh appraisal)
- Adding new items to an existing schedule (engagement ring purchase,
  fine art acquisition, etc.)
- Removing items (sold, gifted, or no longer owned)

Notes:
- Personal inland marine floater coverage is generally broader than the
  HO scheduled-personal-property endorsement: all-risk vs named-perils,
  no deductible vs HO deductible, agreed-value vs actual cash value
- Standard categories: jewelry, fine art, furs, silverware/flatware,
  cameras and photographic equipment, musical instruments, golf
  equipment, sporting equipment, firearms (in some states), antiques
- Companion to Virginia personal lines: typically issued alongside or
  separate from the homeowners policy
