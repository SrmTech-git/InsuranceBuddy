ACORD 62VA (1997-06) — Virginia Property Supplement Ordinance or Law Coverage; Additional Cov. Available
=========================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 62VA
Edition: 1997-06
Title: Virginia Property Supplement Ordinance or Law Coverage; Additional Cov. Available
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Virginia state variant)
States: VA
Policy term: Matches the underlying property policy (typically 12 months); election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (ordinance/law election); change in coverage

Purpose:
Virginia-required notice and election form for Ordinance or Law
coverage. After a covered property loss, building codes may require
reconstruction to higher current standards than what existed before
the loss. Standard property policies include limited ordinance/law
coverage (typically 10% of Coverage A); this form notifies the
insured of the standard coverage and offers additional coverage as
an endorsement.

Captures:
- Statutory description of ordinance or law coverage
- Three coverage components typically:
  * Coverage A — Loss to Undamaged Portion of Building (cost to
    demolish undamaged portions that code requires removed)
  * Coverage B — Demolition Cost (cost of demolition and debris
    removal beyond standard limits)
  * Coverage C — Increased Cost of Construction (cost to rebuild
    to current code standards)
- Standard included coverage (typically 10% of Coverage A)
- Additional coverage available with selected higher limits
- Insured's election to accept or reject additional ordinance/law
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Virginia property submission — election captured at issuance
- Renewal where the ordinance/law election is being modified
- Anytime the insured wishes to add or remove additional coverage

Notes:
- Ordinance/law coverage matters most for older homes where current
  building codes require significant upgrades after a partial loss
  (e.g., electrical, plumbing, structural)
- Without additional coverage, the insured may face significant out-
  of-pocket costs to bring a partially-damaged older home back to
  current code
