ACORD 290TN (2014-12) — Tennessee Personal Automobile Application Section
==========================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290TN
Edition: 2014-12
Title: Tennessee Personal Automobile Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Tennessee state variant)
States: TN
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Tennessee. Used
in combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application —
Applicant Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The
290TN holds the auto-specific data needed to underwrite a Tennessee
auto policy under Tenn. Code Ann. § 55-12.

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM/UIM, MedPay, comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Tennessee-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity is captured on ACORD 88, not duplicated here)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290TN) rather than the standalone full app (90TN)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Tennessee operates an electronic insurance verification program (IVPS)
- UM coverage mandatory; insured may reject in writing
- Use 290TN with ACORD 88; use 90TN standalone
- Tennessee minimum liability limits: 25/50/15 (TCA § 55-12-107)
- TN UM included by default at BI limits; rejectable in writing
  (TCA § 56-7-1201). UIM offered; REDUCED-BY structure.
- TN is at-fault with modified comparative fault (50% bar)
- State regulator: Tennessee Department of Commerce and Insurance (TDCI)
- Electronic verification: "Drive Insured TN"
