ACORD 61MI (2025-07) — Michigan Choice of Bodily Injury Liability Coverage Limits
==================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 61MI
Edition: 2025-07
Title: Michigan Choice of Bodily Injury Liability Coverage Limits
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (BI limits selection required); change of BI limits

Purpose:
Captures the Michigan insured's selection of Bodily Injury (BI)
Residual Liability coverage limits. Post-2019 no-fault reform
(effective July 2, 2020) introduced choices for residual liability
limits that drivers can carry in addition to mandatory PIP and PPI.
This form documents the insured's affirmative selection of BI limits,
including any election to keep the statutory default minimum versus a
higher limit.

Captures:
- Description of available BI limit options (e.g., 50/100, 100/300,
  250/500, 500/1000)
- Description of the statutory default minimum BI limit
- Insured's selection of BI per-person / per-accident limits
- Disclosure that BI residual liability covers claims by injured third
  parties beyond what PIP / PPI cover
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Michigan auto submission — BI limits selection required as part
  of post-reform coverage menu
- Renewal where BI limits are being modified
- Midterm endorsement changing BI limits

Notes:
- Post-2019 no-fault reform: Michigan moved from a single statutory
  BI minimum to a default with multiple selectable higher options
- BI residual liability is distinct from PIP (which covers the
  insured's own injuries) and from PPI (which covers property damage
  to others' real property only, not vehicles)
- Companion forms: 90MI (Personal Auto App), 66MI (PIP Medical
  Selection — Individual), 62MI (Collision Options)
