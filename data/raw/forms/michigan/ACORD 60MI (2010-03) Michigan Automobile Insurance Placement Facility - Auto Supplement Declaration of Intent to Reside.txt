ACORD 60MI (2010-03) — Michigan Automobile Insurance Placement Facility: Michigan Auto Supplement – Declaration of Intent to Reside
====================================================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 60MI
Edition: 2010-03
Title: Michigan Automobile Insurance Placement Facility: Michigan Auto Supplement – Declaration of Intent to Reside
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying MAIPF auto policy term (typically 6 months); declaration persists until residency changes
Transaction types: New MAIPF business; renewal where residency must be reaffirmed

Purpose:
Captures the insured's declaration that they reside in Michigan (or
have a definite intent to reside in Michigan for at least 30 days) —
a requirement for coverage through the Michigan Automobile Insurance
Placement Facility (MAIPF), Michigan's auto residual market /
assigned-risk pool. Drivers who cannot obtain coverage in the
voluntary market apply through MAIPF, which requires bona fide
Michigan residency.

Captures:
- Michigan address (current and intended)
- Declaration of current Michigan residence or definite intent to
  reside in Michigan for at least 30 days
- Statement explaining the basis for residency / intent
- Acknowledgment that MAIPF eligibility depends on this declaration
- Acknowledgment of the requirement to maintain Michigan no-fault
  insurance coverage during the residency period
- Driver license number
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New MAIPF auto submission for an applicant denied in the voluntary
  market
- Renewal of MAIPF coverage where residency must be reaffirmed
- Reporting a change in residency that affects MAIPF eligibility

Notes:
- MAIPF (Michigan Automobile Insurance Placement Facility) is
  Michigan's auto residual market — provides coverage to drivers
  rejected by the voluntary market
- MCL 500.3301 et seq. authorizes MAIPF
- Voluntary-market counterpart: 860MI (2020-07) — same underlying
  declaration purpose ("Declaration of Intent to Reside") but used
  for voluntary-market submissions rather than MAIPF
- The form title difference is the key tell:
  * 60MI title explicitly leads with "Michigan Automobile Insurance
    Placement Facility" — MAIPF / assigned risk
  * 860MI title omits the MAIPF prefix — voluntary market
- The 2010-03 edition predates the 2019 no-fault reform
