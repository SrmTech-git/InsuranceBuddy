ACORD 136MI (2025-07) — Michigan Selection of Personal Injury Protection (PIP) Medical Coverage - Commercial Business
======================================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 136MI
Edition: 2025-07
Title: Michigan Selection of Personal Injury Protection (PIP) Medical Coverage - Commercial Business
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying commercial auto policy term (typically 12 months); selection persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (commercial PIP tier election); change of PIP tier; fleet adjustments

Purpose:
Commercial counterpart to 66MI. Captures the business entity's
selection of a Personal Injury Protection (PIP) medical coverage tier
for a commercial / fleet auto policy under Michigan's post-2019
no-fault reform (MCL 500.3107c, effective July 2, 2020). Election is
made at the entity / fleet level rather than per individual.

Captures:
- Description of each PIP medical tier available to commercial fleets
  (unlimited, $500K, $250K, $250K with managed-care exclusions, $50K,
  Medicaid-only opt-out where applicable)
- Disclosure of premium and exposure differences across tiers
- Business / fleet identification (named insured, policy number)
- Entity representative's selection of tier
- Disclosure that the election applies to all covered fleet vehicles
- Authorized representative signature and date

When used:
- New Michigan commercial auto submission — PIP tier selection required
- Renewal of commercial fleet where PIP tier is being re-selected
- Adding fleet vehicles to an existing policy
- Midterm change to fleet PIP coverage

Notes:
- Michigan's 2019 no-fault reform applies to commercial / fleet autos
  as well as personal — fleets had to elect PIP tiers post-reform
- For commercial fleets, the practical considerations differ from
  personal: drivers are W-2 employees who often have employer health
  coverage (which may qualify for $50K tier); fleet medical exposure
  for the entity is also different from individual exposure
- MCCA per-vehicle assessment applies to unlimited-tier commercial
  fleets
- Companion forms: 137MI (Commercial Auto Coverages section), 138MI
  (Garage and Dealers), 90MI / 66MI (personal counterparts)
