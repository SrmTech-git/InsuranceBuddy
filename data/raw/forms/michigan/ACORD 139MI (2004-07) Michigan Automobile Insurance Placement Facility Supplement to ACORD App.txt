ACORD 139MI (2004-07) — Michigan Automobile Insurance Placement Facility Supplement to ACORD App. – Application for Commercial, Public Automobiles and Corporately Owned Vehicles
==================================================================================================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 139MI
Edition: 2004-07
Title: Michigan Automobile Insurance Placement Facility Supplement to ACORD App. – Application for Commercial, Public Automobiles and Corporately Owned Vehicles
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying MAIPF commercial auto policy term (typically 12 months)
Transaction types: New MAIPF commercial business; renewal; fleet adjustments

Purpose:
MAIPF-specific supplement for commercial, public, and corporately
owned auto applications. Used when a commercial fleet operator cannot
obtain coverage in the voluntary market and applies to the Michigan
Automobile Insurance Placement Facility (MAIPF) — Michigan's auto
residual market. Used alongside the national commercial auto
application package (ACORD 125 + 127 + state coverage forms).

Captures:
- Business identification and contact information
- Type of commercial auto operation (trucking, public auto, corporate
  fleet, etc.)
- Description of fleet vehicles and operations
- Reasons for voluntary-market denial (typically required by MAIPF)
- Prior insurance history and lapses
- MAIPF-specific underwriting disclosures
- Authorized representative signature

When used:
- New MAIPF commercial / public auto submission
- Renewal of MAIPF commercial coverage
- Adding vehicles or operations to existing MAIPF fleet policy

Notes:
- MAIPF authority: MCL 500.3301 et seq.
- Companion forms: 60MI (personal MAIPF residency declaration), 137MI
  (commercial coverages section), 136MI (commercial PIP medical
  selection), national 125 / 127 (commercial app + business auto section)
- Predates the 2019 no-fault reform; underwriting language may not
  reflect post-reform PIP tier mechanics
