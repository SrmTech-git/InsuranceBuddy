ACORD 50MI (2010-08) — State of Michigan Certificate of No-Fault Insurance
===========================================================================
Form number: ACORD 50MI
Edition: 2010-08
Title: State of Michigan Certificate of No-Fault Insurance
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); reissued at each renewal
Transaction types: N/A (proof-of-insurance card, not a transaction document)

Purpose:
Proof of no-fault insurance issued under a Michigan personal auto policy.
Carried in the vehicle to satisfy Michigan's no-fault statute
(MCL 500.3101 et seq.), which requires every vehicle owner to maintain
Personal Protection Insurance (PIP), Property Protection Insurance (PPI),
and Residual Liability (BI/PD) coverage.

Captures:
- Insured's name and address
- Policy number
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Year, make, model, and VIN of the insured vehicle
- Insurer name and NAIC code
- Agent / agency name and contact (if applicable)

When used:
- Carried in the vehicle as proof of no-fault insurance
- Presented to law enforcement during a traffic stop or accident investigation
- Presented to the Michigan Secretary of State for vehicle registration / renewal
- Required to be shown at the scene of any accident

Notes:
- Michigan's no-fault system requires PIP medical coverage; since the
  2019 no-fault reform (effective July 2, 2020), insureds choose from
  tiered PIP medical limits — Medicaid-only opt-out, $50K (Medicare /
  qualifying coverage), $250K, $500K, or unlimited
- The card is titled "Certificate of No-Fault Insurance" rather than
  "Identification Card" — distinct from other states' terminology
- The 2010-08 edition predates the 2019 reform and likely does NOT reflect
  post-reform PIP-tier disclosure that newer cards may include
