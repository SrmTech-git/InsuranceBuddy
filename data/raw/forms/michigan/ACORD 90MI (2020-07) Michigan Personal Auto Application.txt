ACORD 90MI (2020-07) — Michigan Personal Auto Application
==========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90MI
Edition: 2020-07
Title: Michigan Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Michigan — the
most complex state auto app in the country due to Michigan's no-fault
system. Captures the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and the
PIP medical tier selection required under Michigan's no-fault statute
(MCL 500.3101 et seq., as amended by the 2019 no-fault reform).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  qualifying health coverage (Medicare/Medicaid) for PIP-tier eligibility
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI/PD (Residual Liability), PIP medical (tier selection),
  PIP non-medical (wage loss / replacement services / survivor),
  PPI (Property Protection Insurance — $1M statutory), UM/UIM,
  comp, coll, optional coverages
- PIP medical tier choice: Medicaid-only opt-out (with proof),
  $50K (qualifying Medicare/health-plan coverage), $250K, $500K,
  or unlimited
- Discounts claimed
- Prior insurance carrier, prior limits, lapse history
- Loss history
- Michigan-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Michigan personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Michigan policy
- Anytime the PIP medical tier selection is being made or changed
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- The 2020-07 edition is the first major edition issued AFTER the July 2,
  2020 effective date of Michigan's no-fault reform, so it incorporates
  PIP-tier disclosure language
- Companion forms typically completed alongside this app:
  - 61MI — Choice of Bodily Injury Liability Coverage Limits
  - 62MI — Collision Insurance Options Notice
  - 66MI — Selection of PIP Medical Coverage (Individual)
  - 60MI — MAIPF Declaration of Intent to Reside (where assigned-risk)
  - 860MI — Auto Supplement Declaration of Intent to Reside
- Michigan Catastrophic Claims Association (MCCA) per-vehicle assessment
  applies to policies with unlimited or higher PIP medical tiers
- Section version (290MI) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
- Michigan default BI limits: 250/500 DEFAULT (MCL § 500.3009).
  Statutory floor: 50/100 — but policy defaults to 250/500 unless
  insured signs DIFS-approved BI form to select lower limits.
- Michigan minimum PD: $10K — same as MN, one of the lowest in the U.S.
  But Michigan also requires SEPARATE Property Protection Insurance
  (PPI) of $1,000,000 for damage to stationary property (buildings,
  parked cars) — unique to MI no-fault.
- Michigan UM/UIM is NOT REQUIRED — optional. Insurer must offer, but
  insured may decline. Critical gap if insured chooses low BI limits
  ($50K/$100K) without UIM.
- Primary statutes: MCL § 500.3101-3179 (Michigan No-Fault Act),
  § 500.3009 (liability minimums), § 500.3107c-d (PIP options),
  § 500.3020/.2123/.3224 (cancellation/non-renewal)
- State insurance regulator: Michigan Department of Insurance and
  Financial Services (DIFS). Michigan Secretary of State handles
  licensing/registration (no DMV).
