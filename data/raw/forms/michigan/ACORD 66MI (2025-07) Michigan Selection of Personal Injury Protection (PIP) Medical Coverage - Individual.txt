ACORD 66MI (2025-07) — Michigan Selection of Personal Injury Protection (PIP) Medical Coverage - Individual
============================================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 66MI
Edition: 2025-07
Title: Michigan Selection of Personal Injury Protection (PIP) Medical Coverage - Individual
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); selection persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (PIP tier election required); change of PIP tier; re-selection at renewal where allowed

Purpose:
The single most consequential form in Michigan personal auto. Captures
the individual insured's selection of a Personal Injury Protection
(PIP) medical coverage tier under Michigan's post-2019 no-fault
reform (MCL 500.3107c, effective July 2, 2020). The insured chooses
from five PIP medical levels, each with different premium impact, and
the choice — once made — applies to all auto-related medical expenses
for that policy.

Captures:
- Description of each PIP medical tier and statutory eligibility:
  * Unlimited PIP medical (the historical Michigan no-fault default)
  * $500,000 per person per accident
  * $250,000 per person per accident
  * $250,000 with managed-care exclusions (where offered)
  * $50,000 per person per accident (requires the insured AND every
    resident relative to have qualifying health coverage — Medicare
    Part A/B counts; Medicaid does NOT qualify for this tier)
  * Opt-out (Medicaid-only) — available only if the insured AND every
    resident relative are enrolled in Medicaid OR have other qualifying
    PIP-equivalent health coverage
- Disclosure of how each tier affects premium and out-of-pocket exposure
- Documentation requirements for $50K and opt-out tiers (proof of
  qualifying coverage)
- Insured's selection of tier
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Michigan personal auto submission — PIP tier selection mandatory
- Renewal where PIP tier is being re-selected
- Midterm change to PIP tier (subject to carrier underwriting)
- Whenever qualifying health coverage status changes for the insured
  or a resident relative (affects eligibility for $50K or opt-out tiers)

Notes:
- The 2019 no-fault reform was Michigan's largest auto insurance
  overhaul in 40+ years; previously, unlimited lifetime medical was
  mandatory for every Michigan auto policy
- Michigan Catastrophic Claims Association (MCCA) per-vehicle assessment
  applies to unlimited tier (and historically to high-tier policies);
  lower tiers carry lower or no MCCA assessment
- The opt-out and $50K tiers carry substantial risk: if the insured
  loses qualifying health coverage, they may be uninsured for serious
  auto injuries
- Companion forms: 90MI (Auto App), 136MI (commercial-business version
  of this PIP selection)
