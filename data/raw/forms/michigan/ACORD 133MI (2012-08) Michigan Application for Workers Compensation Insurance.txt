ACORD 133MI (2012-08) — Michigan Application for Workers' Compensation Insurance
=================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 133MI
Edition: 2012-08
Title: Michigan Application for Workers' Compensation Insurance
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: 12 months (typical for WC; subject to audit)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change, audit-driven adjustment

Purpose:
Michigan-specific application for Workers' Compensation Insurance.
Captures the employer, operations, payroll by classification, and
loss history needed to underwrite a Michigan WC policy. Michigan WC
operates as a competitive market with an assigned-risk facility
(Michigan Workers' Compensation Placement Facility, MWCPF) for
employers unable to obtain coverage in the voluntary market.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Employer (named insured): name, address, FEIN, business structure
- Business description, operations, NAICS / SIC
- Years in business, number of employees
- Locations (each Michigan location)
- Payroll by classification:
  * NCCI class code (or Michigan-specific class code where applicable)
  * Class description
  * Annual payroll amount
  * Number of employees in each class
- Officer / partner / family member inclusion or exclusion elections
  (see companion 172MI for partner/spouse/child/parent exclusion)
- Subcontractor / 1099 contractor exposure
- Prior carrier and experience modification factor (NCCI-based)
- 3-5 year loss history (paid, reserved, incurred by year)
- Safety programs, drug-testing programs, return-to-work programs
- Other states coverage required
- Additional insureds, waiver of subrogation requirements
- General information: other policies in force, foreign operations,
  prior cancellations, prior denials
- Michigan-specific WC disclosures and acknowledgments
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Michigan WC submission to a voluntary-market carrier
- Renewal of existing Michigan WC policy
- Submission to MWCPF (assigned-risk) when voluntary market declines —
  see companion 173MI for placement-facility-specific person exclusions

Notes:
- Michigan WC is NOT monopolistic — both voluntary market and
  assigned-risk facility (MWCPF) coverage available
- Companion forms:
  * 172MI — Application for Exclusion of Partner(s), Spouse, Child
    or Parent (allows family member exclusion from WC)
  * 173MI — Michigan WC Placement Facility Specific Person Exclusion
- Experience modification factor (ex-mod) heavily influences MI WC
  pricing — established by NCCI
