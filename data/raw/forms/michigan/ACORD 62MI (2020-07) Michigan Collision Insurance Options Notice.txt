ACORD 62MI (2020-07) — Michigan Collision Insurance Options Notice
===================================================================
Form number: ACORD 62MI
Edition: 2020-07
Title: Michigan Collision Insurance Options Notice
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months); election persists until changed in writing
Transaction types: New business (collision option election); change to collision coverage

Purpose:
Notice and election form describing Michigan's three collision coverage
options — Limited Collision, Broad Form Collision, and Standard
Collision — and capturing the insured's selection. Michigan's
collision options differ from most states because of how fault is
treated under the no-fault statute.

Captures:
- Description of Limited Collision (pays only when the at-fault driver
  is more than 50% at fault; insured collects from at-fault driver
  in most scenarios)
- Description of Broad Form Collision (pays regardless of fault; no
  deductible if not more than 50% at fault, deductible applies if more
  than 50% at fault)
- Description of Standard Collision (pays regardless of fault, with
  the chosen deductible applying in all cases)
- Insured's selection of collision option
- Selected deductible
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Michigan auto submission — collision option election when comp/
  coll is being added
- Renewal where collision option is being modified
- Midterm change to collision coverage type or deductible

Notes:
- Michigan's three-option collision structure is distinct nationally —
  it reflects the interaction of no-fault with property damage liability
- Limited Collision is cheapest but provides the least protection
- Broad Form is middle-ground; Standard is most expensive
- Companion forms: 90MI (Auto App), 61MI (BI Limits Choice)
- 2020-07 edition is the first major edition after the July 2, 2020
  no-fault reform effective date
