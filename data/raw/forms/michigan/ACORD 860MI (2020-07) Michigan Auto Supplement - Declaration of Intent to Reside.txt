ACORD 860MI (2020-07) — Michigan Auto Supplement – Declaration of Intent to Reside
===================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 860MI
Edition: 2020-07
Title: Michigan Auto Supplement – Declaration of Intent to Reside
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Michigan state variant)
States: MI
Policy term: Matches the underlying auto policy term; declaration persists until residency changes
Transaction types: New business (residency declaration); renewal where residency must be reaffirmed; residency change

Purpose:
Captures the insured's declaration of Michigan residence — or
definite intent to reside in Michigan for at least 30 days — for the
voluntary auto market. Michigan auto policies require the insured
to be a Michigan resident or to have demonstrable intent to reside
in Michigan; this supplement documents that declaration outside of
the MAIPF residual-market context.

Captures:
- Michigan address (current and intended)
- Declaration of current Michigan residence or definite intent to
  reside in Michigan for at least 30 days
- Statement explaining the basis for residency / intent
- Acknowledgment that policy eligibility and PIP tier qualifications
  depend on Michigan residency
- Acknowledgment of the requirement to maintain Michigan no-fault
  insurance coverage during the residency period
- Driver license number
- Insured signature and date

When used:
- New Michigan voluntary-market auto submission — declaration captured
  at issuance
- Renewal where residency must be reaffirmed
- Reporting a change in residency that affects policy eligibility

Notes:
- 860MI is the VOLUNTARY-MARKET counterpart to 60MI (which is the
  MAIPF / assigned-risk version). Same underlying declaration purpose,
  different distribution channel:
  * 60MI title leads with "Michigan Automobile Insurance Placement
    Facility" — MAIPF only
  * 860MI title is the generic "Michigan Auto Supplement" — voluntary
    market
- 2020-07 edition is the first major edition after the July 2, 2020
  no-fault reform effective date — reflects post-reform language and
  the importance of Michigan residency to PIP tier eligibility
  (residency required to qualify for opt-out and $50K PIP tiers
  under MCL 500.3107c)
- 30-day residency / intent threshold is the standard Michigan auto
  residency standard tied to compulsory insurance requirements
