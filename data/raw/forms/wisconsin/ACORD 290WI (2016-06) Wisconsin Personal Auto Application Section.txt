ACORD 290WI (2016-06) — Wisconsin Personal Auto Application Section
====================================================================
Form number: ACORD 290WI
Edition: 2016-06
Title: Wisconsin Personal Auto Application Section
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Wisconsin state variant)
States: WI
Policy term: Matches the underlying personal auto policy term (typically 6 or 12 months)
Transaction types: New business, renewal, midterm change — used as the auto-specific section in a modular ACORD application package

Purpose:
Auto-specific section of a personal auto application in Wisconsin. Used
in combination with ACORD 88 (Personal Insurance Application —
Applicant Information Section), which captures applicant identity. The
290WI holds the auto-specific data needed to underwrite a Wisconsin
auto policy under Wis. Stat. § 632.32 (compulsory auto insurance).

Captures:
- Drivers: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation,
  3-5 year violation/accident history
- Vehicles: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM (mandatory), UIM (where offered), MedPay,
  comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages and discounts
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Wisconsin-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature
(NOTE: Applicant identity is captured on ACORD 88, not duplicated here)

When used:
- New business — when the agency uses ACORD's modular section system
  (88 + 290WI) rather than the standalone full app (90WI)
- Renewal where the carrier requires fresh auto-section data while
  reusing applicant info from 88
- Adding a vehicle or driver to an existing modular-section policy

Notes:
- Wisconsin moved from financial responsibility to compulsory auto
  insurance in 2010 (Wis. Stat. § 632.32)
- Wisconsin requires UM; UIM is offered but may be rejected
- Use 290WI with ACORD 88; use 90WI standalone
- Wisconsin minimum liability limits: 25/50/10 (Wis. Stat. § 344.15)
- WI UM MANDATORY at 25/50 (Wis. Stat. § 632.32(4)); cannot be waived.
  UIM optional since 2011; if purchased, minimum 50/100.
- WI is at-fault with modified comparative negligence (50% bar)
- State regulator: Wisconsin OCI; WisDOT handles SR-22
- Electronic verification: WisDOT Safety Responsibility
