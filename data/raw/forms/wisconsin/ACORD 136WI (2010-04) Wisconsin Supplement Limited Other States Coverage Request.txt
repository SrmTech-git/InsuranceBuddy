ACORD 136WI (2010-04) — Wisconsin Supplement Limited Other States Coverage Request
===================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 136WI
Edition: 2010-04
Title: Wisconsin Supplement Limited Other States Coverage Request
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Wisconsin state variant)
States: WI
Policy term: Matches the underlying WC policy term (typically 12 months)
Transaction types: New business (Other States Coverage election); change in other-states exposure

Purpose:
Wisconsin Workers' Compensation supplement requesting "Limited Other
States Coverage" — Item 3.C of the WC policy — which extends Wisconsin
WC coverage to employees who temporarily work in other listed states.
Without Other States Coverage, a Wisconsin WC policy may not respond
to injuries that occur outside Wisconsin. This form lists the other
states where coverage is needed.

Captures:
- Employer / policy information
- States to be added to Item 3.C "Other States Coverage" of the WC
  policy
- Description of employee activities in the other states (travel,
  temporary work, sales calls, etc.)
- Estimated payroll exposure in each other state
- Confirmation that no employees are HIRED or RESIDE in the listed
  other states (typically a condition of "limited" other states
  coverage; permanent presence in another state usually requires
  WC coverage IN that state via a separate policy or extraterritorial
  endorsement)
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Wisconsin WC submission where employees travel to other states
- Adding states to Other States Coverage when business expands
  exposure
- Midterm endorsement adding states

Notes:
- "Other States Coverage" (Item 3.C) is distinct from "States
  Covered" (Item 3.A — the primary state listed on the policy)
- Limited Other States Coverage typically applies only to:
  * Employees temporarily in the other state
  * Activities of less than 90 days (carrier-specific)
  * Non-resident employees
- For employees hired in or residing in another state, separate
  state WC coverage is required (some carriers use multi-state
  endorsement; others require separate policies)
- Companion forms: 133WI (Pool), 4WI (FROI)
