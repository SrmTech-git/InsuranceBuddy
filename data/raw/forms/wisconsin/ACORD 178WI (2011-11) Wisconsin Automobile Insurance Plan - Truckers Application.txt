ACORD 178WI (2011-11) — Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan – Truckers Application
===================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 178WI
Edition: 2011-11
Title: Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan – Truckers Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Wisconsin state variant)
States: WI
Policy term: 12 months (typical for WAIP truckers)
Transaction types: New WAIP business, renewal, fleet adjustments

Purpose:
Application for trucker / motor carrier automobile insurance through
the Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan (WAIP) — Wisconsin's auto
residual market / assigned-risk pool. Used when a trucking operation
is unable to obtain coverage in the voluntary market. Truckers
typically have heightened underwriting requirements due to higher
exposures and federal MCS-90 minimum limits for interstate
operations.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Named insured (motor carrier): name, address, FEIN, business
  structure, USDOT number, MC number (interstate operations), Wisconsin
  intrastate authority where applicable
- Operations: types of cargo, radius of operations (local, regional,
  national), interstate vs intrastate
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, GVWR class (light/medium/heavy
  truck, tractor, trailer), garaging, use
- Drivers: CDL info, endorsements, training, hours-of-service
  compliance, MVR
- Cargo / commodity types (HAZMAT, household goods, general freight,
  refrigerated, etc.)
- Coverages selected: liability (CSL — typically $750K-$1M minimum
  for interstate fleets per MCS-90, sometimes $5M for HAZMAT), UM/UIM,
  MedPay, comp, coll, hired auto, non-owned auto, trailer interchange,
  motor truck cargo (often separate)
- Limits and deductibles
- Prior insurance and denial history
- Loss history
- WAIP-specific disclosures, including federal MCS-90 filing
  requirements
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Wisconsin trucker submission to WAIP after voluntary-market
  denial
- Renewal of WAIP trucker coverage
- Adding tractors, trailers, or drivers to existing WAIP fleet

Notes:
- Trucking is the highest-exposure commercial auto risk class —
  voluntary-market access has been challenging, increasing reliance
  on WAIP and similar residual markets
- Federal motor carrier minimums (FMCSA / MCS-90):
  * $750,000 for general freight interstate carriers (>$10K GVWR)
  * $5,000,000 for HAZMAT haulers (varies by HAZMAT class)
  * Higher for bulk oil, passenger carriers
- Wisconsin intrastate truckers must comply with state minimums
  (Wis. Stat.); interstate truckers must additionally comply with
  FMCSA
- Companion forms: 97WI (WAIP Private Passenger), 177WI (WAIP
  Business Auto)
