ACORD 177WI (2011-11) — Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan Business Auto Application
======================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 177WI
Edition: 2011-11
Title: Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan Business Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Wisconsin state variant)
States: WI
Policy term: 12 months (typical for WAIP business auto)
Transaction types: New WAIP business, renewal, fleet adjustments

Purpose:
Application for commercial / business automobile insurance through
the Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan (WAIP) — Wisconsin's auto
residual market / assigned-risk pool. Used when a commercial fleet
operator (other than truckers — see 178WI) is unable to obtain
coverage in the voluntary market. WAIP assigns applicants to
participating carriers.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Named insured (business entity): name, address, FEIN, business
  structure
- Business description, operations, NAICS / SIC
- Years in business, number of employees
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, type (sedan, light truck, van,
  bus, etc.), garaging address, use (service, delivery, sales,
  passenger transport, etc.)
- Drivers: license info, history, training
- Coverages selected: liability (CSL or split limits), UM/UIM,
  MedPay, comp, coll, hired auto, non-owned auto
- Limits and deductibles
- Prior insurance and denial history (REQUIRED for WAIP eligibility)
- Loss history (commercial fleets typically 3-5 years)
- WAIP-specific disclosures and acknowledgments
- Authorized signatory and date

When used:
- New Wisconsin business auto submission to WAIP after voluntary-
  market denial (non-trucker fleets)
- Renewal of WAIP business auto coverage
- Adding vehicles or drivers to existing WAIP fleet

Notes:
- WAIP = Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan — administered by AIPSO
- For truckers / motor carriers, see companion form 178WI (WAIP
  Truckers Application) — separate underwriting and rate structure
- For private passenger, see companion form 97WI
- Federal motor carrier minimums (MCS-90) may apply to interstate
  fleets — separate from WAIP limits
