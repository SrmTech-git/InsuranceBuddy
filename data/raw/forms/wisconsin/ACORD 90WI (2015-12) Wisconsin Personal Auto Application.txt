ACORD 90WI (2015-12) — Wisconsin Personal Auto Application
===========================================================
Form number: ACORD 90WI
Edition: 2015-12
Title: Wisconsin Personal Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Wisconsin state variant)
States: WI
Policy term: 6 months (typical for personal auto); 12-month terms also issued depending on carrier
Transaction types: New business; renewal applications (where required); adding driver/vehicle; significant midterm changes

Purpose:
Standalone application for a personal auto policy in Wisconsin. Captures
the applicant, drivers, vehicles, coverages, and state-required
disclosures needed to underwrite, bind, and issue a Wisconsin auto
policy under Wisconsin's compulsory auto insurance statute (Wis. Stat.
§ 632.32).

Captures:
- Producer / agency information
- Applicant: name, address, DOB, marital status, occupation, contact
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status, occupation
- Driver history (typically 3-5 years): accidents, violations, suspensions
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, body type, garaging address
- Vehicle use: pleasure / commute / business, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages: BI, PD, UM (mandatory), UIM (where offered), MedPay,
  comp, coll
- Deductibles (comp and coll)
- Optional coverages
- Discounts claimed
- Prior insurance carrier and lapse history
- Loss history
- Wisconsin-required disclosures and acknowledgments
- Fraud statement and applicant signature

When used:
- New business — applying for a Wisconsin personal auto policy
- Renewal applications (where required)
- Adding a driver or vehicle to an existing Wisconsin policy
- Midterm changes that require fresh underwriting

Notes:
- Wisconsin moved from financial responsibility to compulsory auto
  insurance in 2010 under Wis. Stat. § 632.32
- Wisconsin requires UM coverage; UIM is offered but may be rejected
- Section version (290WI) drops applicant identity in favor of ACORD 88
- Wisconsin minimum liability limits: 25/50/10 (Wis. Stat. § 344.15) —
  PD minimum $10K is the LOWEST in the spreadsheet
- Wisconsin UM: MANDATORY at 25/50 minimum; cannot be waived
  (Wis. Stat. § 632.32(4)). UM covers BI only — not property damage.
- Wisconsin UIM: NOT mandatory (since 2011 amendments to § 632.32);
  insurer must NOTIFY policyholder UIM is available; insured may
  reject in writing. If purchased, minimum 50/100.
- WI is at-fault with MODIFIED COMPARATIVE NEGLIGENCE (50% bar)
  (Wis. Stat. § 895.045) — slightly different from MN's 51% bar
- Primary statutes: Wis. Stat. § 344.15 (minimums), § 632.32 (UM
  mandatory, UIM optional since 2011, MedPay must offer), § 631.36
  (cancellation/non-renewal); Wis. Admin. Code Ch. Ins 6
- State regulator: Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance
  (OCI); WisDOT DMV handles SR-22 filings and Safety Responsibility
  Law enforcement
- Electronic verification: WisDOT Safety Responsibility system
