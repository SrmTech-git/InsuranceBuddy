ACORD 97WI (2003-10) — Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan Private Passenger Auto Application
==============================================================================================
Form number: ACORD 97WI
Edition: 2003-10
Title: Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan Private Passenger Auto Application
Type: Insurance form (ACORD industry standard, Wisconsin state variant)
States: WI
Policy term: 6 months (typical for WAIP private passenger auto) or 12 months depending on assignment
Transaction types: New business (WAIP submission after voluntary-market denial), renewal, change in driver/vehicle

Purpose:
Application for private passenger automobile insurance through the
Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan (WAIP) — Wisconsin's auto residual
market / assigned-risk pool. Used when an applicant is unable to
obtain coverage in the voluntary market (typically due to driving
record, prior cancellations, or other underwriting factors). WAIP
assigns applicants to participating carriers in proportion to their
voluntary-market share.

Captures:
- Producer / agency information (where applicable; WAIP submissions
  may be made by independent agents or directly by the applicant)
- Applicant information: name, address, DOB, contact, occupation
- Driver list: license number/status, DOB, marital status,
  occupation, prior cancellations and denials (key to WAIP eligibility)
- Driver history: violations, accidents, license suspensions —
  typically more extensive than voluntary-market apps
- Vehicle list: VIN, year/make/model, garaging address, ownership,
  use, annual mileage
- Lienholder / additional interest information
- Coverages selected: BI, PD, UM (mandatory in WI), UIM, MedPay,
  comp, coll — typically at or near state minimums for WAIP risks
- Deductibles
- Prior insurance and denial history (REQUIRED for WAIP eligibility
  — typically need documentation of voluntary-market denials)
- Loss history
- WAIP-specific disclosures and acknowledgments
- Applicant signature and date

When used:
- New Wisconsin private passenger auto submission to WAIP after
  voluntary-market denial
- Renewal of WAIP coverage (some risks return to voluntary market
  after good loss experience)
- Adding driver or vehicle to existing WAIP policy

Notes:
- WAIP = Wisconsin Automobile Insurance Plan — administered by AIPSO
  (Automobile Insurance Plans Service Office) on behalf of Wisconsin
- Applicants typically need documentation of voluntary-market denials
  (carrier-specific — often 2+ rejections)
- WAIP coverage is more expensive than voluntary market; coverage
  limits typically at state minimums (insureds can elect higher with
  premium impact)
- Companion forms: 177WI (WAIP Business Auto), 178WI (WAIP Truckers)
