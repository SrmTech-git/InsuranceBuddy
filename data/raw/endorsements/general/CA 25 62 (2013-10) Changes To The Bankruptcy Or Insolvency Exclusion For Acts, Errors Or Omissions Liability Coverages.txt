CA 25 62 (2013-10) — Changes To The Bankruptcy Or Insolvency Exclusion For Acts, Errors Or Omissions Liability Coverages
=========================================================================================================================
Form number: CA 25 62
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Changes To The Bankruptcy Or Insolvency Exclusion For Acts, Errors Or Omissions Liability Coverages
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Modifies the mechanics of the exception to the bankruptcy/insolvency
exclusion in the AEO (Acts, Errors or Omissions) Liability section of
CA 00 25. The base form's AEO section excludes coverage for claims
arising from the bankruptcy or insolvency of an insurer the dealer
placed coverage with for a customer, but contains an exception when
the placed insurer had an A.M. Best's rating of B+ or higher at time
of placement. This endorsement makes that threshold variable rather
than fixed.

Captures:
- Replaces the hardcoded A.M. Best's B+ threshold in the AEO
  bankruptcy/insolvency exception with a fill-in schedule
- Allows the parties to designate a different rating bureau and/or a
  different minimum rating threshold for the exception to apply

When used:
- Situations where the insured and insurer agree to a different financial
  strength standard than the ISO default A.M. Best's B+ threshold
- Insurers who prefer a different rating agency or a higher/lower
  minimum rating for the coverage exception

Notes:
- Does NOT eliminate the bankruptcy/insolvency exclusion — it only
  changes the mechanics of the exception that can restore coverage
- CA 25 65 is the alternative if the goal is to eliminate all exceptions
  to the exclusion (i.e., make the bankruptcy/insolvency exclusion
  absolute)
- The schedule must be completed with the agreed rating bureau and
  minimum credit score/rating; an incomplete schedule may leave the
  exception unenforceable
