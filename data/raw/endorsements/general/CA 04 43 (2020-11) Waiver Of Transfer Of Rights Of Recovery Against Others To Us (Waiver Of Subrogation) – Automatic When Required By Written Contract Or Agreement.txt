CA 04 43 (2020-11) — Waiver Of Transfer Of Rights Of Recovery Against Others To Us (Waiver Of Subrogation) – Automatic When Required By Written Contract Or Agreement
=====================================================================================================================================================================
Form number: CA 04 43
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Waiver Of Transfer Of Rights Of Recovery Against Others To Us (Waiver Of Subrogation) – Automatic When Required By Written Contract Or Agreement
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Waives the insurer's subrogation rights against any party when a written
contract or agreement in effect before the loss requires the named insured
to waive subrogation. "Automatic" means the waiver applies to all qualifying
contracts without requiring the insured to name each third party on the
endorsement — far more practical for insureds with many vendor, contractor,
or tenant agreements that contain waiver-of-subrogation clauses.

Captures:
- Blanket waiver of the insurer's right to recover payments from a third party
  when a pre-loss written contract required the named insured to grant that
  waiver
- Applies automatically to any qualifying contract — no scheduling required
- Does not apply retroactively to contracts executed after a loss occurs

When used:
- Any commercial auto account with ongoing contracts (leases, construction
  agreements, service contracts, vendor agreements) that include standard
  waiver-of-subrogation language
- Required by most commercial leases, general contractors, and large
  certificate holders
- Preferred over CA 04 44 (scheduled version) when the insured has multiple
  or fluid third-party relationships

Notes:
- The key requirement is that the written contract must have been in place
  BEFORE the loss — the insured cannot execute a new contract after a loss
  to retroactively waive subrogation
- CA 04 44 is the scheduled (non-automatic) version, requiring each party
  to be listed by name; use CA 04 44 when the carrier wants more control
  over which parties receive the waiver
- A waiver of subrogation affects the carrier's ability to recover losses;
  underwriters may price this endorsement separately for high-severity fleets
- Certificates of insurance should reflect this endorsement when required by
  contract (ACORD 25 has a checkbox for waiver of subrogation)
