CA 99 54 (2013-10) — Covered Auto Designation Symbol
====================================================
Form number: CA 99 54
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Covered Auto Designation Symbol
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Creates a custom covered auto designation symbol when the standard
ISO symbol system (Symbols 1-9) does not adequately define which autos
should be covered under a specific commercial auto program. The standard
symbols (1 = any auto, 7 = specifically described autos, 8 = hired autos,
9 = non-owned autos, etc.) may not fit unusual fleet compositions or
program designs. This endorsement allows the insurer and insured to craft
a unique symbol with a customized definition of covered autos tailored
to their specific needs.

Captures:
- Custom definition of which autos qualify as "covered autos" under
  the designated symbol
- Applied to specific coverages in the declarations to define the auto
  scope for those coverages
- Used where standard Symbols 1-9 do not precisely describe the intended
  covered auto population

When used:
- Specialty auto programs with unusual vehicle classifications or
  coverage triggers not captured by standard symbols
- Fleet programs where covered auto eligibility has specific criteria
  beyond standard symbol definitions
- Program business or specialty underwriting where customization of
  the covered auto definition is required

Notes:
- The ISO symbol system is designed to cover most commercial auto
  scenarios; CA 99 54 is used when the standard system falls short
- Custom symbols must be carefully defined to avoid ambiguity at claim
  time — vague definitions create coverage disputes
- This endorsement is most commonly seen in specialty or program markets
  rather than standard commercial auto placements
