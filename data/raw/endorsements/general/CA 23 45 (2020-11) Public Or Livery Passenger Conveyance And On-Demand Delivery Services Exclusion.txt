CA 23 45 (2020-11) — Public Or Livery Passenger Conveyance And On-Demand Delivery Services Exclusion
====================================================================================================
Form number: CA 23 45
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Public Or Livery Passenger Conveyance And On-Demand Delivery Services Exclusion
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends the public/livery conveyance exclusion (CA 23 44) to also exclude
on-demand delivery services — gig-economy delivery platforms such as
DoorDash, Amazon Flex, Instacart, and similar services. Added in the
2020-11 edition to address the proliferation of gig delivery activity, which
creates the same underwriting exposure as TNC rideshare but in the delivery
context: employees using company vehicles (or personal vehicles on company
time) for off-policy gig work.

Captures:
- Exclusion of coverage for vehicles used as for-hire passenger conveyances
  (taxis, TNC, livery) — same as CA 23 44
- PLUS exclusion of coverage while the vehicle is being used for on-demand
  delivery services (gig platforms, app-dispatched delivery)
- Applies during the period the vehicle is available for or actively engaged
  in gig delivery or rideshare dispatch

When used:
- CA 00 20 and CA 00 01 policies where the underwriter wants to exclude
  both rideshare AND gig delivery exposures in a single endorsement
- Fleet accounts where drivers might use company vehicles for side income
  through delivery apps
- Any account where the broader exclusion is preferred over the narrower
  CA 23 44 (passenger only)

Notes:
- CA 23 44 covers passenger conveyance only; CA 23 45 covers both passenger
  AND delivery — use CA 23 45 when both exposures need to be excluded
- Gig delivery exclusions have become increasingly standard as app-based
  delivery platforms expanded significantly; insurers do not price standard
  commercial auto for that use
- Carriers should verify that fleet drivers have contractual restrictions
  against using company vehicles for gig work, supporting the policy exclusion
