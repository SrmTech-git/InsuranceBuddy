CA 20 71 (2020-11) — Auto Loan/Lease Gap Coverage
=================================================
Form number: CA 20 71
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Auto Loan/Lease Gap Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Covers the difference ("gap") between the actual cash value (ACV) settlement
the insurer pays on a total-loss covered auto and the outstanding loan or
lease balance owed on that vehicle. When a vehicle is totaled and the ACV
is less than the loan payoff amount — which commonly occurs with new vehicles
due to rapid depreciation — the insured is left personally responsible for
the remaining balance. This endorsement eliminates that out-of-pocket gap.

Captures:
- Gap payment between the ACV total-loss settlement and the outstanding
  loan or lease balance
- Applies to vehicles financed or leased through a financial institution
  or lessor
- Covers the gap on a per-vehicle basis for scheduled autos or eligible
  financed vehicles

When used:
- Company fleets with financed or leased vehicles where negative equity
  (owe more than ACV) is possible
- Any commercial account with newer vehicles under loan or lease that
  could be total losses
- Especially relevant for vehicles purchased with low down payments,
  longer loan terms (60–84 months), or rapidly depreciating vehicle types

Notes:
- Gap coverage is very common in personal auto; CA 20 71 brings the same
  concept to the commercial auto space
- The gap is most pronounced in the first 1–3 years of a loan/lease and
  narrows as the loan amortizes; gap coverage becomes less valuable for
  older vehicles with equity
- Does not pay for amounts attributable to overdue loan payments, late
  charges, or financial penalties from the lender — only the true ACV-to-
  balance gap
- Lenders and lessors often require gap coverage as a lease or loan condition
  for commercial fleets
