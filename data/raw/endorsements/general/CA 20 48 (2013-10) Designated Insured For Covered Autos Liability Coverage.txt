CA 20 48 (2013-10) — Designated Insured For Covered Autos Liability Coverage
============================================================================
Form number: CA 20 48
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Designated Insured For Covered Autos Liability Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Adds a designated person or organization as an insured for covered autos
liability coverage under the named insured's CA policy — essentially
expanding who qualifies as "an insured" for auto liability purposes to
include a scheduled third party. Unlike a standard additional insured
endorsement that focuses on protecting a third party from claims arising
from the named insured's operations, CA 20 48 gives the designated insured
broader insured status for the auto liability section.

Captures:
- A specifically named person or organization added as an insured for
  covered autos liability (BI/PD)
- The designated insured's liability coverage is provided to the same extent
  as the named insured for claims involving covered autos

When used:
- Joint ventures or business partners that share vehicle operations and need
  insured status on each other's policies
- Government entities or project owners that require contractor auto policies
  to name them as insureds (not just additional insureds)
- Any arrangement where a third party needs full insured treatment (not just
  AI status) for the auto liability coverage

Notes:
- The designated insured is added as an insured for covered autos liability;
  the scope of their coverage is generally limited to what the underlying
  "Who Is An Insured" provision provides — the endorsement adds the party
  by name but does not independently expand coverage beyond that baseline
- Used in conjunction with other endorsements (primary/noncontributory,
  waiver of subrogation) for comprehensive contractual compliance packages
- Should be scheduled carefully — broad insured designation can affect
  the policy's limits allocation if both parties have claims on the same
  policy
