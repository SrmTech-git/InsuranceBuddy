CA 99 59 (2013-10) — Garagekeepers Coverage – Customers' Sound-Receiving Equipment
==================================================================================
Form number: CA 99 59
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Garagekeepers Coverage – Customers' Sound-Receiving Equipment
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy with garagekeepers coverage
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Extends garagekeepers coverage to include customers' sound-receiving
equipment (radios, audio systems, CB radios, and similar equipment)
installed in customers' vehicles. Standard garagekeepers coverage covers
the vehicle itself but typically excludes audio, electronic, and custom
equipment installed in the vehicle. This endorsement adds coverage for
sound-receiving equipment owned by the customer and installed in their
vehicle while the vehicle is in the insured's care, custody, or control.

Captures:
- Physical damage (comprehensive and collision) coverage for customers'
  sound-receiving equipment permanently installed in customers' vehicles
  while in the insured's garagekeepers custody
- Equipment must be permanently installed — portable sound equipment
  is not covered under this endorsement
- Covers damage to the equipment during the garagekeepers period
  (repair, storage, service)
- Theft of sound-receiving equipment is excluded
- Subject to any applicable sublimit for sound-receiving equipment

When used:
- Auto repair shops and service operations with garagekeepers coverage
  where customers bring in vehicles with aftermarket audio systems
- Specialty shops (audio/stereo installers, custom shops) where
  high-value audio equipment in customer vehicles is a routine exposure
- Any garage operation where customer vehicle electronic equipment loss
  during custody is a meaningful risk

Notes:
- Standard garagekeepers coverage excludes electronic equipment; this
  endorsement is the add-back for sound/audio equipment specifically
- Compare with CA 99 60 (Audio, Visual And Data Electronic Equipment
  Coverage Added Limits), which addresses broader electronic equipment
  coverage for the insured's own vehicles
- High-value aftermarket audio equipment can be worth thousands of dollars;
  confirm sublimits are adequate for the operations handled
