CA 99 40 (2013-10) — Exclusion Or Excess Coverage Hazards Otherwise Insured
===========================================================================
Form number: CA 99 40
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Exclusion Or Excess Coverage Hazards Otherwise Insured
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Excludes coverage or converts coverage to excess for specific hazards that
are otherwise insured under another policy. Used to prevent overlapping or
duplicate coverage when the insured has a separate policy covering a
specific auto-related exposure. Rather than having two policies potentially
responding to the same claim, this endorsement designates the commercial
auto policy as either excluded or excess for the identified hazard, ensuring
the specifically tailored policy responds first.

Captures:
- Exclusion of named hazards from the commercial auto policy where
  those hazards are covered under a separate, identified policy
- Alternatively, converts coverage to excess-only for designated hazards
  where another policy provides primary coverage
- Coordinates coverage between the commercial auto policy and other
  identified insurance programs

When used:
- Programs where the insured has overlapping coverage from multiple
  policies (e.g., a separate contractor's equipment policy and a commercial
  auto policy that both cover certain vehicles)
- Situations where an underwriter wants to confirm the commercial auto
  policy does not respond to specific hazards already covered elsewhere
- Any commercial auto program requiring coordination with another policy

Notes:
- This endorsement requires clear identification of both the excluded/excess
  hazard and the other policy that covers it
- Improperly worded coordination of coverage endorsements can leave gaps
  if the "other" policy also excludes or limits coverage for the hazard
- Brokers should confirm that the identified other coverage is actually
  in force and adequate before relying on this endorsement for coverage coordination
