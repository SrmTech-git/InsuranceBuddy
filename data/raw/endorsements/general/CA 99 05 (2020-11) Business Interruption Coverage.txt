CA 99 05 (2020-11) — Business Interruption Coverage
===================================================
Form number: CA 99 05
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Business Interruption Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Adds a daily-benefit business interruption coverage to a commercial auto
policy when a specifically scheduled covered auto is out of service due
to a covered physical damage loss. This is a scheduled daily indemnity
form — the insured selects a daily benefit amount and a maximum benefit
period; coverage is not based on actual income loss documentation in the
way standalone commercial property BI is. A 24-hour waiting period applies
before the daily benefit begins. Coverage ends when the vehicle is repaired,
replaced, or the maximum benefit days are exhausted.

Captures:
- A scheduled daily benefit amount for each day a specifically listed
  covered auto is out of service following a covered physical damage loss
- Subject to a 24-hour waiting period before the daily benefit begins
- Coverage ends at the earlier of vehicle repair/replacement or the
  maximum benefit period scheduled in the endorsement

When used:
- Businesses where revenue is directly tied to specific vehicles being
  operational (trucking, delivery, contractors, specialty equipment operators)
- Any commercial auto program where vehicle downtime creates quantifiable
  income loss
- Programs for fleets where each vehicle contributes directly to revenue generation

Notes:
- 2020-11 edition; updated in conjunction with the 2020-11 commercial auto
  program revision
- This is a relatively uncommon endorsement for most commercial auto programs;
  more commonly used in specialty fleet and trucking programs
- Business interruption coverage under a commercial auto policy is narrower
  than standalone BI coverage — it is vehicle-specific and tied to physical
  damage losses only
- Insureds with broader operational continuity concerns should also consider
  commercial property BI coverage
