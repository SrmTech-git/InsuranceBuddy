CA 99 61 (2013-10) — Loss Payable Clause – Audio, Visual And Data Electronic Equipment Coverage Added Limits
============================================================================================================
Form number: CA 99 61
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Loss Payable Clause – Audio, Visual And Data Electronic Equipment Coverage Added Limits
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy with CA 99 60 electronic equipment coverage
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Adds a loss payable clause specifically to the audio, visual, and data
electronic equipment coverage provided under CA 99 60. When a lender
or lessor has a financial interest in electronic equipment installed in
a covered vehicle (equipment financed separately or as part of a vehicle
package), this endorsement directs the electronic equipment insurance
proceeds to the named loss payee. Companion form to CA 99 60.

Captures:
- Loss payable designation for a named loss payee with a financial
  interest in the insured's audio, visual, or data electronic equipment
- Directs CA 99 60 electronic equipment claim payments to the
  named loss payee
- Standard loss payee protections for the identified equipment lienholder

When used:
- Programs where electronic equipment installed in covered autos is
  separately financed
- Equipment leasing arrangements where the lessor requires loss payable
  coverage on their equipment
- Any CA 99 60 program where a third party has a security interest
  in the covered electronic equipment

Notes:
- Must be used in conjunction with CA 99 60 (Audio, Visual And Data
  Electronic Equipment Coverage Added Limits) — CA 99 61 has no
  independent coverage function
- The standard vehicle loss payable (CA 99 44) does not automatically
  extend to separately financed equipment; CA 99 61 is needed when
  the equipment financier is different from the vehicle lienholder
- Verify that the loss payee named in CA 99 61 matches the actual
  equipment financier or lessor to avoid payment disputes at claim time
