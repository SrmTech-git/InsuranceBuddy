CA 20 13 (2013-10) — Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Schedule Of Limits For Owned Autos
====================================================================================
Form number: CA 20 13
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Schedule Of Limits For Owned Autos
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Allows a leasing or rental company to assign different liability limits to
different vehicles or vehicle categories on their owned fleet rather than
applying a single blanket limit to all covered autos. Useful when the fleet
contains a mix of vehicle types with different risk profiles — for example,
standard passenger cars at one limit and larger commercial vehicles or
specialty vehicles at higher limits.

Captures:
- Separate scheduled liability limits (BI/PD) for each scheduled auto or
  class of autos on the leasing/rental company's policy
- Replaces the single blanket limit that would otherwise apply to all owned
  autos equally

When used:
- Large fleet owners with heterogeneous fleets where risk and contractual
  requirements vary by vehicle class
- Leasing companies whose lease agreements specify different minimum
  liability limits for different vehicle types
- Programs where regulatory requirements mandate higher limits for
  certain vehicle categories (e.g., passenger vans vs. sedans)

Notes:
- This endorsement provides coverage design flexibility for the lessor's
  own fleet policy; it does not alter coverage for lessees (whose limits
  are governed by their own policy or the rental agreement)
- Scheduled limits can result in audit complexity at renewal if the fleet
  composition changes frequently; carriers may prefer blanket limits for
  simpler fleet programs
