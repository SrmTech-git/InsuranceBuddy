CA P 101 (2022-03) — Notice Section Of Important Provisions – Single Interest Automobile Physical Damage Insurance Policies
===========================================================================================================================
Form number: CA P 101
Edition: 2022-03
Title: Notice Section Of Important Provisions – Single Interest Automobile Physical Damage Insurance Policies
Type: ISO Commercial Auto policyholder advisory notice
States: All

Purpose:
Policyholder notice specifically for the single-interest automobile physical
damage insurance program (CA 26 series), explaining important provisions
of that program to covered borrowers. Unlike standard commercial auto
advisory notices (CA P 001-013) which address business policyholders,
CA P 101 is directed at individual borrowers who are the subject of
lender-placed (force-placed) single-interest CPI coverage. Explains the
nature of lender-placed insurance — that it protects the lender's interest,
not the borrower's — and communicates key provisions and borrower rights.

Content:
- Explanation that single-interest CPI coverage is placed by the lender
  to protect the lender's collateral interest, not the borrower's property
- Key provisions of the CA 26 program affecting the borrower
- Borrower rights and notification requirements
- How the single-interest policy relates to (but does not replace)
  the borrower's own auto insurance
- Important limitations of single-interest coverage from the borrower's
  perspective

When used:
- Issued to borrowers on loans covered under CA 26 01 (individual policy)
  or CA 26 02 (master policy) single-interest programs
- Satisfies state consumer notice requirements for lender-placed insurance
  that must be disclosed to the borrower

Notes:
- The 100-series numbering (CA P 101 vs. CA P 001-013) indicates this is
  a CPI/single-interest specific notice rather than a standard commercial
  auto program notice
- State regulations governing lender-placed insurance often require specific
  borrower disclosures; CA P 101 provides ISO's standard form for this purpose
- Borrowers who receive this notice are not the named insured — the lender
  is; this notice explains that distinction and its practical implications
