CA 23 25 (2013-10) — Coverage For Injury To Leased Workers
==========================================================
Form number: CA 23 25
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Coverage For Injury To Leased Workers
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends the commercial auto policy to cover bodily injury to workers leased
to the named insured from staffing agencies or professional employer
organizations (PEOs). The base CA 00 01 excludes BI to employees; leased
workers may fall into an ambiguous category — they work for the named insured
but are technically employees of the staffing agency. This endorsement
provides coverage for those leased workers' BI claims arising from auto
accidents.

Captures:
- Bodily injury coverage for leased workers (temporary employees, staffing
  agency workers) injured in a covered auto accident during the course
  of their work for the named insured
- Removes the uncertainty about whether leased workers qualify for the
  employee BI exclusion or are treated as third-party claimants

When used:
- Any named insured that uses temporary staffing agency workers who
  operate company vehicles or ride in company vehicles as part of their
  work
- Motor carriers with owner-operators or temporary dispatched drivers
  from staffing agencies
- Construction, agriculture, and logistics companies with high temp-worker
  vehicle usage

Notes:
- Workers' compensation should be the primary coverage for leased worker
  injuries; CA 23 25 is a backup for situations where WC does not fully
  respond or the leased worker relationship creates ambiguity about
  WC applicability
- The staffing agency typically carries its own WC; the named insured's
  auto policy with this endorsement provides additional protection
- Without this endorsement, a leased worker injured in the named insured's
  vehicle may fall into a coverage gap between the employee exclusion
  (in the CA policy) and the third-party coverage (which they qualify for
  if not an employee)
