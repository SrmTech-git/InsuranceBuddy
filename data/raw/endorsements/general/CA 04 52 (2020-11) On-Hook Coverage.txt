CA 04 52 (2020-11) — On-Hook Coverage
=====================================
Form number: CA 04 52
Edition: 2020-11
Title: On-Hook Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Provides physical damage coverage for vehicles in tow — non-owned autos that
the named insured is transporting on a hook, tow bar, or tow line. Standard
garagekeepers coverage applies to vehicles in the insured's care for service
or storage; it does not clearly cover a vehicle while it is actively being
towed. On-hook coverage fills that gap for tow truck operators and recovery
services.

Captures:
- Physical damage to a non-owned auto being towed by the insured's tow truck
  or recovery vehicle (the vehicle "on the hook")
- Coverage during active towing — while the vehicle is being transported from
  point of pickup to the drop-off location
- Comprehensive and collision losses to the towed vehicle are typically
  covered under a single limit
- Coverage ends once the vehicle is parked or placed in storage (at which
  point garagekeepers coverage applies)

When used:
- Towing companies, roadside assistance operators, and vehicle recovery
  services
- Any account with tow trucks that regularly haul non-owned vehicles
- Distinguishes the towing exposure from the garagekeepers exposure —
  both endorsements may be needed for a full tow operation

Notes:
- On-hook and garagekeepers coverage work together for a towing operation:
  on-hook covers vehicles in transit on the tow truck; garagekeepers covers
  vehicles stored at the tow yard or in the shop
- The limit for on-hook coverage should reflect the maximum value of vehicles
  likely to be towed — luxury or exotic vehicle transport requires higher limits
- Unlike garagekeepers, on-hook coverage applies to vehicles the insured
  actively transports, not just stores; the vehicle's owner is not present
  to accept the risk
- Some towing contracts may shift liability to the tow operator, making
  on-hook coverage especially important
