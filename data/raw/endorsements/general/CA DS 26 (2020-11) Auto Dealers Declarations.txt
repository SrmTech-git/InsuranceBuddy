CA DS 26 (2020-11) — Auto Dealers Declarations
==============================================
Form number: CA DS 26
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Auto Dealers Declarations
Type: ISO Commercial Auto declarations form
States: All

Policy term: Sets the policy period for the CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form
Transaction types: New business; renewal; endorsement

Purpose:
The declarations page for the CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form.
The most complex of the three commercial auto declarations forms, it
documents the auto dealer's bundled coverage structure — covered autos
liability, general liability, acts/errors/omissions (AEO) liability,
garagekeepers, open lot physical damage, and all associated limits and
deductibles. Also records the dealer's franchises, locations, estimated
annual sales volume, and service department revenue (used for rating).

Captures:
- Named insured (dealer entity), DBA names, and all franchise locations
- Policy period
- Covered auto symbols and liability limits
- General liability and AEO limits and sublimits
- Garagekeepers coverage basis, limits, and deductibles per location
- Open lot physical damage coverage and inventory limits
- Schedule of covered autos (loaners, demonstrators, service vehicles)
- Annual sales volume, used car volume, service revenue estimates
- All CA 27 series and other endorsements attached
- Premium breakdown by coverage section

When used:
- Every CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form policy
- Required for all franchised and independent auto dealer programs

Notes:
- The CA DS 26 is the companion form to the CA 00 25 and CA 26 03
  (which handles the single-interest program declarations); they serve
  different program components
- Dealer declarations are among the most information-dense in commercial
  lines due to the CA 00 25's bundled multi-coverage structure
- Annual revenue estimates in the declarations directly affect premium;
  dealers should report accurate sales and service volumes to avoid
  premium audits or coverage disputes
