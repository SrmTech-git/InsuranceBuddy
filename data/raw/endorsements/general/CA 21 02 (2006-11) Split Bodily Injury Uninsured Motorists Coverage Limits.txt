CA 21 02 (2006-11) — Split Bodily Injury Uninsured Motorists Coverage Limits
============================================================================
Form number: CA 21 02
Edition: 2006-11
Title: Split Bodily Injury Uninsured Motorists Coverage Limits
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Establishes split per-person/per-accident limits for bodily injury uninsured
motorist (UM) coverage on a commercial auto policy. Rather than a single
combined UM limit, this endorsement sets separate limits — one per claimant
and one per accident — consistent with the split-limit structure common in
many state UM requirements and commercial auto programs.

Captures:
- UM bodily injury coverage structured as a per-person limit and a per-
  accident aggregate limit (e.g., $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident)
- Applies to bodily injury claims only (not property damage UM — see UMPD)
- The split-limit structure controls the maximum payout per injured person
  and per occurrence

When used:
- Commercial auto policies in states that permit or require split-limit UM
- Accounts that prefer the split-limit structure for cost control (the per-
  person cap limits high-severity single-claimant payouts)
- When the underlying liability is written on a split limit basis and the
  carrier wants UM limits to mirror the liability structure

Notes:
- CA 21 02 (BI only) vs. CA 21 07 (both BI and PD): use CA 21 02 in states
  where UM covers only bodily injury; CA 21 07 for states where UM includes
  property damage
- Split limits (e.g., 100/300) cap per-claimant recovery at $100K regardless
  of the number of claimants or accident severity; a CSL (combined single
  limit) of $300K would allow one claimant to receive the full $300K
- Some states mandate that commercial UM limits match liability limits
  unless the insured rejects higher limits in writing
