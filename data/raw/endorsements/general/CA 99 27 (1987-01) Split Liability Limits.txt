CA 99 27 (1987-01) — Split Liability Limits
===========================================
Form number: CA 99 27
Edition: 1987-01
Title: Split Liability Limits
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Converts the standard commercial auto single combined single limit (CSL)
liability coverage into split limits — separate limits for bodily injury
per person, bodily injury per occurrence, and property damage per
occurrence. The base commercial auto forms use a single combined limit;
some states, insurers, or insureds prefer the traditional split-limit
structure (e.g., 100/300/100). This endorsement restructures the liability
coverage accordingly.

Captures:
- Separate per-person bodily injury limit
- Separate per-occurrence bodily injury limit
- Separate per-occurrence property damage limit
- Replaces the single combined single limit with the three-part split
  limit structure

When used:
- Commercial auto policies where the insured or state requires split limits
- Programs where the CSL equivalent does not match the insured's
  desired or contractually required split-limit structure
- Any commercial auto policy where split liability limits are preferred
  over a combined single limit

Notes:
- 1987-01 edition — an older form that has remained in use because the
  split vs. CSL structure question is a longstanding program design choice
- Most modern commercial auto programs use CSL; split limits are more
  common in personal auto
- When using split limits, all three components must be adequate — a
  low per-person limit with a high per-occurrence limit may not provide
  adequate protection in severe single-claimant accidents
