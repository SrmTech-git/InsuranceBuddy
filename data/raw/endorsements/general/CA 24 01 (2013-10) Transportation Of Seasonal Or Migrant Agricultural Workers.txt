CA 24 01 (2013-10) — Transportation Of Seasonal Or Migrant Agricultural Workers
===============================================================================
Form number: CA 24 01
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Transportation Of Seasonal Or Migrant Agricultural Workers
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Provides commercial auto liability coverage for the transportation of
seasonal or migrant agricultural workers in covered autos. The Migrant
and Seasonal Agricultural Worker Protection Act (MSPA, 29 CFR Part 500
Subpart D) requires farm labor contractors and agricultural employers who
transport workers to carry insurance. This endorsement ensures the CA
policy responds to those MSPA obligations and includes a mandatory 30-day
prior-notice-of-cancellation requirement to the U.S. Department of Labor.

Captures:
- Liability coverage (BI/PD) for the transportation of seasonal or migrant
  agricultural workers in covered autos
- Compliance with MSPA insurance requirements (29 CFR §500.121): $100,000
  per seat, capped at $5,000,000 per vehicle
- 30-day prior cancellation notice to the DOL Wage and Hour Division as
  required by MSPA

When used:
- Agricultural growers, farm labor contractors, and farm operations that
  transport workers to and from fields
- Any insured required to comply with MSPA insurance requirements for
  agricultural worker transportation
- Labor contractors who specialize in agricultural worker transport

Notes:
- MSPA vehicle safety and insurance standards (29 CFR Part 500 Subpart D)
  apply to any vehicle transporting migrant or seasonal agricultural workers —
  there is no minimum worker-count threshold for MSPA compliance
- The MSPA insurance formula is $100,000 per seat with a $5,000,000 per-vehicle
  cap (29 CFR §500.121); this is a DOL/MSPA requirement, distinct from FMCSA
  passenger carrier limits
- State agricultural labor laws may impose additional requirements beyond
  federal minimums; verify applicable state regulations
- Passenger vans and buses used for worker transport may require CDLs and
  state public utility commission authority depending on vehicle size and state
