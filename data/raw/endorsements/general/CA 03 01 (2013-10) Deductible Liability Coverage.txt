CA 03 01 (2013-10) — Deductible Liability Coverage
==================================================
Form number: CA 03 01
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Deductible Liability Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Introduces a per-accident liability deductible under a commercial auto policy.
The insurer defends and settles claims; deductible reimbursement from the insured
is discretionary — the insurer may seek reimbursement but is not obligated to
advance the deductible on behalf of the insured. Applies to CA 00 01 (Business
Auto), CA 00 20 (Motor Carrier), CA 00 05 (Garage), and CA 00 25 (Auto Dealers).

Captures:
- Per-accident deductible amount, applicable to all liability (BI + PD) or to
  property damage only, depending on schedule selection
- Insurer's discretionary right to seek reimbursement from the insured for
  amounts paid within the deductible
- Premium credit per the ISO rating plan for the selected deductible amount

When used:
- Large fleet or commercial accounts seeking a premium reduction in exchange
  for retaining a portion of their liability exposure
- Accounts with favorable loss history where per-accident retention is appropriate

Notes:
- The defining distinction between CA 03 01 and CA 03 02 is the payment
  obligation: CA 03 01 uses "may" pay and seek reimbursement (discretionary);
  CA 03 02 uses "will" pay and seek reimbursement (mandatory)
- Both forms offer flexible deductible structures (all-liability or PD-only);
  neither is restricted to a "combined" or "split" model
- The deductible is a reimbursement obligation, not a true SIR; the insurer
  controls defense and settlement regardless of deductible application
- Collateral requirements for deductible reimbursement are typically negotiated
  separately in a loss-sensitive program agreement
