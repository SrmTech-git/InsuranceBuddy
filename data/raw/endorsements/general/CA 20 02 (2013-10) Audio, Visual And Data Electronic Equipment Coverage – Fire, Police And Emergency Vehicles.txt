CA 20 02 (2013-10) — Audio, Visual And Data Electronic Equipment Coverage – Fire, Police And Emergency Vehicles
===============================================================================================================
Form number: CA 20 02
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Audio, Visual And Data Electronic Equipment Coverage – Fire, Police And Emergency Vehicles
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends physical damage coverage to the specialized audio, visual, and data
electronic equipment permanently installed in fire, police, and emergency
vehicles. The base CA 00 01 policy limits or excludes coverage for electronic
equipment; emergency vehicles carry extremely high-value radio systems,
mobile data terminals, video equipment, and communications arrays that exceed
the base form's coverage parameters.

Captures:
- Physical damage coverage for permanently installed AV and data equipment
  in scheduled fire, police, and emergency vehicles
- Covers: mobile data terminals, two-way radio systems, dashcams and body
  cam recording equipment, GPS/AVL systems, emergency dispatch consoles,
  and similar fixed electronic equipment
- Coverage limit for the equipment (scheduled or per vehicle)

When used:
- Municipal and county governments insuring fire trucks, police vehicles,
  ambulances, and emergency management vehicles
- Private ambulance and emergency transport companies
- Any account with specialized emergency vehicles where the installed
  electronics represent significant value beyond the vehicle itself

Notes:
- Standard CA physical damage covers the vehicle itself; the exclusion or
  limitation on electronic equipment in CA 00 01 is designed to avoid
  covering consumer electronics and aftermarket add-ons — but it catches
  legitimate essential equipment in emergency vehicles
- The equipment in a modern police cruiser or paramedic unit can represent
  $30,000–$80,000+ in communications and data systems — far exceeding the
  value that base CA physical damage would cover without this endorsement
- Should be scheduled with up-to-date equipment values per vehicle to avoid
  underinsurance at the time of a total loss or fire
