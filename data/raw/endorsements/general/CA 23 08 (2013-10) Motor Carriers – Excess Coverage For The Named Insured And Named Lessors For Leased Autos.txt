CA 23 08 (2013-10) — Motor Carriers – Excess Coverage For The Named Insured And Named Lessors For Leased Autos
==============================================================================================================
Form number: CA 23 08
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Motor Carriers – Excess Coverage For The Named Insured And Named Lessors For Leased Autos
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Provides excess liability coverage for the named motor carrier and specified
lessors for claims arising from leased autos. In motor carrier operations,
owner-operators lease their equipment to carriers; when a leased truck is
involved in an accident, multiple parties may have liability exposure —
the owner-operator's primary policy, the carrier's policy, and the equipment
lessor. This endorsement layers the carrier's coverage above the owner-
operator's primary coverage for leased autos.

Captures:
- Excess liability for the named insured (motor carrier) when an owner-
  operator's leased truck is involved in a covered auto accident
- Protects named lessors (equipment owners) as excess insureds
- Defines the relationship between the owner-operator's primary policy and
  the carrier's excess layer

When used:
- Motor carriers that lease equipment from owner-operators under trip or
  permanent lease arrangements
- Carriers that want to ensure they have excess coverage above the owner-
  operator's primary limits for leased-in equipment
- Fleet programs with mixed owned and leased equipment

Notes:
- The 90-day lease provision under federal regulations (49 CFR §376.12)
  affects how liability flows between carriers and owner-operators; this
  endorsement works within that regulatory framework
- Without this endorsement, the carrier's CA 00 20 policy may be treated
  as primary alongside the owner-operator's policy, creating coverage
  conflicts; the excess structure prevents that
- CA 23 12 (Named Lessee as Insured) is the companion endorsement that
  adds the lessee (the carrier) to the owner-operator's policy
