CA 20 14 (2013-10) — Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Second Level Coverage
=======================================================================
Form number: CA 20 14
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Second Level Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Establishes the leasing or rental company's policy as a second-level (excess)
layer of liability coverage that activates only after the lessee's or renter's
primary auto insurance is exhausted. Unlike contingent coverage (CA 20 09),
which is a backstop for when lessee insurance fails entirely, second-level
coverage is an intentional program design where both the lessee's primary
coverage and the lessor's second-level coverage are expected to respond in
defined sequence.

Captures:
- Excess liability coverage on the leasing/rental company's policy that
  sits above the renter's primary auto insurance
- Defines the amount of coverage (limit) available at the second level
- Specifies triggering conditions: lessee's primary coverage must respond
  first and be exhausted or unavailable before the second level activates

When used:
- Rental car programs that offer renters a "second level" of liability
  protection as an optional purchase (damage waivers and liability
  supplements in rental agreements)
- Fleet lease programs with tiered coverage structures where the lessee
  carries primary and the lessor provides excess
- Any structured two-tier lease/rental liability program

Notes:
- Second-level coverage is intentional layering — both tiers are expected
  to work together; compare to CA 20 09 (contingent coverage) which is
  a safety net for the unexpected failure of lessee insurance
- Consumer rental programs often market the second-level coverage as
  "additional liability protection" or "supplemental liability coverage"
  (SLP) — this endorsement provides the policy mechanism
- The rental company charges a daily fee for second-level protection; the
  CA 20 14 endorsement covers those claims when the renter elects the option
