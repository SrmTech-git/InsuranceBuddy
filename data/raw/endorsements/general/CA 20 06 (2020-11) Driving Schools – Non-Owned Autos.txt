CA 20 06 (2020-11) — Driving Schools – Non-Owned Autos
======================================================
Form number: CA 20 06
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Driving Schools – Non-Owned Autos
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends the driving school's CA policy to cover liability arising from the
use of non-owned autos — specifically vehicles owned by students — during
instruction. Some driving schools provide their own vehicles (covered as
owned autos under CA 00 01); others conduct lessons in the student's own
car. When the instructor uses the student's vehicle, a standard CA policy
may not cover the school's liability if an accident occurs in a non-owned
vehicle used for instruction.

Captures:
- Non-owned auto liability coverage for the named insured (the driving
  school) when instructors operate students' personal vehicles during
  lessons
- Coverage applies during supervised instruction only, while the named
  insured's employee-instructor is in the vehicle
- Protects the school from vicarious liability for accidents during
  student-vehicle instruction

When used:
- Driving schools that offer "behind-the-wheel" instruction in the
  student's own vehicle
- Commercial driver training programs where training may occur in a
  student-owned commercial vehicle

Notes:
- Without this endorsement, a school that teaches in the student's car
  has potential liability exposure that falls outside the standard CA 00 01
  non-owned auto definition — the school isn't leasing or borrowing the car
  in the traditional sense
- The student's own personal auto policy may respond first, but the school
  needs coverage for its own organizational liability
- This endorsement covers the school's exposure; it does not insure the
  student's vehicle (physical damage to the student's car remains the
  student's concern under their own policy)
