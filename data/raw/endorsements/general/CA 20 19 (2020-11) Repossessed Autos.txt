CA 20 19 (2020-11) — Repossessed Autos
======================================
Form number: CA 20 19
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Repossessed Autos
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends commercial auto coverage to vehicles that the named insured has
repossessed from a defaulting borrower. After repossession, the lender
or their agent (a repossession company) takes temporary custody of the
vehicle pending resale; during this period the vehicle is neither fully
owned in the usual sense nor insured by the defaulting borrower. This
endorsement ensures the repossessing party has liability and physical
damage coverage during the post-repossession period.

Captures:
- Liability (BI/PD) coverage for repossessed autos while in the named
  insured's custody
- Physical damage coverage for repossessed autos during the custody period
- Applies from the time of repossession until the vehicle is sold or
  returned to the borrower

When used:
- Auto lenders, credit unions, and finance companies that repossess
  vehicles from defaulting borrowers
- Repossession agencies acting as agents for lenders
- Dealers that purchase and resell repossessed vehicles before sale

Notes:
- Standard CA 00 01 covers "owned autos" — a repossessed vehicle is in a
  gray area: the lender may have a security interest but not clear title
  until the repossession process is complete; this endorsement resolves
  that ambiguity
- Repossession companies face both driving liability (while moving the
  repossessed vehicle) and storage liability (while the vehicle is on
  the lot awaiting sale); both exposures are addressed
- For high-volume repossession operations, inland marine (garagekeepers
  floater) may also be relevant for physical damage on the storage lot
