CA 01 21 (2013-10) — Limited Mexico Coverage
============================================
Form number: CA 01 21
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Limited Mexico Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends the covered territory of a CA 00 01 or CA 00 20 policy to provide
limited liability and physical damage coverage for covered autos traveling
into Mexico. The base commercial auto coverage form excludes Mexico entirely;
this endorsement carves out a narrow exception for short-duration, border-area
trips by owned autos.

Captures:
- Liability coverage for bodily injury and property damage occurring in Mexico
  for trips within 25 miles of the U.S.-Mexico border and a duration limit of
  10 days per trip
- Physical damage coverage on the same restricted basis
- Coverage is excess over any valid and collectible Mexican insurance

When used:
- Commercial accounts that operate near the U.S.-Mexico border and whose
  vehicles occasionally cross for short trips (deliveries, service calls)
- Trucking or distribution operations with incidental Mexico exposure
- NOT a substitute for a full Mexican auto policy issued by an admitted
  Mexican insurer — for sustained Mexico operations, a separate Mexican
  policy is required and this endorsement should not be relied upon

Notes:
- Mexico requires vehicles to carry insurance from an admitted Mexican insurer;
  a U.S. policy alone (even with this endorsement) does not satisfy Mexican
  financial responsibility law — this endorsement covers the U.S. insurer's
  indemnification obligation, but the insured still needs a Mexican liability
  card at the border
- Coverage under this endorsement is strictly limited: distance from border,
  trip duration, and specific covered autos must all be within the endorsement's
  parameters or coverage does not apply
- For significant Mexico exposure, excess and surplus or specialty Mexican auto
  markets are the appropriate solution, not this endorsement
