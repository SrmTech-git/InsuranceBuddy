CA 99 30 (2013-10) — Tapes, Records And Discs Coverage
======================================================
Form number: CA 99 30
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Tapes, Records And Discs Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Adds coverage for tapes, records, discs, and similar audio/visual media
stored in covered autos. Standard commercial auto physical damage
coverage excludes electronic media (tapes, CDs, records, and similar
items) from coverage because they are considered personal property rather
than part of the vehicle. This endorsement extends physical damage
coverage to include these items when they are in a covered auto and
damaged in a covered loss.

Captures:
- Physical damage coverage for tapes, records, compact discs, and
  similar physical media objects used with audio, visual, or data
  electronic equipment in covered autos
- Coverage applies when the media is damaged as part of a covered
  physical damage loss to the vehicle; applies under Comprehensive
- Subject to a $200 per-occurrence sublimit; no deductible applies
- Covers physical media objects only — not intangible digital files
  or content stored in cloud or internal device memory

When used:
- Vehicles where employees regularly carry audio/visual media for
  business use (mobile music, audio books, training materials)
- Any commercial auto policy where employees transport personal or
  business media in covered vehicles

Notes:
- This is a 2013-10 edition form that predates the modern era of
  digital streaming and cloud-based media; physical media (CDs, DVDs,
  tapes) has become less common in vehicles
- Modern equivalents may involve damage to digital media storage devices —
  those are addressed by CA 99 60 (Audio, Visual And Data Electronic
  Equipment Coverage Added Limits) rather than this form
- This endorsement has limited practical use in current programs given
  the decline of physical media in vehicles; it remains available for
  operations that still use physical audio/visual media
