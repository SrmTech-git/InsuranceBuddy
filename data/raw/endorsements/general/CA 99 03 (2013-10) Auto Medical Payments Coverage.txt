CA 99 03 (2013-10) — Auto Medical Payments Coverage
===================================================
Form number: CA 99 03
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Auto Medical Payments Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy (CA 00 01, CA 00 20, CA 00 25)
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Adds auto medical payments coverage (MedPay) to a commercial auto policy.
The base CA 00 01, CA 00 20, and CA 00 25 do not include medical payments
coverage by default — it must be added by endorsement. MedPay covers
reasonable medical expenses for bodily injury sustained by the named
insured and passengers in covered autos, regardless of fault, up to the
selected limit. Serves as a no-fault first-party medical expense benefit
in states that have not enacted mandatory PIP.

Captures:
- Reasonable and necessary medical and funeral expenses for bodily injury
  caused by accident involving a covered auto
- Covers the named insured, family members, and other occupants of
  covered autos
- No-fault benefit — pays regardless of who caused the accident
- Subject to a per-person limit selected at policy inception

When used:
- Commercial auto programs in non-PIP states where the insured wants
  no-fault medical benefits for vehicle occupants
- Employers who want medical payments coverage for employees using
  company vehicles
- Any commercial auto policy where MedPay is desired as a supplemental
  medical benefit

Notes:
- MedPay is distinct from PIP (Personal Injury Protection) — PIP covers
  broader income loss and rehabilitation; MedPay is limited to medical
  and funeral expenses
- In PIP states, PIP endorsements (CA 21 series) replace or supplement MedPay
- The 2013-10 edition is the current ISO standard; this endorsement applies
  across all three base commercial auto forms
