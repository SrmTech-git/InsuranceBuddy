CA 99 47 (2013-10) — Employee As Lessor
=======================================
Form number: CA 99 47
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Employee As Lessor
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Modifies the commercial auto policy to provide coverage when an employee
leases or rents their personally owned vehicle to the employer for business
use. In some arrangements, employees formally lease their personal vehicles
to their employer rather than receiving a mileage reimbursement; the
vehicle is then used as a "hired auto" under the commercial auto policy.
Without this endorsement, the employee-as-lessor arrangement may create
a coverage gap because the employee's personal auto policy may exclude
business/commercial use, and the employer's commercial auto may not
recognize the employee's vehicle as a covered hired auto.

Captures:
- The employee-lessor is added as an insured under the employer's
  commercial auto policy for liability arising from use of the leased vehicle
- The leased vehicle is treated as a covered auto owned by the named insured
  (not merely as a hired auto), making liability and physical damage coverage
  available on the same terms as owned fleet vehicles
- Addresses both the liability and physical damage exposure for the
  employee-owned, employer-leased vehicle

When used:
- Employer programs where employees lease personal vehicles to the company
- Vehicle allowance or lease-to-employer programs
- Any arrangement where an employee's personal vehicle is formally
  designated as a hired auto under the employer's commercial auto policy

Notes:
- The legal form of the employee-to-employer vehicle arrangement matters
  for coverage purposes; a written lease agreement supports the hired auto
  treatment
- Employees in these arrangements should also confirm their personal auto
  policy covers commercial use or that the commercial auto policy provides
  adequate protection for their vehicle
- This is a relatively specialized form not commonly encountered in
  standard commercial auto programs
