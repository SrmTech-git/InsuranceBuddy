CA 99 33 (2013-10) — Employees As Insureds
==========================================
Form number: CA 99 33
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Employees As Insureds
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Extends insured status under the commercial auto liability coverage to
employees who use their own personally-owned vehicles for the named
insured's business. Under the base commercial auto forms, employees driving
the named insured's covered autos are already insureds. The gap this
endorsement fills is different: when an employee uses their personally-owned
auto on company business, that vehicle is not a "covered auto" under the
employer's BAP, so the employee has no insured status under the employer's
policy for that trip. CA 99 33 extends insured status to employees in
that non-covered-auto scenario, making the employer's policy respond on
excess over the employee's personal auto coverage.

Captures:
- Employee insured status for auto liability coverage when using their
  personally-owned autos for the named insured's business
- The employer's commercial auto policy responds on an excess basis over
  the employee's personal auto insurance in these situations
- Provides individual employees with liability protection and defense
  under the employer's CA policy for personal-vehicle business trips

When used:
- Employers whose employees use personal vehicles for business errands,
  client visits, or other company purposes
- Any commercial auto program where employee-owned vehicle use on company
  business creates a meaningful liability exposure for both the employee
  and the employer
- Programs seeking to confirm that the employer's CA policy responds when
  an employee's personal coverage is inadequate or absent

Notes:
- The gap this endorsement addresses is the personal-vehicle-on-company-
  business scenario — not the employer-owned fleet vehicle scenario;
  employees driving fleet vehicles are already insureds under the base forms
- The employer's policy typically responds excess over the employee's
  personal auto policy limit
- Compare with CA 04 39 (Volunteer Hired Autos) and CA 99 34 (Social
  Service Agencies – Volunteers As Insureds) which cover non-employee
  volunteer drivers
