CA 20 09 (2013-10) — Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Contingent Coverage
=====================================================================
Form number: CA 20 09
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Contingent Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Provides contingent liability coverage to a vehicle leasing or rental
company for claims arising from lessee/renter use of leased vehicles —
but only when the lessee's own insurance is unavailable, insufficient,
or exhausted. The leasing company's policy becomes a backstop rather than
primary coverage, protecting the lessor from gaps in the lessee's insurance
program without converting the lessor's policy into the primary layer.

Captures:
- Contingent (excess) liability for BI/PD claims arising from a lessee's
  or renter's use of a leased/rented vehicle
- Coverage triggers only when the lessee has no valid insurance or their
  limits are insufficient to cover the claim
- Protects the leasing company from exposure when lessees fail to maintain
  required insurance

When used:
- Leasing and rental companies that require lessees to carry their own auto
  insurance but want a backstop for compliance failures or coverage gaps
- Fleet programs where the lessor wants excess protection without becoming
  the primary insurer
- Rental programs with high-risk customer segments where insurance lapses
  are possible

Notes:
- "Contingent" means the coverage only fires when the lessee's own insurance
  is missing or exhausted — it is NOT a primary layer; the lessee's policy
  must respond first
- Differs from CA 20 14 (Second Level Coverage) in that CA 20 09 is
  triggered by absence of lessee coverage, while CA 20 14 is an intentional
  second-layer design where both layers are part of the program structure
- Many leasing companies use CA 20 09 as a financial backstop while
  requiring lessees to maintain minimum liability limits as a lease condition
