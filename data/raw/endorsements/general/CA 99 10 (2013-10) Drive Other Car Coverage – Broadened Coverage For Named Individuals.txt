CA 99 10 (2013-10) — Drive Other Car Coverage – Broadened Coverage For Named Individuals
========================================================================================
Form number: CA 99 10
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Drive Other Car Coverage – Broadened Coverage For Named Individuals
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Extends commercial auto liability and physical damage coverage to named
individuals when they drive autos they do not own — including personal
vehicles. Company executives, owners, and employees who drive company-provided
vehicles often have no personal auto policy of their own; when they
drive a non-owned vehicle (a rental, a spouse's car, a borrowed vehicle),
their employer's commercial auto policy does not automatically cover them.
This endorsement fills that gap by providing named individuals with coverage
while driving other autos not covered by the commercial policy.

Captures:
- Liability coverage for named individuals operating non-owned autos
  not covered by the commercial auto policy
- Physical damage coverage (if selected) for non-owned autos while
  operated by named individuals
- Covers the named individual personally in a manner similar to a
  personal auto policy for non-owned vehicle use

When used:
- Business owners and executives who rely solely on a company car and
  have no personal auto policy
- Key employees who are provided company vehicles and drive non-owned
  autos on personal time
- Any individual named on a commercial auto policy who needs protection
  when driving autos not listed on the policy

Notes:
- This is the commercial auto equivalent of the "Drive Other Car"
  coverage available on personal auto policies
- Without this endorsement, a business owner driving a rental car
  on a personal trip has no commercial auto coverage and may have
  no personal auto coverage either — a significant gap
- Named individuals must be specifically scheduled on the endorsement;
  coverage is not blanket for all employees
