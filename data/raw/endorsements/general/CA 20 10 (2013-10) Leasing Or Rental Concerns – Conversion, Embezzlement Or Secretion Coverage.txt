CA 20 10 (2013-10) — Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Conversion, Embezzlement Or Secretion Coverage
================================================================================================
Form number: CA 20 10
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Leasing Or Rental Concerns – Conversion, Embezzlement Or Secretion Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Covers physical damage loss to a leased or rented vehicle that occurs when
the lessee converts (fraudulently assumes ownership), embezzles, or secretes
the vehicle. Standard comprehensive (other-than-collision) coverage covers
theft by strangers, but when the person who took the vehicle had lawful
initial possession as a lessee or renter, the "theft" exclusion may apply.
This endorsement specifically addresses the lessee-fraud scenario.

Captures:
- Physical damage loss when a lessee or renter converts, embezzles, or
  removes (secretes) the leased vehicle with fraudulent intent
- Coverage for the leasing/rental company as the vehicle owner
- Covers the gap in standard comprehensive coverage that arises because
  the person who took the vehicle had lawful initial possession

When used:
- Auto leasing companies with long-term lease portfolios
- Rental car companies with open-ended rental agreements
- Any leasing program where the lessee's fraud or failure to return the
  vehicle is a material exposure

Notes:
- Standard theft coverage requires a "taking by stealth" — when a lessee
  simply refuses to return a vehicle or disappears with it, it may not
  qualify as theft under the base CA 00 01 policy
- Conversion in the legal sense means treating another's property as your
  own; secretion means hiding or concealing the vehicle from the owner;
  both are lessee-fraud scenarios this endorsement addresses
- Recovery is capped at 75% of the vehicle's ACV less any rental or lease
  deposit already paid by the lessee — full ACV is not restored by this
  endorsement
- Working with law enforcement to report the vehicle as stolen (after a
  reasonable period) may also be necessary alongside the insurance claim
