CA 26 01 (2020-11) — Single Interest Automobile Physical Damage Insurance Policy (Individual Policy Form)
=========================================================================================================
Form number: CA 26 01
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Single Interest Automobile Physical Damage Insurance Policy (Individual Policy Form)
Type: ISO Commercial Auto coverage form
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying loan or lease term
Transaction types: New business; force-placed coverage; lender-required coverage

Purpose:
The individual policy form for single-interest automobile physical damage
insurance — coverage that protects only the lender's financial interest in
a financed vehicle, not the borrower's. Issued on a per-borrower, per-vehicle
basis when a lender needs to force-place physical damage coverage on a specific
loan where the borrower has failed to maintain required insurance. Protects
the outstanding loan balance up to the vehicle's ACV.

Captures:
- Physical damage (comprehensive and collision) coverage for the lender's
  financial interest in a single financed vehicle
- Named beneficiary is the lender; the borrower is not a protected party
- Coverage for the vehicle's ACV up to the outstanding loan balance
- May include conversion, embezzlement, or secretion coverage to protect
  against borrower-related vehicle loss

When used:
- Force-placed auto insurance when a borrower lapses coverage on a financed
  vehicle and the lender must protect its collateral
- Individual loans where the lender needs coverage for a specific vehicle
  and borrower without enrolling in a master program

Notes:
- CA 26 01 is an individual policy form (one borrower, one vehicle);
  CA 26 02 is the finance master policy form for portfolio-level programs
- The borrower is NOT covered by this policy and receives no benefit
  from a physical damage claim; any payout goes to the lender to satisfy
  the loan balance
- Lenders typically notify borrowers of force-placed coverage; the premium
  is often added to the borrower's loan balance, increasing the amount owed
- Also called collateral protection insurance (CPI) in the lending industry
