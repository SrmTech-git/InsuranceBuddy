CA 22 01 (1987-01) — Named Individuals – Broadened Personal Injury Protection Coverage
======================================================================================
Form number: CA 22 01
Edition: 1987-01
Title: Named Individuals – Broadened Personal Injury Protection Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: FL, KS, KY, MI, MN, ND, NJ, NY, OR, UT

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends and broadens personal injury protection (PIP) coverage for specifically
named individuals under a commercial auto policy. PIP (no-fault coverage) pays
for medical expenses, lost wages, and other out-of-pocket costs regardless of
fault; the standard commercial auto PIP may have lower limits or narrower
eligibility than an individual owner or executive needs. This endorsement
provides enhanced PIP for scheduled individuals.

Captures:
- Broadened PIP benefits (higher limits, broader eligibility, or both) for
  named individuals beyond standard commercial PIP coverage
- Medical expense coverage, income continuation/lost wages, and other PIP
  benefits for the scheduled individuals and their relatives arising out of
  use or operation of any motor vehicle

When used:
- Sole proprietors, partners, officers, or executives who ride in or use
  company vehicles and want higher or broader PIP protection than the
  standard commercial auto PIP provides
- Any commercial auto in a PIP/no-fault state where key individuals need
  enhanced first-party coverage
- Only relevant in states that require or permit PIP on commercial autos
  (many no-fault states limit PIP to personal auto)

Notes:
- PIP availability and requirements vary significantly by state; in most
  states PIP is associated with personal auto policies (PAP) and may not
  apply to commercial vehicles or may apply in modified form
- The 1987-01 edition is very old; verify that this form remains current
  in the applicable state's commercial auto filing before using
- In no-fault states like Michigan, Florida, and New York, PIP mechanics
  differ; state-specific endorsements typically govern PIP for commercial
  vehicles in those jurisdictions
