CA 04 21 (2020-11) — Full Safety Glass Coverage
===============================================
Form number: CA 04 21
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Full Safety Glass Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Waives the comprehensive deductible for loss or damage to safety glass
(windshields, windows, sunroofs) on covered autos. Without this endorsement,
a comprehensive deductible applies to glass claims just as it would to any
other comprehensive loss. For fleets with high windshield replacement
frequency, eliminating the deductible for glass-only claims reduces
administrative friction and out-of-pocket cost per incident.

Captures:
- Full coverage (no deductible) for damage to safety glass caused by a
  comprehensive peril (road debris, vandalism, weather)
- Applies to all scheduled autos with comprehensive coverage under the policy
- The comprehensive deductible continues to apply to non-glass comprehensive
  losses (theft, fire, hail damage to body panels, etc.)

When used:
- Fleet accounts where windshield chips and cracks are a recurring expense
- Accounts in states or regions with high road debris or hail frequency
- Any account that prefers to eliminate the per-occurrence deductible cost
  for the most common type of comprehensive claim

Notes:
- Commonly referred to informally as "zero-deductible glass" coverage
- Some states require insurers to offer this coverage by statute for personal
  auto policies (e.g., Florida); for commercial auto it is optional
- Adding this endorsement will increase comprehensive premium, so the net
  benefit depends on glass claim frequency — high-frequency fleets benefit
  most
- Glass damage from a collision (rather than a comprehensive peril) is a
  collision loss covered under collision coverage, not comprehensive; CA 04 21
  has no effect on collision coverage or its deductible
