CA 20 21 (2013-10) — Snowmobiles
================================
Form number: CA 20 21
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Snowmobiles
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends commercial auto coverage to snowmobiles used in the named insured's
operations. Snowmobiles do not meet the standard ISO definition of "auto"
because they are not designed for use on public roads and are not registered
for highway use; they are also distinct from "mobile equipment." This
endorsement brings scheduled snowmobiles within the CA policy's covered
auto framework.

Captures:
- Liability (BI/PD) coverage for snowmobiles used in the insured's
  operations (on trails, private property, or wherever used)
- Physical damage coverage for scheduled snowmobiles
- May cover employees and authorized operators using the insured's
  snowmobiles in the course of business

When used:
- Resorts, lodges, and recreational outfitters that operate snowmobile fleets
- Utility companies, municipalities, or government agencies using snowmobiles
  for winter access and maintenance in remote areas
- Agricultural operations using snowmobiles for winter farm or range work

Notes:
- Most states require snowmobile registration and operators to carry liability
  coverage; this endorsement facilitates meeting those requirements under the
  commercial auto policy
- Physical damage on snowmobiles may alternatively be handled under a
  recreational vehicle or inland marine floater, which may offer better
  rates or broader coverage for off-road recreational equipment
- Snowmobile coverage under CA is similar to golf cart coverage (CA 04 45)
  and motorcycle coverage — all involve vehicles outside the standard
  "auto" definition that require an endorsement to bring into the CA program
