CA 23 09 (2013-10) — Motor Carriers – Insurance For Non-Trucking Use
====================================================================
Form number: CA 23 09
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Motor Carriers – Insurance For Non-Trucking Use
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Provides liability coverage for an owner-operator's truck when it is being
used for personal, non-commercial purposes — NOT under dispatch or in the
service of the motor carrier. When an owner-operator is under dispatch,
the carrier's primary policy applies; when they use their truck for personal
errands, the carrier's policy is typically excluded and the owner-operator's
personal-use coverage applies. This endorsement (commonly called "bobtail"
or "deadhead" insurance) fills that personal-use gap.

Captures:
- Liability coverage (BI/PD) for the owner-operator's tractor while being
  used for purposes outside of the motor carrier's operations
- Applies when the unit is not under dispatch, not pulling a loaded trailer
  for the carrier, and not generating revenue for the carrier
- Sometimes called "non-trucking use" or "NTU" coverage in the industry

When used:
- Owner-operators who lease to a carrier (the carrier covers them when
  under dispatch; this endorsement covers them between loads)
- Trucking lease programs where the carrier's policy excludes non-dispatch use
- Bobtail situations: tractor traveling without a trailer, traveling home,
  or running personal errands

Notes:
- "Bobtail" technically refers to a tractor running without a trailer;
  "deadhead" refers to running with an empty trailer; CA 23 09 covers
  both scenarios broadly as "non-trucking use"
- This endorsement is often purchased by owner-operators as a stand-alone
  or as part of their own small fleet policy to sit alongside the carrier's
  policy and eliminate the non-dispatch gap
- Without NTU coverage, an owner-operator involved in an accident while
  driving home may have no coverage from either the carrier's policy (not
  under dispatch) or their personal auto policy (a commercial vehicle)
