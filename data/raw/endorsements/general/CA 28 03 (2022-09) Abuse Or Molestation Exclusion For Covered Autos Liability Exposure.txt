CA 28 03 (2022-09) — Abuse Or Molestation Exclusion For Covered Autos Liability Exposure
========================================================================================
Form number: CA 28 03
Edition: 2022-09
Title: Abuse Or Molestation Exclusion For Covered Autos Liability Exposure
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All except FL, NY, TX

Policy term: Matches underlying covered autos liability policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Excludes coverage for bodily injury or property damage arising from abuse
or molestation from the covered autos liability coverage portion of
commercial auto policies. While CA 27 21 through CA 27 24 address the
dealer GL/AEO context (CA 00 25), CA 28 03 operates on the covered autos
liability side — applicable to commercial auto policies broadly (CA 00 01,
CA 00 20, CA 00 25 auto coverages) where the insurer will not underwrite
abuse/molestation liability arising out of covered auto operations.

Captures:
- Exclusion of bodily injury and property damage arising from actual,
  alleged, or threatened abuse or molestation connected to covered autos
- Also excludes negligent hiring, employment, investigation, supervision,
  retention, and failure-to-report claims where the underlying conduct
  falls within the abuse or molestation exclusion

When used:
- Commercial auto programs where the insurer excludes abuse/molestation
  from auto liability as an underwriting position
- Transportation network and passenger transport operations where
  abuse/molestation exposure in covered autos is a concern
- Any commercial auto program issued after 2022-09 where the insurer
  adopts the abuse/molestation exclusion for the auto side

Notes:
- 2022-09 edition form; part of ISO's broader 2022 abuse/molestation
  endorsement suite across commercial lines; replaces the previously
  available CA 25 28 (now withdrawn)
- Distinct from CA 27 21: that form applies to the CA 00 25 dealer GL/AEO
  coverages; CA 28 03 applies to the covered autos liability side
- For dealers, both CA 27 21 and CA 28 03 may be attached — covering
  the GL/AEO exclusion and the auto liability exclusion separately
- Ride-share, transportation, and livery operations should pay particular
  attention to this exclusion as driver/passenger abuse claims are a
  known emerging exposure
