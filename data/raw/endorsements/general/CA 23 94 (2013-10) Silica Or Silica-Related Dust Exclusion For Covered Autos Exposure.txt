CA 23 94 (2013-10) — Silica Or Silica-Related Dust Exclusion For Covered Autos Exposure
=======================================================================================
Form number: CA 23 94
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Silica Or Silica-Related Dust Exclusion For Covered Autos Exposure
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Excludes bodily injury and property damage claims arising from silica dust
or silica-related dust exposure from the commercial auto liability coverage.
Silicosis (lung disease caused by silica dust inhalation) has been the
subject of mass tort litigation; this exclusion prevents the commercial auto
policy from being implicated in silicosis BI claims when the exposure is
connected to auto-related operations (e.g., a truck delivering sand or
cement mix creates a silica dust exposure to workers loading/unloading).

Captures:
- Exclusion of BI claims for silicosis, silica-related disease, or exposure
  to silica or silica-related dust arising from the use of covered autos
- Excludes PD claims related to silica contamination connected to auto use
- Applies to liability under the commercial auto policy (not physical damage)

When used:
- Commercial auto policies for accounts in mining, quarrying, construction,
  cement/concrete distribution, or any industry where silica dust exposure
  is a material risk
- Carriers whose underwriting guidelines exclude silica-related exposure
  across all lines including commercial auto
- Accounts with large truck fleets delivering silica-containing materials
  (sand, gravel, concrete, glass)

Notes:
- Silica exclusions were widely adopted across commercial lines (CGL, WC,
  auto) in the 2000s-2010s following the asbestos litigation experience;
  CA 23 94 is the commercial auto-specific version
- The exclusion targets claims where silica exposure is connected to covered
  auto use; claims unrelated to auto operations are not affected
- Accounts with significant silica exposure typically need specialty
  coverage or riders addressing the silicosis exposure separately from
  standard commercial lines
