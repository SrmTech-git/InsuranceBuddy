CA 05 24 (2020-11) — Non-Ownership Liability Coverage For Volunteers
====================================================================
Form number: CA 05 24
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Non-Ownership Liability Coverage For Volunteers
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends the named insured's non-owned auto liability coverage to include
autos owned by volunteers while those volunteers are performing duties on
behalf of the organization. The standard non-owned auto coverage under
CA 00 01 typically covers employees' personal vehicles used for business;
this endorsement explicitly adds volunteers — who are not employees — to
that coverage, protecting the organization from vicarious liability arising
from volunteer-driver accidents.

Captures:
- Non-owned auto liability coverage for the named insured's vicarious
  liability when a volunteer's personal auto is involved in an accident
  during organizational activities
- Coverage is excess over the volunteer's own personal auto insurance
- Applies to liability (BI/PD) only — not physical damage to the
  volunteer's personal vehicle

When used:
- Nonprofit organizations, churches, civic associations, and volunteer-driven
  service organizations (food banks, hospices, Meals on Wheels programs, etc.)
- Any named insured that relies on volunteers who drive their own vehicles
  to perform work on behalf of the organization
- Complements CA 04 39 (Volunteer Hired Autos) — the two endorsements address
  different aspects of the volunteer vehicle exposure

Notes:
- Without this endorsement, a volunteer-driver accident could expose the
  organization to uncovered vicarious liability — volunteers are not employees
  or partners/members, so the CA 00 01 base non-owned auto provisions do not
  extend insured status to them
- This endorsement covers the ORGANIZATION's liability — not the volunteer's
  personal liability (the volunteer's own PAP remains primary for their
  personal exposure)
- CA 04 39 treats volunteer vehicles as "hired autos" and may also trigger
  physical damage coverage; CA 05 24 is purely a liability extension on
  the non-owned auto side
- Volunteers should maintain their own personal auto insurance; the
  organization's coverage is excess and does not replace the volunteer's PAP
