CA 99 44 (2013-10) — Loss Payable Clause
========================================
Form number: CA 99 44
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Loss Payable Clause
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Adds a loss payable clause to a commercial auto physical damage policy,
directing physical damage claim payments to a named loss payee — typically
a lienholder, lender, or financing institution that has a security interest
in the covered vehicle. Protects the lender's financial interest in the
collateral by ensuring that physical damage insurance proceeds are paid
to the lender (or jointly to lender and insured) rather than only to the
insured borrower.

Captures:
- Payment of physical damage proceeds to a named loss payee with a
  financial interest in the covered vehicle
- Standard loss payee clause: the loss payee's rights are contingent
  on the insured's rights — if the insured's coverage is voided by fraud
  or policy violation, the loss payee's claim is also impaired
- Applies to scheduled vehicles with corresponding loss payee designations

When used:
- Any commercial auto policy where a vehicle is financed and the lender
  requires a loss payable clause
- Fleet vehicles subject to equipment financing or leaseback arrangements
- Standard practice for financed commercial vehicles of all types

Notes:
- CA 99 44 is a standard loss payee clause — the loss payee's rights
  are contingent on the insured's coverage remaining valid; it is NOT
  a lender's loss payable clause (which would protect the lender even
  if the insured's coverage is voided by the insured's own acts)
- Loss payees should be named specifically with correct lender names
  and loan identification; generic "as their interest may appear" language
  may create dispute over which lender receives payment
- Compare with CA 99 61 (Loss Payable Clause – Audio, Visual And Data
  Electronic Equipment Coverage Added Limits) for loss payable on
  electronic equipment coverage specifically
