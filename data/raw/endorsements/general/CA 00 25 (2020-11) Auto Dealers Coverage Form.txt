CA 00 25 (2020-11) — Auto Dealers Coverage Form
================================================
Form number: CA 00 25
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Auto Dealers Coverage Form
Type: ISO Commercial Auto coverage form
States: All

Policy term: 12 months standard
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm endorsement; policy rewrite

Purpose:
ISO coverage form designed specifically for auto dealerships. Unlike CA 00 01,
which must be paired with a separate CGL policy to cover premises and products
liability, CA 00 25 bundles covered autos liability, physical damage, dealer
inventory (open lot), garagekeepers, and a general liability section in a
single form. This integration is necessary because a standard CGL excludes
auto-related liability that is the core exposure for a dealer.

Captures:
- Covered autos liability (BI/PD) for owned, hired, and non-owned autos
- Dealer inventory physical damage: open lot coverage for autos held for
  sale (vehicles owned, consigned, or held under floorplan financing)
- Garagekeepers coverage for customers' autos in the dealer's care, custody,
  or control for service, repair, storage, or safekeeping
- General Liability coverages section (premises BI/PD, products/completed
  operations, personal and advertising injury, medical payments)
- Acts, Errors and Omissions (AEO) liability for wrongful acts in the sale,
  lease, financing, or service of vehicles
- Drive-away operations, false pretense, broad form products (added by CA 25
  and CA 27 series endorsements)

When used:
- New franchise or independent auto dealership account
- Renewal of existing dealer policy
- Accounts selling new or used cars, trucks, motorcycles, or RVs
- Does NOT apply to service-only garages without a dealer sales operation
  (use CA 00 01 + CGL for repair shops without inventory)

Notes:
- The CA 27 series endorsements are new forms added in the 2020-11 and
  2022-09 revision cycles (drone exclusions, cannabis exclusions, cross-suit
  exclusion, LLC auto status, abuse/molestation); they do not supersede the
  CA 25 series — both coexist
- Open lot coverage is physical damage on dealer-owned inventory; it differs
  from garagekeepers (which covers customer vehicles)
- AEO coverage is a significant feature: covers the dealer for wrongful acts
  in the sales/F&I process — e.g., misrepresentation, failure to disclose,
  improper financing disclosures
- Floorplan lenders are typically named as loss payees on the open lot
  physical damage section
- CA DS 26 is the companion declarations form for this coverage form
