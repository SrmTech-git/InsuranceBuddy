CA 27 13 (2019-12) — Cannabis Exclusion With Hemp Exception For General Liability Coverages
============================================================================================
Form number: CA 27 13
Edition: 2019-12
Title: Cannabis Exclusion With Hemp Exception For General Liability Coverages
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All (multi-state; part of ISO's December 2019 cannabis exclusion filing)

Policy term: Matches underlying CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Adds a cannabis exclusion to the General Liability coverages (Coverage A
and Coverage B) of the CA 00 25, with a carve-out preserving coverage
for hemp-derived goods. Part of the ISO December 2019 cannabis exclusion
suite rolled out across all commercial lines.

Captures:
- Exclusion of bodily injury, property damage, and personal and
  advertising injury arising from the design, cultivation, manufacture,
  storage, processing, packaging, handling, testing, distribution, sale,
  serving, furnishing, possession, or disposal of cannabis
- Exclusion of claims from inhalation, ingestion, absorption, or contact
  with cannabis (actual, alleged, or suspected)
- Hemp exception: the exclusion does NOT apply to goods containing or
  derived from hemp (seeds, food, clothing, lotions, oils, extracts,
  building materials, paper)

When used:
- Dealers or insurers seeking to exclude cannabis-related GL claims while
  preserving coverage for hemp-derived products
- The standard cannabis exclusion option when hemp exposure is acceptable

Notes:
- The hemp exception is voided if hemp goods are prohibited under
  applicable state or local law where the injury or damage occurs
- CA 27 14 is the alternative that adds a second exception for dealers
  acting in a lessor capacity (leasing to cannabis tenants)
- Filed December 2019 — separate from the November 2020 CA 27 revision
  cycle; note that the file was originally mislabeled with a 2020-11
  edition date
