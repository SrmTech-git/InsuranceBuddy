CA 99 38 (1987-01) — Split Liability Limits – Garages
=====================================================
Form number: CA 99 38
Edition: 1987-01
Title: Split Liability Limits – Garages
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Converts the combined single limit (CSL) liability coverage under the
CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form to split limits — separate per-person,
per-occurrence bodily injury limits and a per-occurrence property damage
limit. The CA 00 25 uses CSL by default; this endorsement provides the
split-limit structure for dealers (and other garage operations) where split
limits are required by the state, preferred by the insured, or required
by a financing arrangement.

Captures:
- Separate per-person bodily injury liability limit
- Separate per-occurrence bodily injury liability limit
- Separate per-occurrence property damage liability limit
- Applies to the covered autos liability and GL liability coverages
  under the CA 00 25

When used:
- Auto dealers in states requiring or accepting split limits
- Dealer programs where split limits are specified by franchise agreement,
  floor plan financing requirements, or other contractual obligations
- Any CA 00 25 program where CSL needs to be restructured as split limits

Notes:
- 1987-01 edition — this is one of the oldest forms in the CA 99 series
  still in active use; the split vs. CSL structure question predates
  modern commercial auto program revisions
- The garage-specific nature of this form (vs. CA 99 27 for general
  commercial auto) addresses the CA 00 25's combined liability structure
  which includes both auto and GL coverages
- Franchise dealer agreements may specify minimum split limits;
  confirm compliance when attaching this endorsement
