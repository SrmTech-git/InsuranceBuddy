CA 23 12 (2013-10) — Motor Carriers – Named Lessee As Insured
=============================================================
Form number: CA 23 12
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Motor Carriers – Named Lessee As Insured
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Adds the motor carrier (lessee) to the owner-operator's commercial auto
policy as an additional insured. In owner-operator lease arrangements,
the truck is owned by the owner-operator but leased to a carrier; both
parties can face liability when the truck is involved in an accident while
under the carrier's dispatch. This endorsement ensures the carrier has
insured status on the owner-operator's underlying policy, which may be
required by federal regulations or lease terms.

Captures:
- Motor carrier (lessee) added as a named insured on the owner-operator's
  CA 00 20 or CA 00 01 policy
- Coverage for the carrier as an insured for liability arising from the
  leased vehicle's operation under the carrier's authority
- Coverage applies for the duration of the lease period, not limited to
  periods when the owner-operator is under the carrier's active dispatch

When used:
- Owner-operator insurance programs where the leasing carrier requires
  insured status on the owner-operator's policy
- Situations where federal or state regulations require the carrier to be
  covered as an insured on owner-operated equipment
- Lease programs pairing CA 23 12 (carrier as insured on owner's policy)
  with CA 23 08 (carrier's excess layer above owner's primary)

Notes:
- Federal motor carrier regulations (49 CFR Part 376) create complex
  liability allocation between carriers and owner-operators; this endorsement
  helps structure the policy to reflect those regulatory requirements
- The carrier being named as insured on the owner-operator's policy does
  not eliminate the carrier's need for its own CA 00 20; it provides an
  additional layer of coverage in the event the owner-operator's policy
  is called upon
- FMCSA Form MCS-90 is typically attached to the carrier's own policy,
  not the owner-operator's policy
