CA 20 55 (2013-10) — Fellow Employee Coverage
=============================================
Form number: CA 20 55
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Fellow Employee Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Removes the fellow employee exclusion from the CA policy's liability section,
allowing coverage for bodily injury claims brought by one employee against
another employee for auto-related injuries. The base CA 00 01 excludes BI
to a fellow employee injured by an insured employee's negligent driving
(those claims are typically handled under workers' compensation); this
endorsement provides the named insured's CA policy as a defense and
indemnification source for such employee-vs-employee auto claims.

Captures:
- Removal of the fellow employee exclusion for bodily injury claims
- Provides CA liability coverage when an injured employee sues a fellow
  employee-driver for auto negligence
- Applies blanket to all employees of the named insured

When used:
- Accounts where employees have successfully sued coworkers for auto-related
  injuries and the employer wants the CA policy to respond
- States where workers' compensation exclusive remedy does not bar auto
  negligence suits between coworkers (depends on state law)
- High-fleet accounts with significant employee-to-employee driving exposure
  (delivery companies, rideshare-adjacent operations)

Notes:
- Workers' compensation generally covers employee injury, but many states
  allow an injured employee to sue a negligent coworker in tort (outside
  of WC exclusive remedy) — in those cases the CA policy is implicated
- CA 20 55 provides blanket coverage for all employees; CA 20 56 provides
  the same coverage but only for specifically designated employees or
  positions — use CA 20 56 when broader coverage is not appropriate for all
  employees
- The employer's own BI coverage under the CA policy does not change;
  this endorsement protects the named insured from being dragged into
  employee-vs-employee lawsuits by ensuring the CA policy covers the
  coworker-defendant
