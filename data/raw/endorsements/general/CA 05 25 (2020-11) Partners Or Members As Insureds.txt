CA 05 25 (2020-11) — Partners Or Members As Insureds
====================================================
Form number: CA 05 25
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Partners Or Members As Insureds
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends the definition of "insured" under a CA 00 01 policy to include
individual partners of a partnership or individual members of a limited
liability company (LLC) when they are using a covered auto in connection
with the named insured's business. The base CA 00 01 insured definition
may not automatically extend to partners/members in their individual capacity
depending on how the named insured entity is structured; this endorsement
eliminates that ambiguity.

Captures:
- Individual partners of a partnership or individual members of an LLC
  qualify as insureds while using a covered auto for the named insured's
  business
- Extends liability protection to these individuals for claims arising from
  their use of covered autos in their capacity as partners or members

When used:
- Partnerships (general partnerships, limited partnerships) where individual
  partners drive company vehicles and need explicit insured status
- LLCs where individual members operate company-owned autos and the insured
  definition under CA 00 01 may leave a gap for member-individuals
- Any account where the entity structure raises questions about whether
  non-employee owners have coverage as insureds under the base policy

Notes:
- The CA 00 01 insured definition extends to "you" (the named insured) and
  anyone else using a covered auto with permission; for corporate entities
  this is reasonably clear, but for partnerships and LLCs the extension to
  individual partners/members is less certain without this endorsement
- Does not affect coverage for employees or other permissive users — those
  are already covered under the base form
- Partners or members using their own personal vehicles (not covered autos)
  for business remain subject to the non-owned auto coverage provisions,
  not this endorsement
