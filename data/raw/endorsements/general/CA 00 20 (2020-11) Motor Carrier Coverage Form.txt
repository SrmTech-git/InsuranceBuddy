CA 00 20 (2020-11) — Motor Carrier Coverage Form
=================================================
Form number: CA 00 20
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Motor Carrier Coverage Form
Type: ISO Commercial Auto coverage form
States: All

Policy term: 12 months standard; other terms available
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm endorsement; policy rewrite

Purpose:
ISO coverage form designed for motor carriers — trucking companies, common
carriers, and contract carriers subject to FMCSA (federal) or state public
liability filing requirements. Parallels CA 00 01 in structure but adds
trucking-specific provisions: trailer interchange, leased unit treatment,
named lessee insured status, and compatibility with the MCS-90 endorsement
required for federally regulated for-hire carriers.

Captures:
- Liability coverage (BI/PD) for owned, hired, and non-owned autos on a
  symbol-driven framework (Symbols 1–9 plus motor-carrier-specific symbols)
- Physical damage: comprehensive, collision, specified causes of loss
- Trailer interchange coverage (fire and theft, or all-cause, via CA 23 13
  or CA 23 98) for non-owned trailers under interchange agreements
- Leased-unit provisions: named lessee as insured (CA 23 12), excess
  coverage for named lessors (CA 23 08)
- Named insured as both owner and operator; recognizes lease structures
  common in trucking

When used:
- New or renewal policy for a for-hire motor carrier subject to FMCSA
  authority (common carrier, contract carrier)
- Trucking risks where trailer interchange is material
- Any account requiring an MCS-90 endorsement (federal filing) — MCS-90
  is attached separately but is designed to work with CA 00 20
- Private carriers operating entirely intrastate may use CA 00 01 instead

Notes:
- FMCSA requires Form MCS-90 for for-hire carriers in interstate commerce;
  CA 00 20 is the policy form that MCS-90 is attached to
- MCS-90 provides absolute liability up to federal minimums ($750K general
  freight, $5M hazmat) regardless of policy exclusions — the most significant
  difference from a standard CA 00 01 account
- Motor Carrier form replaced the older Truckers form (CA 00 12) after the
  2010 multistate revision; CA P 005 advisory notice describes the transition
- CA 23 series endorsements are specifically designed for motor carrier
  exposures (rolling stores, wrong delivery, UIIE, public livery exclusion)
- 2020-11 edition added public/livery and on-demand delivery exclusions
  (CA 23 44, CA 23 45) to address TNC/gig-economy exposures
