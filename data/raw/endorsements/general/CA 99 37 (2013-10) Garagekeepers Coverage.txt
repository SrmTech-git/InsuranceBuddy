CA 99 37 (2013-10) — Garagekeepers Coverage
===========================================
Form number: CA 99 37
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Garagekeepers Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy (CA 00 01)
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Adds garagekeepers coverage to a CA 00 01 Business Auto Coverage Form
for auto service operations. The CA 00 01 does not include garagekeepers
coverage; it must be added by endorsement. Garagekeepers coverage protects
auto repair shops, service stations, parking operations, and similar
businesses against loss or damage to customers' vehicles while in the
insured's care, custody, or control. Without this coverage, these
businesses have a significant gap in their property damage liability.

Captures:
- Physical damage to customers' vehicles while in the insured's care,
  custody, or control (for service, storage, or repair)
- Coverage options: legal liability (only when insured is legally liable),
  direct primary (regardless of fault), or direct excess (excess over
  customer's own insurance)
- Applies to autos left in the insured's custody for service, repair,
  storage, or safekeeping

When used:
- Auto repair shops, body shops, service stations, oil change operations
- Parking garages, valet parking services
- Car washes with customer vehicle storage
- Any business that takes custody of customers' autos as part of operations
  and uses the CA 00 01 as its base commercial auto form

Notes:
- The CA 00 25 Auto Dealers Coverage Form includes garagekeepers as a
  built-in coverage option; CA 99 37 is for non-dealer service operations
  using CA 00 01
- The coverage basis (legal liability vs. direct) significantly affects
  when and how claims are paid; direct primary is the broadest and most
  customer-friendly option
- Per-vehicle and per-occurrence limits must be adequate for the
  highest-value customer vehicles the business handles
