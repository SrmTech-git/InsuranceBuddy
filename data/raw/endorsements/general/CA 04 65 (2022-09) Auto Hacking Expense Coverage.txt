CA 04 65 (2022-09) — Auto Hacking Expense Coverage
==================================================
Form number: CA 04 65
Edition: 2022-09
Title: Auto Hacking Expense Coverage
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Covers expenses incurred to respond to and recover from a cyber attack or
unauthorized electronic access that takes control of, disables, or compromises
the electronic systems of a covered auto. Added in the 2022-09 revision as
connected and semi-autonomous vehicles became more prevalent in commercial
fleets. The base CA 00 01 (2020-11 edition) added a cyber exclusion for auto-
hacking losses; this endorsement buys back the coverage for the response
expense portion.

Captures:
- Diagnostic costs to determine the extent of a cyber compromise of a
  covered auto's electronic systems
- Costs to repair, reprogram, or replace compromised electronic components,
  software, or firmware
- Rental or substitute transportation costs while the compromised auto
  is being repaired
- Does not cover liability claims arising from a hack that causes an
  accident (that remains subject to the cyber exclusion in the base form)

When used:
- Fleet operators with connected vehicles (telematics-equipped trucks,
  internet-connected passenger vehicles, semi-autonomous commercial vehicles)
- Any account where electronic system integrity is critical to safe operation
- High-value or specialty vehicles with significant embedded software
  (electric vehicles, autonomous delivery vehicles)

Notes:
- Eligible vehicles are limited to private passenger types and light/medium
  trucks — not all commercial vehicles qualify; verify vehicle type before
  attaching
- The 2020-11 edition of CA 00 01 introduced a cyber/hacking exclusion;
  without CA 04 65, a vehicle compromised by a hacker has no coverage for
  the cost to diagnose and restore the electronic systems
- This endorsement covers the insured's OWN expense to restore the vehicle;
  it does not cover third-party liability if a hacked vehicle causes an
  accident — that liability exposure may require a separate cyber liability
  policy
- CA P 013 (advisory notice) was issued in 2022 to explain the new abuse/
  molestation endorsements and this hacking coverage endorsement to policyholders
- Coverage is expected to grow in relevance as commercial fleets adopt more
  connected and autonomous technology
