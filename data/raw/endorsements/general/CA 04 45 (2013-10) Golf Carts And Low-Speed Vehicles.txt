CA 04 45 (2013-10) — Golf Carts And Low-Speed Vehicles
======================================================
Form number: CA 04 45
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Golf Carts And Low-Speed Vehicles
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Extends commercial auto coverage to golf carts and other low-speed vehicles
used in the named insured's operations. These vehicles typically fall outside
the standard definition of "auto" in CA 00 01 because they are not licensed
for road use and may not meet the definition of a land motor vehicle; this
endorsement explicitly brings them within covered auto status. "Low-speed
vehicle" relies on the applicable state or federal LSV classification (federal
definition caps top speed at 20–25 mph) rather than a speed threshold defined
within the endorsement itself.

Captures:
- Liability (BI/PD) coverage for golf carts and low-speed vehicles used in
  the insured's operations
- Physical damage coverage where elected
- Vehicles may be scheduled or covered on a blanket basis depending on
  endorsement structure

When used:
- Golf courses, resorts, retirement communities, and campgrounds that own
  and operate fleets of golf carts
- Industrial facilities, warehouses, and large campuses using low-speed
  utility vehicles or personal mobility devices
- Any business where golf carts, NEVs (neighborhood electric vehicles), or
  similar low-speed equipment are a meaningful operational asset

Notes:
- Without this endorsement, golf carts and LSVs may be uninsured under the
  commercial auto policy — they may also be excluded from general liability
  as "mobile equipment" once they are used on public roads
- Some states have separate licensing and registration requirements for
  low-speed vehicles (NEVs), which may affect how they are treated under
  state financial responsibility laws
- Coverage for golf carts used only on private property (never on public
  roads) may alternatively be handled under a property or inland marine
  floater; consult coverage territory and use limitations
