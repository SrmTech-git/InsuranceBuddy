CA 20 70 (2013-10) — Coverage For Certain Operations In Connection With Railroads
=================================================================================
Form number: CA 20 70
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Coverage For Certain Operations In Connection With Railroads
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Modifies or removes the railroad protective liability exclusion in the CA
00 01 policy for insureds whose vehicles and operations take place in, on,
or adjacent to railroad rights-of-way. Standard commercial lines policies
(both CA and CGL) exclude liability for damage to railroad property or
claims arising from railroad operations; contractors and vendors working
near railroads need this exclusion modified to obtain meaningful coverage.

Captures:
- Removal or modification of the railroad exclusion for specifically
  scheduled operations conducted in connection with railroads
- Liability coverage for BI and PD arising from covered auto use near or
  on railroad rights-of-way during the described operations

When used:
- Infrastructure contractors whose vehicles operate in railroad corridors
  (utility contractors, road crews near crossings, telecom contractors
  with equipment crossing rail lines)
- Railroad vendors and service providers whose vehicles must enter
  railroad property
- Any account whose scope of work contractually requires auto coverage
  in railroad environments

Notes:
- Railroad companies typically require separate Railroad Protective Liability
  (RPL) policies from contractors working near tracks; CA 20 70 modifies
  the contractor's own CA policy but does NOT substitute for an RPL policy
  that the railroad itself may require as a named insured
- The scope of operations covered by this endorsement should be clearly
  described — only the scheduled operations have the exclusion removed
- Underwriters price railroad exposure carefully due to the catastrophic
  loss potential; this endorsement may require special underwriting
  approval and higher premiums
