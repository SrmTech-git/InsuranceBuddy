CA 26 02 (2020-11) — Single Interest Automobile Physical Damage Insurance Policy (Finance Master Policy Form)
=============================================================================================================
Form number: CA 26 02
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Single Interest Automobile Physical Damage Insurance Policy (Finance Master Policy Form)
Type: ISO Commercial Auto coverage form
States: All

Policy term: Ongoing; individual loans attach and terminate as originated and paid off
Transaction types: New business; ongoing master program enrollment

Purpose:
The master program form for single-interest automobile physical damage
insurance, covering a lender's entire portfolio of auto loans under a single
master policy. Rather than issuing individual policies per borrower (CA 26 01),
a master program covers all qualifying loans in the lender's portfolio that
lack required borrower-maintained insurance. Administratively efficient for
lenders with large loan portfolios.

Captures:
- Physical damage coverage for the lender's interest across all enrolled
  loans in the portfolio
- Coverage automatically applies to loans where the borrower's own insurance
  has lapsed or been cancelled
- Tracking and monitoring system for borrower insurance compliance integrated
  into the program
- Claims paid to the lender for outstanding loan balances on total-loss or
  damaged vehicles

When used:
- Banks, credit unions, and auto finance companies with large auto loan
  portfolios needing a blanket CPI program
- Any lender that wants systematic insurance compliance monitoring and
  automatic force-placement when borrowers lapse
- Portfolio-level coverage rather than loan-by-loan individual policies

Notes:
- CA 26 02 (master form) is used with CA 26 03 (declarations), CA 26 04
  (additional coverage), CA 26 05 (additional property), CA 26 06 (blanket
  loan payment), and CA 26 07 (involuntary unemployment) — the CA 26 series
  is an integrated set of forms for lender CPI programs
- Master programs are administered by insurance companies specializing in
  lender-placed insurance; they are not typically offered through standard
  commercial auto brokers
- Individual borrowers are notified before force-placement per federal
  and state lending regulations
