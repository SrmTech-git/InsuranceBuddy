CA 20 01 (2020-11) — Lessor – Additional Insured And Loss Payee
===============================================================
Form number: CA 20 01
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Lessor – Additional Insured And Loss Payee
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Adds the lessor of a leased vehicle as an additional insured for liability
and as a loss payee for physical damage on the insured's commercial auto
policy. When a company leases vehicles from a fleet leasing company or
financial institution, the lessor requires both liability AI status (in
case they are named in a suit) and loss payee status (to protect their
financial interest in the physical asset).

Captures:
- Named lessor added as additional insured for covered autos liability
  (BI/PD arising from the use of the leased vehicle)
- Named lessor added as loss payee for physical damage coverage on the
  leased vehicle(s)
- Schedule of leased autos and the associated lessor information

When used:
- Fleet leases where a leasing company (e.g., Enterprise Fleet Management,
  ARI, LeasePlan) provides vehicles and requires AI/loss payee status
- Financial institution vehicle leases where the lienholder or lessor
  demands policy evidence of their interests
- Any long-term commercial vehicle lease with a third-party owner

Notes:
- Differs from a simple loss payee endorsement: CA 20 01 provides both
  liability AI status AND physical damage loss payee status to the lessor,
  reflecting the lessor's dual exposure (liability as vehicle owner, and
  financial interest in the physical asset)
- The lessor is protected only for claims arising from the lessee's use
  of the covered leased autos — not for the lessor's own negligence
- CA 20 08 is the parallel form designed for use on the LEASING COMPANY'S
  own policy (the lessor insures the fleet and adds lessees); CA 20 01
  is used on the LESSEE'S policy (the named insured is the company using
  the leased vehicles)
