CA 23 86 (2013-10) — Exclusion Of Terrorism Above Minimum Statutory Limits
==========================================================================
Form number: CA 23 86
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Exclusion Of Terrorism Above Minimum Statutory Limits
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Partially excludes terrorism coverage by capping the terrorism liability at
state-mandated minimum liability limits, excluding terrorism losses above
those minimums. This is a hybrid approach: the policy still responds to
terrorism claims up to the state financial responsibility minimum (ensuring
compliance with state liability laws), but excess terrorism exposure above
the minimum is excluded.

Captures:
- Terrorism liability coverage limited to state-mandated financial
  responsibility minimums only (e.g., the statutory minimum BI and PD limits)
- Exclusion of terrorism coverage above those minimums
- The policy still responds at the minimum level, satisfying state auto
  insurance laws

When used:
- States where auto financial responsibility laws require minimum liability
  coverage even for excluded perils, and the carrier needs to satisfy the
  state minimum while excluding excess terrorism exposure
- Commercial auto programs where the insurer provides minimum statutory
  coverage but does not want to extend full policy limits to terrorism

Notes:
- The "above minimum statutory limits" structure satisfies state mandatory
  auto insurance requirements while limiting the carrier's terrorism exposure
- CA 23 84 is the full terrorism exclusion (no coverage); CA 23 86 is the
  partial exclusion (minimum coverage only)
- CA 23 87 is the NBC version of this partial exclusion for nuclear,
  biological, or chemical terrorism specifically
