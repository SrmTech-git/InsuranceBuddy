CA 21 46 (1993-12) — Split Underinsured Motorists Coverage Limits
=================================================================
Form number: CA 21 46
Edition: 1993-12
Title: Split Underinsured Motorists Coverage Limits
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Establishes split per-person/per-accident limits for underinsured motorist
(UIM) coverage that includes both bodily injury and property damage on a
commercial auto policy. UIM coverage applies when the at-fault driver has
insufficient liability insurance to fully compensate the injured party;
this endorsement structures that coverage with split limits.

Captures:
- UIM coverage (BI and PD) in split per-person / per-accident limit format
- Applies when the at-fault driver's liability limits are exhausted and
  the damages exceed those limits, up to the insured's UIM split limits

When used:
- Commercial auto policies in states where UIM covers both BI and PD and
  split limits are available
- Accounts that prefer split-limit UIM to match their liability structure

Notes:
- Mirrors CA 21 07 (UM) for underinsured motorist coverage
- CA 21 51 is the BI-only UIM split-limit form; CA 21 46 covers both BI and PD
- UIM triggers differently from UM: UM requires the tortfeasor to have
  NO insurance; UIM requires the tortfeasor to have SOME insurance but
  insufficient to cover all damages
- In some states commercial vehicles may reject or limit UIM coverage;
  verify state statute requirements
- This is an older edition (1993); some states may have more current
  state-specific UIM forms that supersede this; verify state filing
  requirements before using
