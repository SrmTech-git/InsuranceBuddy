CA 23 84 (2013-10) — Exclusion Of Terrorism
===========================================
Form number: CA 23 84
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Exclusion Of Terrorism
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Excludes coverage for losses arising from any act of terrorism as defined
by TRIA and applicable ISO definitions. TRIA requires insurers to offer
terrorism coverage; this endorsement is the exclusion instrument that
removes terrorism-related claims from the CA policy. It is not a
declination document — it is the policy form itself that effects the
exclusion once the named insured has declined the offer.

Captures:
- Exclusion of all BI, PD, and physical damage claims arising from a
  certified act of terrorism under TRIA
- A "certified act of terrorism" under TRIA requires a determination by
  the Secretary of the Treasury; losses must exceed the program's
  aggregate industry loss threshold (currently $200 million) before TRIA
  certification applies

When used:
- Any commercial auto account where the named insured has declined the
  insurer's TRIA terrorism coverage offer
- Most large commercial auto renewals where terrorism is separately priced
  and offered under TRIA requirements; if declined, this endorsement
  attaches

Notes:
- Under TRIA (Terrorism Risk Insurance Act), insurers must OFFER terrorism
  coverage to commercial policyholders; if the insured declines, the
  exclusion endorsement must attach
- CA 23 85 excludes specifically nuclear, biological, or chemical (NBC)
  terrorism — a distinct category with different pricing and availability
- CA 23 86 and CA 23 87 are partial-exclusion forms that exclude terrorism
  coverage ABOVE state-mandated minimum liability limits, allowing the
  policy to still respond at the minimum-required level while excluding
  excess terrorism exposure
- Terrorism coverage pricing has decreased significantly since 9/11 as
  the reinsurance market matured; many insureds now accept terrorism coverage
