CA 99 90 (2013-10) — Optional Limits – Loss Of Use Expenses
===========================================================
Form number: CA 99 90
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Optional Limits – Loss Of Use Expenses
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Provides optional increased limits for loss-of-use expenses when a
covered auto rented from others (hired auto) is damaged and the rental
company charges for the period the vehicle is out of service for repair.
The standard commercial auto form typically includes a low default limit
for loss-of-use charges that a rental or leasing company imposes on the
insured following damage to a rented vehicle. This endorsement allows the
insured to select higher limits for these charges, which can be substantial
for commercial-class rental vehicles.

Captures:
- Increased limits for loss-of-use charges imposed by rental/leasing
  companies when a hired auto is damaged and out of service
- Covers the daily rental company's revenue loss claim for the period
  the vehicle is being repaired
- Elected limit applies per occurrence for hired auto loss-of-use charges

When used:
- Any commercial auto program where the insured frequently rents
  commercial vehicles (trucks, vans, specialty equipment)
- Businesses that rent high-value or high-revenue commercial vehicles
  where loss-of-use charges can be substantial
- Programs where the default loss-of-use limit is insufficient for
  the actual rental class being used

Notes:
- Compare with CA 99 23 (Rental Reimbursement Coverage), which covers
  the insured's cost to rent a substitute vehicle; CA 99 90 covers
  the rental company's loss-of-use charges imposed on the insured
  when a rented vehicle is damaged — these are different exposures
- The base CA 00 01 physical damage coverage extension for loss of use
  provides $20 per day with a $600 maximum; CA 99 90 allows purchasing
  higher per-day and aggregate limits beyond those base amounts
- Commercial truck and equipment rental companies charge significant
  daily rates for loss of use; the $20/$600 base limit is often
  exhausted quickly for commercial-class vehicles
- Review what the rental agreement specifies for loss-of-use liability
  to determine whether the CA 99 90 limit is adequate
