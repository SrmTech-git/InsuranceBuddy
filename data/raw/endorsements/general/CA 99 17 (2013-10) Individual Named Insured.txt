CA 99 17 (2013-10) — Individual Named Insured
=============================================
Form number: CA 99 17
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Individual Named Insured
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying commercial auto policy
Transaction types: New business; endorsement addition

Purpose:
Modifies a commercial auto policy when the named insured is an individual
person rather than a business entity. The standard commercial auto forms
(CA 00 01, CA 00 20, CA 00 25) are designed for business organizations;
when an individual is the named insured, certain coverage extensions and
definitions need adjustment to provide appropriate personal coverage for
the individual and household family members. This endorsement makes those
modifications — particularly expanding the definition of insured to include
family members and providing personal auto-equivalent protections.

Captures:
- Expansion of insured status to include the named individual's family
  members for covered auto liability and medical payments coverage
- Modifications to bring commercial auto coverage in line with personal
  auto policy protections when the insured is an individual
- May include uninsured motorists and other coverages applicable to
  individual insureds

When used:
- Any individual named insured on a commercial auto policy (most commonly
  sole proprietors, but also individuals who are named insureds on any CA form)
- ISO rules mandate attaching CA 99 17 at no additional premium whenever a
  private passenger auto or light truck is covered on a policy insuring an
  individual person
- Any commercial auto policy where family member insured status is needed

Notes:
- Without this endorsement, the named individual's spouse and family members
  may not have insured status under the commercial auto policy — a gap
  that this form closes
- The endorsement bridges the coverage gap between commercial auto forms
  (designed for businesses) and personal auto forms (designed for individuals)
- Applies to any individual named insured, not only sole proprietors;
  "sole proprietors" is the most common case but not the only one
