CA 20 27 (2013-10) — Registration Plates Not Issued For A Specific Auto
=======================================================================
Form number: CA 20 27
Edition: 2013-10
Title: Registration Plates Not Issued For A Specific Auto
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Addresses the coverage question that arises when registration plates are
not permanently assigned to a single vehicle — such as dealer license plates,
in-transit plates, or fleet plates that move between vehicles. Standard CA
policy language ties coverage to specific identified autos; this endorsement
clarifies that covered auto status applies to any vehicle bearing the
scheduled registration plates, regardless of which specific vehicle is
using the plate at a given time.

Captures:
- Coverage for any vehicle using the named insured's dealer plates,
  in-transit plates, or other non-vehicle-specific registration plates
- Liability and physical damage coverage follow the plate rather than
  requiring a specific VIN to be scheduled

When used:
- Auto dealers that use dealer plates on demo vehicles, test drives, and
  vehicles in transit
- Businesses that use fleet or in-transit plates across multiple interchangeable
  vehicles
- Any account where plate assignment is not vehicle-specific and coverage
  needs to follow plate use

Notes:
- Without this endorsement, a CA policy scheduled to specific VINs might
  not respond to a loss involving a dealer plate on an unlisted vehicle
- Dealer plate coverage is a routine need for any dealership; this
  endorsement is commonly paired with a dealer's CA 00 01 or CA 00 25 policy
- Coverage limits and conditions still apply normally; only the vehicle
  identification method changes (plate-based vs. VIN-based)
