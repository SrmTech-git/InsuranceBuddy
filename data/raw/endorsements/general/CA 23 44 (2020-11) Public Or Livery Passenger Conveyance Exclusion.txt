CA 23 44 (2020-11) — Public Or Livery Passenger Conveyance Exclusion
====================================================================
Form number: CA 23 44
Edition: 2020-11
Title: Public Or Livery Passenger Conveyance Exclusion
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm add

Purpose:
Explicitly excludes coverage for vehicles used as public or livery passenger
conveyances — taxis, limousines, and vehicles operating under a transportation
network company (TNC) arrangement such as Uber or Lyft. The 2020-11 edition
of CA 00 20 added this exclusion to prevent motor carrier policies from
inadvertently covering for-hire passenger transport exposures that are priced
and regulated differently.

Captures:
- Exclusion of liability and physical damage coverage while a covered auto
  is being used to carry passengers for hire as a taxi, livery, or TNC vehicle
- Applies when the vehicle is dispatched for passenger transport as a
  commercial-for-hire service

When used:
- Motor carrier and commercial auto policies where the underwriter wants
  to confirm that for-hire passenger transport is not covered
- Any CA 00 20 policy where there is concern about drivers using covered
  autos for rideshare or taxi services on the side
- Standard language confirming the CA policy does not extend to TNC or
  taxi exposure

Notes:
- TNC/rideshare coverage (Uber, Lyft, etc.) requires specific TNC endorsements
  that are typically issued at the TNC platform level; the standard commercial
  auto policy is not designed to cover this exposure
- CA 23 45 is a broader version that excludes BOTH livery passenger conveyance
  AND on-demand delivery services (gig delivery like DoorDash, Amazon Flex)
- Carriers writing large fleet accounts should confirm drivers are not
  enrolled in TNC platforms using covered vehicles — this exclusion makes
  clear the policy does not cover those activities
