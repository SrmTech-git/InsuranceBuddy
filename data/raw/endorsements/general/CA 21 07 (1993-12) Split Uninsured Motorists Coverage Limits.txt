CA 21 07 (1993-12) — Split Uninsured Motorists Coverage Limits
==============================================================
Form number: CA 21 07
Edition: 1993-12
Title: Split Uninsured Motorists Coverage Limits
Type: ISO Commercial Auto endorsement
States: All

Policy term: Matches underlying policy
Transaction types: New business; renewal; midterm change

Purpose:
Establishes split per-person/per-accident limits for uninsured motorist (UM)
coverage that includes both bodily injury (BI) and property damage (PD) on
a commercial auto policy. Used in states where UM coverage extends to
property damage (in addition to bodily injury) and the insured wants split
limits rather than a combined single limit.

Captures:
- UM coverage (BI and PD) structured as split per-person / per-accident
  limits
- Both bodily injury and property damage claims from uninsured motorists
  are subject to the scheduled limits

When used:
- Commercial auto policies in states where UM covers both BI and PD and
  split limits are permitted
- Accounts that want to match their liability split-limit structure for UM

Notes:
- CA 21 07 covers BOTH BI and PD; CA 21 02 covers BI ONLY — select the
  appropriate form based on the state's UM statute
- This is an older edition (1993); some states may have more current
  state-specific UM forms that supersede this; verify state filing
  requirements before using
- CA 21 46 and CA 21 51 are the UIM (underinsured motorist) equivalents
