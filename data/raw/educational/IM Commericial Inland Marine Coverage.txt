# Commercial Inland Marine - Coverage Overview
LOB: Inland Marine (IM)
Type: Commercial
Source: Industry Training Material

## WHAT IT IS:
Commercial Inland Marine is an "a la carte" insurance product. No coverage is included simply by having 
a policy — coverages must be individually selected. Each selected Coverage Part includes some coverage 
by default, but individual items can be added, upgraded, limited, or excluded depending on the coverage 
part chosen.

## HOW IT WORKS:
Commercial Inland Marine is structured around Coverage Parts — each one designed to cover a specific 
type of property or business exposure. There are 36 coverage parts available. A customer selects one or 
more Coverage Parts based on their business needs and exposures. Customization options vary by 
coverage part.

## COVERAGE PARTS — EXAMPLES BY INDUSTRY:
- Art and Entertainment: Theatrical Property, Musical Instruments, Fine Arts
- Construction: Builder's Risk, Contractor's Equipment, Installation Floater
- Other: Golf Course, Physicians' and Dentists' Equipment

Some coverage parts have multiple variations available. Selection depends on the customer's business 
needs and the specific exposures they have.

## FORM STRUCTURE:
Inland Marine forms typically separate legal verbiage (coverage details) from schedules of items 
(where limits and/or covered locations/buildings are listed). When a coverage part is added to a policy, 
this most often triggers two forms to be included — one for legal verbiage and one for the schedule.

## DECLARATIONS (DEC) vs QUOTE:
- The Dec and Quote use very similar layouts
- The Dec will most often reference a form number (e.g. "Refer to IM 99 99") rather than displaying 
  the limit directly
- The Quote page generally shows limits and deductibles
- In some cases where the schedule form lacks legal verbiage implying coverage, the Quote will also 
  say "Refer to IM 99 99"

## WHAT IT COVERS:
Coverage varies entirely by Coverage Part selected. Examples include:
- Musical instruments and fine arts
- Theatrical property
- Contractor's equipment
- Builder's risk during construction
- Installation floaters
- Golf course property
- Medical and dental equipment

## WHAT IT DOESN'T COVER:
- No coverage is included by default — all coverage must be affirmatively selected
- Each Coverage Part has its own exclusions and limitations
- Customization options and available additions vary by Coverage Part

## RELATED COVERAGES:
- Commercial General Liability (CGL) — often paired with IM on a commercial account
- Businessowners Policy (BOP) — may include some inland marine type coverage for smaller risks
- Commercial Package Policy (CPP) — IM is commonly a component of a CPP

## KEY TERMS:
- Coverage Part: The individual unit of coverage within an IM policy, each covering a specific 
  property type or exposure
- Schedule: The form listing specific items, limits, and/or locations covered
- A La Carte: No coverage is automatic — each Coverage Part must be selected individually
- Floater: A type of inland marine coverage that follows property regardless of location
